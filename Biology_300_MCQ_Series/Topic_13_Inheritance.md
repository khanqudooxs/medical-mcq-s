# Topic 13: Inheritance (300 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Mendelian Genetics, Laws of Inheritance & Terminology

1. **The study of heredity and variations is called:**
   A) Evolution
   B) Genetics
   C) Ecology
   D) Embryology
   *Answer: B (Explanation: Heredity is the transmission of traits from parents to offspring).*

2. **Who is known as the "Father of Genetics"?**
   A) Charles Darwin
   B) Gregor Mendel
   C) Robert Hooke
   D) Watson and Crick
   *Answer: B (Note: He performed experiments on pea plants in 1860s).*

3. **On which specific plant did Mendel conduct his pioneering genetic experiments?**
   A) Rose
   B) Garden Pea (Pisum sativum)
   C) Sunflower
   D) Wheat
   *Answer: B*

4. **How many pairs of contrasting traits (mutazaad khususooyat) did Mendel study in the pea plant?**
   A) 3
   B) 7 (e.g., Height, Seed color, Seed shape, etc.)
   C) 10
   D) 46
   *Answer: B*

5. **An alternative form of a specific gene or trait is known as an:**
   A) Locus
   B) Allele
   C) Chromosome
   D) Phenotype
   *Answer: B (e.g., T and t are alleles for height).*

6. **The specific physical location of a gene on a chromosome is called the:**
   A) Center
   B) Locus (plural: Loci)
   C) Tip
   D) Cell
   *Answer: B*

7. **If an individual possesses two identical alleles for a trait (e.g., TT or tt), they are described as:**
   A) Heterozygous
   B) Homozygous
   C) Hemizygous
   D) Hybrid
   *Answer: B*

8. **If an individual possesses two different alleles for a trait (e.g., Tt), they are described as:**
   A) Homozygous
   B) Heterozygous (Hybrid)
   C) Pure-bred
   D) Recessive
   *Answer: B*

9. **The allele that expresses its phenotype even in the presence of a different allele (in the heterozygous state) is:**
   A) Recessive
   B) Dominant
   C) Weak
   D) Silent
   *Answer: B (e.g., in Tt, 'T' is dominant and the plant is Tall).*

10. **The allele whose effect is masked in the presence of a dominant allele is described as:**
    A) Dominant
    B) Recessive
    C) Co-dominant
    D) Mutant
    *Answer: B (It only expresses its phenotype in a homozygous state like 'tt').*

11. **The observable physical appearance or functional traits of an organism are its:**
    A) Genotype
    B) Phenotype
    C) Karyotype
    D) Isotype
    *Answer: B (e.g., 'Tall' or 'Dwarf').*

12. **The internal genetic makeup or combination of alleles in an organism is its:**
    A) Phenotype
    B) Genotype
    C) Archetype
    D) Serotype
    *Answer: B (e.g., 'TT', 'Tt', or 'tt').*
