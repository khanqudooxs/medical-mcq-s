1. **The permanent and irreversible increase in the physical size of an organism is defined as:**
   A) Nutrition
   B) Growth
   C) Development
   D) Respiration
   *Answer: B (Note: Development encompassess both growth and cellular differentiation).*

2. **Plants typically exhibit growth that continues throughout their entire lifespan; this is known as:**
   A) Determinant growth
   B) Indeterminate growth
   C) Limited growth

   *Answer: B*

   A) Nodes

   A) Along the lateral sides of the stem

   C) At the base of mature leaves
   D) Exclusively within flower structures

7. **What occurs during the physiological process of 'Apical Dominance'?**
   A) Lateral branches grow more rapidly than the main shoot

   C) Roots grow exceptionally long and deep
   D) The plant undergoes sudden senescence and dies
   *Answer: B (The main shoot continues to grow upward preferentially).*

8. **How can 'Apical Dominance' be artificially overcome to promote bushier growth?**
