# Topic 15: Ecology (300 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Ecosystem, Energy Flow & Populations

1. **The study of the relationship between living organisms and their environment is:**
   A) Biology
   B) Ecology
   C) Evolution
   D) Genetics
   *Answer: B (Explanation: Ernst Haeckel coined this term).*

2. **A biological community and its physical environment together form an:**
   A) Population
   B) Ecosystem
   C) Biome
   D) Biosphere
   *Answer: B*

3. **Which of the following is included in the 'Abiotic' (non-living) components of an ecosystem?**
   A) Plants and Animals
   B) Water, Soil, Light, and Air
   C) Bacteria and Fungi
   D) Humans
   *Answer: B*

4. **'Biotic' components of an ecosystem are:**
   A) Rocks
   B) Living organisms (Producers, Consumers, Decomposers)
   C) Temperature
   D) Oxygen
   *Answer: B*

5. **Organisms that prepare their own food are called:**
   A) Consumers
   B) Producers (Autotrophs - like Green Plants)
   C) Decomposers
   D) Parasites
   *Answer: B*

6. **Herbivores are animals that only eat:**
   A) Meat
   B) Plants/Grass
   C) Both plants and meat
   D) Dead matter
   *Answer: B (Primary Consumers).*

7. **Carnivores are animals that eat:**
   A) Grass
   B) Meat/Other animals
   C) Flowers
   D) Stones
   *Answer: B (Secondary/Tertiary Consumers).*

8. **What do Omnivores (e.g., Humans, Bears) eat?**
   A) Only meat
   B) Both plants and animals
   C) Only air
   D) Only water
   *Answer: B*

9. **What is the primary role of Decomposers (Saprotrophs)?**
   A) Making food
   B) Breaking down dead organic matter and recycling nutrients
   C) Hunting
   D) Flying
   *Answer: B (e.g., Fungi and Bacteria).*

10. **What does a Food Chain show?**
    A) Type of plants
    B) Flow of energy from one organism to another in a sequence
    C) Sound of animals
    D) Color of flowers
    *Answer: B*

11. **What is a Food Web?**
    A) Single sequence
    B) Network of interconnected food chains in an ecosystem
    C) A spider web
    D) Collection of books
    *Answer: B (More stable than a single chain).*

12. **A 'Trophic Level' refers to an organism's ______ .**
    A) Height of animal
    B) Feeding level/position in a food chain
    C) Speed of running
    D) Weight of organism
    *Answer: B*

13. **The first (1st) Trophic level is always a ______ .**
    A) Herbivore
    B) Producer (Plants)
    C) Carnivore
    D) Decomposer
    *Answer: B*

14. **What is the ultimate source of energy in an ecosystem?**
    A) Moon
    B) Sun
    C) Water
    D) Heat
    *Answer: B*

15. **The 10% Law of energy transfer states that only 10% of energy is ______ .**
    A) Wasted
    B) Transferred to the next trophic level
    C) Converted to heat
    D) Destroyed
    *Answer: B*

16. **Which of the following are types of Ecological Pyramids?**
    A) Pyramid of Numbers
    B) Pyramid of Biomass
    C) Pyramid of Energy
    D) All of these
    *Answer: D*

17. **A Pyramid of Energy is always ______ .**
    A) Inverted
    B) Upright
    C) Circular
    D) Random
    *Answer: B (As energy is lost as heat at each level).*

18. **The term 'Biomass' refers to the ______ .**
    A) Weight of rocks
    B) Total weight/mass of living matter in a given area
    C) Speed of wind
    D) Number of stars
    *Answer: B*

19. **Symbinsis refers to two different species ______ .**
    A) Fighting
    B) Living together in a close relationship
    C) Avoiding each other
    D) Facing extinction
    *Answer: B*

20. **In Mutualism, both species ______ .**
    A) Are harmed
    B) Benefit (e.g., Lichens, Nitrogen-fixing bacteria)
    C) Are unaffected
    D) Face only death
    *Answer: B*

21. **In Commensalism, one species benefits while the other is ______ .**
    A) Benefited
    B) Harmed
    C) Neither harmed nor benefited
    D) Killed
    *Answer: C*

22. **In Parasitism, one species benefits while the other (Host) is ______ .**
    A) Benefited
    B) Harmed (e.g., Mosquito, Tapeworm)
    C) Happy
    D) Energized
    *Answer: B*

23. **What is Predation?**
    A) Living together
    B) One animal (Predator) killing and eating another (Prey)
    C) Eating grass
    D) Sleeping
    *Answer: B (e.g., Lion and Deer).*

24. **Interspecific Competition occurs between ______ .**
    A) Same species
    B) Different species (competing for the same resource like food/space)
    C) Plants only
    D) No competition
    *Answer: B*

25. **The term 'Niche' refers to an organism's ______ in an ecosystem.**
    A) Physical address
    B) Role/Function/Profession (what it eats, where it lives)
    C) Size
    D) Color
    *Answer: B (E.P. Odum defined it).*

26. **'Habitat' refers to an organism's ______ .**
    A) Profession
    B) Physical address (Home/Living place)
    C) Weight
    D) Age
    *Answer: B*

27. **Ecological Succession refers to a community's ______ change.**

    B) Gradual/Sequential change over time

    D) Final
    *Answer: B*

28. **Where does Primary Succession begin?**

    B) Forest after a fire
    C) Garden
    D) River
    *Answer: A*

29. **Where does Secondary Succession take place?**
    A) In areas where soil is already present (e.g., after forest fire or abandoned farm)
    B) Dry desert
    C) Bare rock
    D) Space
    *Answer: A (Faster than primary succession).*

30. **Pioneer Species are those that ______ succession.**
    A) Terminate
    B) Start (e.g., Lichens on rocks)
    C) Slow down
    D) Pause
    *Answer: B*

31. **The Climax Community is the ______ stage of succession.**
    A) First
    B) Stable/Final and complex stage
    C) Middle
    D) Weakest
    *Answer: B*

32. **Biogeochemical cycles involve the cycling of ______ .**
    A) Vehicles
    B) Chemical nutrients (Carbon, Nitrogen, Water, etc.)
    C) Rocks only
    D) Money
    *Answer: B*
