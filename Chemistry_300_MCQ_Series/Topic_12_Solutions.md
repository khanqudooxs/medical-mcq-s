# Topic 12: Solutions (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Definitions and Types of Solutions

1. **What is a 'Solution'?**
   A) A heterogeneous mixture of two or more substances
   B) A homogeneous mixture of two or more substances
   C) A chemical compound
   D) A pure liquid
   *Answer: B*

2. **The component of a solution present in a smaller amount is called the:**
   A) Solvent
   B) Solute
   C) Residue
   D) Filtrate
   *Answer: B*

3. **The component of a solution present in a larger amount is called the:**
   A) Solute
   B) Solvent (the dissolving medium)
   C) Catalyst
   D) Precipitate
   *Answer: B*

4. **A solution containing water as the solvent is called a/an:**
   A) Non-aqueous solution
   B) Aqueous solution
   C) Organic solution
   D) Solid solution
   *Answer: B*

5. **A solution containing a solvent other than water (like Ethanol or Ether) is a/an:**
   A) Aqueous solution
   B) Non-aqueous solution
   C) Saturated solution
   D) Basic solution
   *Answer: B*

6. **Air is an example of a ______ - ______ solution.**
   A) Gas-Liquid
   B) Gas-Gas
   C) Solid-Gas
   D) Liquid-Liquid
   *Answer: B*

7. **The oxygen dissolved in lake water is an example of a ______ - ______ solution.**
   A) Gas-Gas
   B) Gas-Liquid
   C) Liquid-Liquid
   D) Solid-Liquid
   *Answer: B*

8. **An alloy like Brass (Zinc in Copper) is an example of a ______ - ______ solution.**
   A) Solid-Solid
   B) Liquid-Solid
   C) Gas-Solid
   D) Solid-Liquid
   *Answer: A*

9. **Sugar dissolved in water is a ______ - ______ solution.**
   A) Solid-Solid
   B) Solid-Liquid
   C) Liquid-Liquid
   *Answer: B*

10. **Soda water (carbonated beverage) is a solution of ______ in ______ .**
    A) $O_2$ in water
    B) $CO_2$ gas in water
    C) Sugar in water
    D) Alcohol in water
    *Answer: B*

11. **An 'Aqueous' solution of alcohol is a ______ - ______ solution.**
    A) Gas-Liquid
    B) Liquid-Liquid
    C) Solid-Liquid
    *Answer: B*

12. **A solution that contains the maximum amount of solute that can be dissolved at a given temperature is:**
    A) Unsaturated
    B) Saturated
    C) Supersaturated
    D) Dilute
    *Answer: B*

13. **A solution that contains less solute than its maximum capacity at that temperature is:**
    A) Saturated
    B) Unsaturated
    C) Supersaturated
    D) Mixed
    *Answer: B*

14. **A solution that contains more dissolved solute than is normally possible at a given temperature is:**
    A) Saturated
    B) Unsaturated
    C) Supersaturated (unstable)
    D) Concentrated
    *Answer: C*

15. **The process of increasing the amount of solvent in a solution to decrease its concentration is:**
    A) Saturation
    B) Dilution
    C) Evaporation
    D) Filtration
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Solubility and Factors

16. **'Solubility' is the ______ amount of solute that can dissolve in a given amount of solvent at a specific temperature.**
    A) Minimum
    B) Maximum
    C) Average
    *Answer: B*

17. **Which rule summarizes the general solubility of substances?**
    A) Opposites attract
    B) Like dissolves like
    C) First come, first served
    D) Heavy dissolves light
    *Answer: B*

18. **A polar solute (like Salt) will dissolve best in a ______ solvent.**
    A) Polar (like water)
    B) Non-polar (like grease)
    C) Gaseous
    D) Very cold
    *Answer: A*

19. **A non-polar solute (like Wax or Grease) will dissolve best in a ______ solvent.**
    A) Polar
    B) Non-polar (like Benzene or $CCl_4$)
    C) Acidic
    D) Basic
    *Answer: B*

20. **How does temperature usually affect the solubility of most SOLIDS in liquids?**
    A) Solubility decreases as temperature increases
    B) Solubility increases as temperature increases
    C) Temperature has no effect
    D) Solubility becomes zero
    *Answer: B*

21. **How does temperature affect the solubility of most GASES in liquids?**
    A) Solubility increases as temperature increases
    B) Solubility decreases as temperature increases (e.g., warm soda goes flat)
    C) Temperature has no effect
    *Answer: B*

22. **'Henry's Law' states that the solubility of a gas in a liquid is directly proportional to the ______ of that gas above the liquid.**
    A) Volume
    B) Partial Pressure
    C) Color
    D) Mass
    *Answer: B*

23. **Pressure has ______ effect on the solubility of solids in liquids.**
    A) A massive
    B) Little to no
    C) A negative
    *Answer: B*

24. **A substance that dissolves in water to form a solution that conducts electricity is an:**
    A) Non-electrolyte
    B) Electrolyte
    C) Organic compound
    D) Insulator
    *Answer: B*

25. **The interaction between solute particles and solvent molecules is called:**
    A) Solvation (or Hydration if solvent is water)
    B) Aggregation
    C) Filtration
    D) Distillation
    *Answer: A*

## Part 3: Units of Concentration

26. **'Molarity' (M) is the number of ______ of solute per ______ of solution.**
    A) grams; liter
    B) moles; liter
    C) moles; kilogram
    D) grams; kilogram
    *Answer: B*

27. **The formula for Molarity is:**
    A) $M = mass / volume$
    B) $M = moles / volume (in Liters)$
    C) $M = volume / moles$
    D) $M = moles + volume$
    *Answer: B*

28. **'Molality' (m) is the number of moles of solute per ______ of solvent.**
    A) Liter
    B) Kilogram (kg)
    C) Gram
    D) Milliliter
    *Answer: B*

29. **Which concentration unit does NOT change with temperature?**
    A) Molarity
    B) Molality (because mass doesn't change with T, but volume does)
    C) Normality
    *Answer: B*

30. **'Mole Fraction' ($X$) is the ratio of the moles of one component to the:**
    A) Mass of solvent
    B) Total moles of all components in the mixture
    C) Volume of solution
    D) Density
    *Answer: B*

31. **The sum of mole fractions of all components in a solution is always:**
    A) 0
    B) 1
    C) 100
    D) Variable
    *Answer: B*

32. **'Percentage by Mass' (% w/w) is calculated as:**
    A) $(Mass of solute / Mass of solution) \times 100$
    B) $(Mass of solute / Volume of solution) \times 100$
    C) $(Moles / Mass) \times 100$
    *Answer: A*

33. **'Percentage by Volume' (% v/v) is calculated as:**
    A) $(Volume of solute / Volume of solution) \times 100$
    B) $(Mass / Volume) \times 100$
    *Answer: A*

34. **'Parts per Million' (ppm) is used for ______ solutions.**
    A) Very concentrated
    B) Very dilute (e.g., pollutants in water)
    C) Solid
    D) Pure
    *Answer: B*

35. **1 ppm is equivalent to ______ of solute per liter of aqueous solution.**
    A) 1 g
    B) 1 mg
    C) 1 kg
    D) 1 mole
    *Answer: B*

36. **'Normality' (N) is based on the number of ______ of solute per liter of solution.**
    A) Moles
    B) Gramm-equivalents
    C) Atoms
    *Answer: B*

37. **The dilution equation used to find the new concentration after adding solvent is:**
    A) $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$
    B) $M_1/V_1 = M_2/V_2$
    C) $M_1 + V_1 = M_2 + V_2$
    *Answer: A*

38. **If you dilute 10 mL of 12 M $HCl$ to a total volume of 100 mL, what is the new molarity?**
    A) 12 M
    B) 1.2 M
    C) 120 M
    D) 0.12 M
    *Answer: B ($12 \times 10 / 100 = 1.2$)*

## Part 4: Colligative Properties

39. **Colligative properties are properties of a solution that depend ONLY on the ______ of solute particles.**
    A) Nature/Type
    B) Number/Concentration
    C) Mass
    D) Color
    *Answer: B*

40. **Which of the following is a colligative property?**
    A) Vapor Pressure Lowering
    B) Boiling Point Elevation
    C) Freezing Point Depression
    D) All of the above (and Osmotic Pressure)
    *Answer: D*

41. **Adding a non-volatile solute to a solvent causes its vapor pressure to:**
    A) Increase
    B) Decrease
    C) Stay same
    *Answer: B*

42. **Adding a solute causes the boiling point of the solution to be ______ than that of the pure solvent.**
    A) Lower
    B) Higher
    C) Equal
    *Answer: B*

43. **Adding a solute causes the freezing point of the solution to be ______ than that of the pure solvent.**
    A) Higher
    B) Lower (e.g., salting roads to melt ice)
    C) Same
    *Answer: B*

44. **The pressure required to stop the flow of solvent through a semi-permeable membrane into a solution is:**
    A) Atmospheric pressure
    B) Osmotic Pressure
    C) Vapor pressure

45. **Which membrane allows only solvent molecules to pass but not solute particles?**

46. **'Isotonic' solutions have the ______ osmotic pressure.**

48. **A 'Hypertonic' solution has a ______ concentration of solute.**

    B) Higher
