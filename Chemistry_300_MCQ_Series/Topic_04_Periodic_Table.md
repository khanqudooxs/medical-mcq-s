# Topic 04: Periodic Table (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: History and the Periodic Law

1. **Who is widely considered the 'Father of the Periodic Table'?**
   A) John Dalton
   B) Dmitri Mendeleev
   C) J.J. Thomson
   D) Robert Boyle
   *Answer: B*

2. **Dmitri Mendeleev organized his first periodic table based on increasing:**
   A) Atomic Number
   B) Atomic Mass
   C) Density
   D) Color
   *Answer: B*

3. **What was a key feature of Mendeleev's table that showed his genius?**
   A) He used different colors for each element
   B) He left gaps for undiscovered elements and predicted their properties
   C) He included only gases
   D) He made the table circular
   *Answer: B*

4. **Who discovered that atomic number, not atomic mass, is the fundamental property of an element?**
   A) Niels Bohr
   B) Henry Moseley
   C) Ernest Rutherford
   D) James Chadwick
   *Answer: B*

5. **The Modern Periodic Law states that the properties of elements are periodic functions of their:**
   A) Atomic Weights
   B) Atomic Numbers
   C) Neutron counts
   D) Valence electrons only
   *Answer: B*

6. **In the modern periodic table, elements are arranged in order of increasing:**
   A) Mass
   B) Atomic Number (number of protons)
   C) Year of discovery
   D) Alphabetical order
   *Answer: B*

7. **A horizontal row in the periodic table is called a:**
   A) Group
   ) Period
   C) Column
   D) Family
   *Answer: B*

8. **A vertical column in the periodic table is called a:**
   A) Period
   B) Group (or Family)
   C) Row
   D) Segment
   *Answer: B*

9. **How many principal periods are there in the modern periodic table?**
   A) 8
   ) 7
   C) 18
   D) 10
   *Answer: B*

10. **How many main vertical groups are there in the modern periodic table?**
    A) 7
    B) 18
    C) 8
    D) 32
    *Answer: B*

11. **Elements in the same group have the same number of ______ .**
    A) Total electrons
    B) Valence electrons (in the outer shell)
    C) Neutrons
    D) Inner shells
    *Answer: B*

12. **Why do elements in the same group have similar chemical properties?**
    A) Because they have the same mass
    B) Because they have the same outer shell electron configuration
    C) Because they were discovered by the same person
    D) Because they are all gases
    *Answer: B*

13. **Which group of elements is known as the 'Alkali Metals'?**
    A) Group 1
    B) Group 2
    C) Group 17
    D) Group 18
    *Answer: A*

14. **Which group of elements is known as the 'Alkaline Earth Metals'?**
    A) Group 1
    B) Group 2
    C) Group 13
    D) Group 18

15. **Which group is known as the 'Halogens'?**
    A) Group 1
    B) Group 16
    C) Group 17
    D) Group 18

## Part 3: Periodic Trends

31. **What happens to the 'Atomic Size' (radius) as you move across a period from left to right?**
    A) It increases
    B) It decreases (due to increased nuclear charge pulling electrons closer)
    C) It stays the same
    D) It fluctuates
    *Answer: B*

32. **What happens to the 'Atomic Size' as you move down a group?**
    A) It decreases
    B) It increases (due to the addition of new energy shells)
    C) It stays the same
    D) It becomes zero
    *Answer: B*

33. **'Ionization Energy' (I.E.) is the energy required to:**
    A) Add an electron to an atom
    B) Remove the most loosely bound electron from an isolated gaseous atom
    C) Break the nucleus of an atom
    D) Change the color of an element
    *Answer: B*

34. **In general, Ionization Energy ______ as you move across a period from left to right.**
    A) Increases
    B) Decreases
    C) Stays the same
    D) Becomes negative
    *Answer: A*

35. **In general, Ionization Energy ______ as you move down a group.**
    A) Increases
    B) Decreases (because the electron is further from the nucleus and easier to remove)
    C) Stays the same
    D) Becomes infinite
    *Answer: B*

36. **'Electronegativity' (E.N.) is a measure of an atom's ability to:**
    A) Release light
    B) Attract shared electrons in a chemical bond
    C) Repel other atoms
    D) Conduct heat
    *Answer: B*

37. **Which element has the highest electronegativity on the Pauling scale?**
    A) Cesium

    C) Oxygen
    D) Francium
    *Answer: B*

    D) Decreases; Decreases
    *Answer: B*

39. **'Electron Affinity' (E.A.) is the energy released when:**
