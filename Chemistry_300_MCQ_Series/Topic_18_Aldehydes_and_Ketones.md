# Topic 18: Aldehydes & Ketones (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Carbonyl Group and Nomenclature

1. **Aldehydes and Ketones are collectively called ______ compounds.**
   A) Alcohol
   B) Carbonyl (due to the $>C=O$ group)
   C) Carboxyl
   D) Amine
   *Answer: B*

2. **In Aldehydes, the carbonyl group is attached to at least one ______ atom.**
   A) Carbon only
   B) Hydrogen atom (always at the end of the chain, $R-CHO$)
   C) Oxygen only
   D) Metal
   *Answer: B*

3. **In Ketones, the carbonyl group is attached to two ______ groups.**
   A) Hydrogen
   B) Alkyl or Aryl groups ($R-CO-R$)
   C) Halogen
   D) Hydroxyl
   *Answer: B*

4. **The hybridization of the carbonyl Carbon atom is:**
   A) $sp$
   B) $sp^2$ (Trigonal Planar)
   C) $sp^3$
   *Answer: B*

5. **The geometry of atoms directly bonded to the carbonyl group is:**
   A) Tetrahedral
   B) Trigonal Planar
   C) Linear
   D) Pyramid
   *Answer: B*

6. **The Carbon-Oxygen double bond consists of one ______ and one ______ bond.**
   A) Sigma; Delta
   B) Sigma ($\sigma$); Pi ($\pi$)
   *Answer: B*

7. **Is the carbonyl group polar?**
   A) Yes (Oxygen is more electronegative than Carbon)
   B) No
   *Answer: A*

8. **In the carbonyl group, the Carbon atom carries a partial ______ charge and the Oxygen carries a partial ______ charge.**
   A) Negative; Positive
   B) Positive ($\delta+$); Negative ($\delta-$)
   *Answer: B*

9. **The first member of the Aldehyde series is:**
   A) Acetaldehyde
   B) Formaldehyde (Methanal, $HCHO$)
   C) Propanal
   *Answer: B*

10. **The first member of the Ketone series is:**
    A) Acetone (Propanone, $CH_3COCH_3$)
    B) Butanone
    C) Propanal
    *Answer: A*

11. **The suffix used for naming Aldehydes in IUPAC is:**
    A) -ol
    B) -al (e.g., Ethanal)
    C) -one
    D) -oic
    *Answer: B*

12. **The suffix used for naming Ketones in IUPAC is:**
    A) -al
    B) -one (e.g., Propanone)
    C) -ol
    *Answer: B*

13. **Aldehydes and Ketones with the same number of carbon atoms are ______ isomers.**
    A) Chain
    B) Functional Isomers (e.g., Propanal and Propanone)
    C) Position
    *Answer: B*

14. **Formalin is a 40% aqueous solution of:**
    A) Ethanol
    B) Formaldehyde ($HCHO$)
    C) Acetic acid
    D) Acetone
    *Answer: B*

15. **Formalin is widely used as a:**
    A) Food flavoring
    B) Preservative for biological specimens and disinfectant
    C) Fuel
    D) Sweetener
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Preparation and Nucleophilic Addition

16. **Oxidation of a Primary alcohol with $K_2Cr_2O_7/H^+$ produces a/an:**
    A) Ketone
    B) Aldehyde
    C) Alkane
    *Answer: B*

17. **Oxidation of a Secondary alcohol produces a/an:**
    A) Aldehyde
    B) Ketone
    C) Acid
    *Answer: B*

18. **Dehydrogenation of alcohols over hot Copper catalyst at 300°C produces:**
    A) Aldehydes (from $1^\circ$) or Ketones (from $2^\circ$)
    B) Alkanes
    C) Ethers
    *Answer: A*

19. **Ozonolysis of Alkenes followed by reduction with Zinc/Water produces:**
    A) Alcohols
    B) Carbonyl compounds (Aldehydes/Ketones)
    C) Acids
    *Answer: B*

20. **Hydration of Alkynes (like Ethyne) in presence of $HgSO_4$ and $H_2SO_4$ produces:**
    A) Methanol
    B) Acetaldehyde (Ethanal)
    C) Formaldehyde
    *Answer: B*

21. **Because the carbonyl carbon is electron-deficient ($\delta+$), it is attacked by ______ .**
    A) Electrophiles
    B) Nucleophiles
    C) Atoms only
    *Answer: B*

22. **The most common reaction type for Aldehydes and Ketones is:**
    A) Electrophilic addition
    B) Nucleophilic Addition Reaction
    C) Substitution
    *Answer: B*

23. **Addition of $HCN$ to a carbonyl compound forms a:**
    A) Halohydrin
    B) Cyanohydrin
    C) Alcohol
    D) Amide
    *Answer: B*

24. **Addition of Sodium Bisulfite ($NaHSO_3$) produces a white crystalline solid, used for ______ of carbonyl compounds.**
    A) Destruction
    B) Purification and separation
    C) Coloring
    D) Burning
    *Answer: B*

25. **Reaction of a carbonyl compound with a Grignard Reagent ($RMgX$) followed by hydrolysis produces an:**
    A) Alkane
    B) Alcohol
    C) Ether
    D) Acid
    *Answer: B*

26. **Formaldehyde + Grignard Reagent $\to$ ______ Alcohol.**
    A) Primary
    B) Secondary
    C) Tertiary
    *Answer: A*

27. **Other Aldehydes + Grignard Reagent $\to$ ______ Alcohol.**
    A) Primary
    B) Secondary
    C) Tertiary
    *Answer: B*

28. **Ketones + Grignard Reagent $\to$ ______ Alcohol.**
    A) Primary
    B) Secondary
    C) Tertiary
    *Answer: C*

29. **Reaction with Ammonia derivatives (like Hydroxylamine, Hydrazine) is used for:**
    A) Making fuels
    ) Identification and characterization of carbonyl compounds
    C) Making salt
    *Answer: B*

30. **Carbonyl compound + Hydroxylamine ($NH_2OH$) $\to$ ______ .**
    A) Hydrazone
    B) Oxime
    C) Semicarbazone
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Oxidation, Reduction, and Named Reactions

31. **Which is more easily oxidized?**
    A) Ketones
    B) Aldehydes
    C) Both are equal
    *Answer: B (Aldehydes have a H atom on the carbonyl carbon that is easy to remove).*

32. **Oxidation of Aldehydes produces a/an:**
    A) Alcohol
    B) Carboxylic Acid (with same no. of carbons)
    C) Ketone
    *Answer: B*

33. **Ketones are resistant to oxidation and require vigorous conditions to form acids with ______ Carbon atoms.**
    A) Same
    B) Fewer
    C) More
    *Answer: B*

34. **Reduction of Aldehydes with $H_2/Ni$ or $NaBH_4$ produces a/an:**
    A) Primary Alcohol
    B) Secondary Alcohol
    C) Alkane
    *Answer: A*

35. **Reduction of Ketones produces a/an:**
    A) Primary Alcohol
    B) Secondary Alcohol
    C) Tertiary Alcohol
    *Answer: B*

36. **'Clemmensen Reduction' uses $Zn-Hg/HCl$ to convert a carbonyl group directly into a:**
    A) $-OH$ group
    B) $-CH_2-$ group (Alkane)
    C) $-CHO$ group
    *Answer: B*

37. **'Wolff-Kishner Reduction' uses Hydrazine ($NH_2NH_2$) and $KOH$ to produce a/an:**
    A) Alcohol
    B) Alkane
    C) Acid
    *Answer: B*

38. **'Aldol Condensation' occurs in aldehydes or ketones that have at least one ______ atom.**
    A) Alpha-Hydrogen ($\alpha-H$)
    B) Oxygen
    C) Chlorine
    *Answer: A (Hydrogen on the carbon next to the carbonyl).*

39. **In Aldol condensation, two molecules combine in the presence of dilute base to form a:**
    A) Ester
    B) $\beta$-hydroxy aldehyde or ketone (Aldol)
    C) Salt
    *Answer: B*

40. **'Cannizzaro Reaction' occurs in aldehydes that have ______ $\alpha-H$ atoms (like Formaldehyde or Benzaldehyde).**
    A) Many
    B) NO
    C) Two
    *Answer: B*

41. **In Cannizzaro reaction, one molecule is ______ to an alcohol and another is ______ to an acid salt (self-redox).**
    A) Oxidized; Reduced
    B) Reduced; Oxidized
    *Answer: B*

42. **Sodium nitroprusside solution gives a ______ color with ketones.**
    A) Blue
    ) Wine-red/Red
    C) Green
    D) Yellow
    *Answer: B*

43. **Carbonyl compounds react with 2,4-DNP (Brady’s reagent) to form ______ precipitates.**
    A) White
    B) Yellow or Orange/Red (crystalline)
    C) Black
    D) Clear
    *Answer: B (Test for $>C=O$ group).*

44. **2,4-DNP stands for:**
    A) 2,4-Dinitrophenol
    B) 2,4-Dinitrophenylhydrazine
    C) 2,4-Dimethylnitropropane
    *Answer: B*

45. **Which reaction is used for the commercial manufacture of Acetic acid from Acetylene?**
    A) Hydrogenation
    B) Catalytic hydration followed by oxidation
    C) Fermentation
    *Answer: B*

## Part 4: Identification and Distinguishing Tests

46. **'Tollen's Reagent' is an ammoniacal solution of:**
    A) Copper sulfate
    B) Silver Nitrate ($AgNO_3$)
    C) Sodium hydroxide
    *Answer: B*

47. **Tollen's Test is also known as the:**
    A) Blue test
    B) Silver Mirror Test
    C) Red test
    *Answer: B*

48. **In Tollen's Test, Aldehydes produce a ______ on the inner wall of the test tube.**
    A) Blue color
    B) Shiny silver mirror
    C) Black smoke
    *Answer: B*

49. **Do Ketones give a positive Tollen's test?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (This distinguishes them from aldehydes).
    *Answer: B*

50. **'Fehling's Solution' and 'Benedict's Solution' contain complexed ______ ions.**
    A) $Ag^+$
    B) $Cu^{2+}$ (Copper)
    *Answer: B*

51. **In Fehling's Test, Aldehydes (aliphatic) reduce the blue solution to a ______ precipitate of $Cu_2O$.**
    A) White
    B) Brick-red
    C) Green
    D) Yellow
    *Answer: B*

52. **Benzaldehyde (an aromatic aldehyde) ______ give a brick-red precipitate with Fehling's solution.**
    A) Does
    B) Does NOT (usually)
    *Answer: B (It is less reactive than aliphatic ones).*

53. **Do Ketones reduce Fehling's or Benedict's solution?**
    A) Yes
    B) No
    *Answer: B*

54. **'Schiff’s Reagent' is a magenta dye decolorized by $SO_2$. Aldehydes restore its ______ color.**
    A) Blue
    B) Pink/Magenta
    C) Yellow
    D) Green
    *Answer: B*

55. **'Iodoform Test' is given by acetaldehyde and methyl ketones (like Acetone) that contain which group?**
    A) $-CHO$
    B) $CH_3-CO-$ (Acetyl group)
    C) $-OH$

56. **A positive Iodoform test is indicated by the formation of a ______ precipitate.**
