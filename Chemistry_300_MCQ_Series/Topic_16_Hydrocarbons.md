# Topic 16: Hydrocarbons (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Alkanes (Saturated Hydrocarbons)

1. **Alkanes are also known as:**
   A) Olefins
   B) Paraffins (meaning 'little affinity')
   C) Acetylenes
   D) Arenes
   *Answer: B*

2. **The general formula for open-chain alkanes is:**
   A) $C_nH_{2n}$
   B) $C_nH_{2n+2}$
   C) $C_nH_{2n-2}$
   D) $C_nH_n$
   *Answer: B*

3. **Hybridization of Carbon in all Alkanes is:**
   A) $sp$
   B) $sp^2$
   C) $sp^3$
   D) $d^2sp^3$
   *Answer: C*

4. **The geometry around each carbon atom in Methane ($CH_4$) is:**
   A) Linear
   B) Trigonal Planar
   C) Tetrahedral
   D) Octahedral
   *Answer: C*

5. **The C-C-C or C-C-H bond angle in Alkanes is approximately:**
   A) 90°
   B) 120°
   C) 109.5°
   D) 180°
   *Answer: C*

6. **Methane is the primary constituent of:**
   A) Natural Gas (Sui gas)
   B) Coal gas
   C) Biogas
   D) All of the above
   *Answer: D*

7. **Alkanes are ______ in water and ______ in non-polar solvents.**
   A) Soluble; Insoluble
   B) Insoluble; Soluble
   C) Soluble; Soluble
   *Answer: B*

8. **'Wurtz Reaction' involves the reaction of alkyl halides with which metal in dry ether?**
   A) Magnesium
   B) Sodium (Na)
   C) Iron
   D) Zinc
   *Answer: B (used to prepare higher alkanes).*

9. **Which reaction is used to prepare methane in the lab?**
   A) Wurtz reaction
   B) Decarboxylation of sodium acetate with soda-lime ($NaOH + CaO$)
   C) Hydrolysis of calcium carbide
   *Answer: B*

10. **The main chemical reaction characteristic of Alkanes is:**
    A) Addition reaction
    B) Substitution Reaction (e.g., Chlorination)
    C) Polymerization
    D) Fermentation
    *Answer: B*

11. **Chlorination of Methane in the presence of sunlight follows which mechanism?**
    A) Ionic mechanism
    B) Free Radical Mechanism
    C) Molecular mechanism
    *Answer: B*

12. **The final product of complete chlorination of Methane is:**
    A) Methyl chloride
    B) Chloroform
    C) Carbon Tetrachloride ($CCl_4$)
    D) Methylene chloride
    *Answer: C*

13. **Combustion of Alkanes in excess oxygen produces ______ and ______ .**
    A) $CO$ and $H_2$
    B) $CO_2$ and $H_2O$
    C) $C$ and $H_2$
    D) Smoke only
    *Answer: B*

14. **Partial combustion of Methane produces 'Carbon Black', which is used in:**
    A) Food
    B) Printing inks and rubber tires
    C) Medicines
    D) Building houses
    *Answer: B*

15. **'Knocking' in car engines is reduced by using fuels with a higher ______ .**
    A) Boiling point
    B) Octane Number
    C) Density
    D) Color
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Alkenes (Unsaturated Hydrocarbons)

16. **Alkenes contain at least one Carbon-Carbon ______ bond.**
    A) Single
    B) Double ($C=C$)
    C) Triple
    *Answer: B*

17. **Alkenes are also known as:**
    A) Paraffins
    B) Olefins (oil-forming)
    C) Acetylenes
    *Answer: B*

18. **The general formula for Alkenes with one double bond is:**
    A) $C_nH_{2n+2}$
    B) $C_nH_{2n}$
    C) $C_nH_{2n-2}$
    *Answer: B*

19. **Hybridization of Carbon atoms involved in a double bond is:**
    A) $sp$
    B) $sp^2$
    C) $sp^3$
    *Answer: B*

20. **The geometry of atoms directly bonded to a $C=C$ unit is:**
    A) Linear
    B) Trigonal Planar
    C) Tetrahedral
    *Answer: B*

21. **A double bond consists of one ______ bond and one ______ bond.**
    A) Sigma; Delta
    B) Sigma ($\sigma$); Pi ($\pi$)
    C) Ionic; Covalent
    *Answer: B*

22. **Alkenes are ______ reactive than Alkanes.**
    A) Less
    B) More (due to the presence of the weaker Pi bond)
    C) Equally
    *Answer: B*

23. **The characteristic reaction of Alkenes is:**
    A) Substitution
    B) Addition Reaction
    C) Decomposition
    *Answer: B*

24. **Addition of Hydrogen to Alkenes in the presence of Nickel catalyst is called:**
    ) Halogenation
    B) Hydrogenation (used to make Ghee from oil)
    C) Hydration
    D) Oxidation
    *Answer: B*

25. **The addition of Bromine water ($Br_2/H_2O$) to an alkene causes the ______ color to disappear.**
    A) Reddish-brown
    B) Green
    C) Blue
    D) Yellow
    *Answer: A (This is a test for unsaturation).*

26. **'Markownikoff’s Rule' is used to predict the product when adding a reagent (like $HBr$) to a/an:**
    A) Symmetrical alkene
    B) Asymmetrical alkene (e.g., Propene)
    C) Alkane
    *Answer: B*

27. **According to Markownikoff’s Rule, the negative part of the reagent goes to the carbon with:**
    A) More hydrogens
    B) Fewer hydrogens
    C) No hydrogens
    *Answer: B*

28. **Treatment of alkene with cold alkaline $KMnO_4$ (Baeyer's Reagent) produces:**
    A) Alkane
    B) Glycols (Diols)
    C) Acids
    D) Ketones
    *Answer: B*

29. **Ethylene is used commercially to manufacture which common plastic?**
    A) PVC
    B) Polyethylene (Polythene)
    C) Teflon
    D) Nylon
    *Answer: B*

30. **Which gas is used for the artificial ripening of fruits?**
    A) Methane
    B) Ethylene (Ethene)
    C) Argon
    D) Neon
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Alkynes (Triple Bonds)

31. **Alkynes contain at least one Carbon-Carbon ______ bond.**
    A) Single
    B) Double
    C) Triple ($C\equiv C$)
    *Answer: C*

32. **Alkynes are also known as the ______ series.**
    A) Paraffin
    B) Olefin
    C) Acetylene
    *Answer: C*

33. **The general formula for Alkynes with one triple bond is:**
    A) $C_nH_{2n}$
    B) $C_nH_{2n-2}$
    C) $C_nH_{2n+2}$
    *Answer: B*

34. **Hybridization of Carbon atoms in a triple bond is:**
    A) $sp^3$
    B) $sp^2$
    C) $sp$
    *Answer: C*

35. **The geometry of Alkynes around the triple bond is:**
    A) Tetrahedral
    B) Trigonal Planar
    C) Linear
    D) Bent
    *Answer: C*

36. **A triple bond consists of one ______ bond and two ______ bonds.**
    A) Sigma; Pi
    B) Pi; Sigma
    C) Ionic; Covalent
    *Answer: A*

37. **The first member of the Alkyne series is:**
    A) Methyne (doesn't exist)
    B) Ethyne (Acetylene, $C_2H_2$)
    C) Propyne
    *Answer: B*

38. **Acetylene is prepared by the reaction of water with:**
    A) Aluminum carbide
    B) Calcium Carbide ($CaC_2$)
    C) Sodium chloride
    D) Magnesium
    *Answer: B*

39. **Oxy-acetylene flame is used for:**
    A) Cooking
    B) Welding and cutting of metals (very high temperature)
    C) Lighting bulbs
    D) Driving cars
    *Answer: B*

40. **Are Alkynes acidic in nature?**
    A) No
    B) Yes, terminal alkynes (like $HC\equiv CH$) can lose a proton to strong bases
    *Answer: B*

41. **Ethyne reacts with ammoniacal silver nitrate to give a ______ precipitate.**
    A) Red
    B) White (Silver acetylide)
    C) Blue
    D) Yellow
    *Answer: B*

42. **Ethyne reacts with ammoniacal cuprous chloride to give a ______ precipitate.**
    A) White
    B) Red (Copper acetylide)
    C) Black
    D) Green
    *Answer: B*

43. **Trimerization of Ethyne (passing through a red-hot tube) produces:**
    A) Ethane
    B) Benzene ($C_6H_6$)
    C) Polyethylene
    D) Methane
    *Answer: B*

44. **Alkynes, like Alkenes, undergo ______ reactions.**
    A) Substitution
    B) Addition
    C) Elimination
    *Answer: B*

45. **Vapor phase partial oxidation of Methane is used to produce ______ .**
    A) Ethanol
    B) Methanol
    C) Acetic acid
    *Answer: B*

## Part 4: Aromatic Hydrocarbons (Benzene)

46. **The simplest aromatic hydrocarbon is:**
    A) Methane
    B) Cyclohexane
    C) Benzene ($C_6H_6$)
    D) Naphthalene
    *Answer: C*

47. **Aromatic compounds are characterized by which structure?**
    A) Straight chain
    B) A stable ring with delocalized pi electrons (Hückel's Rule)
    C) Only single bonds
    D) Only Oxygen atoms
    *Answer: B*

48. **In Benzene, all Carbon atoms are ______ hybridized.**
    A) $sp^3$
    B) $sp^2$
    C) $sp$
    *Answer: B*

49. **The shape of the Benzene molecule is:**
    A) Tetrahedral
    B) Planar Hexagonal
    C) Linear
    D) Sphere
    *Answer: B*

50. **Benzene was first isolated from coal tar by ______ in 1825.**
    A) Kekulé
    B) Michael Faraday
    C) John Dalton
    D) Marie Curie
    *Answer: B*

51. **The structure of benzene consisting of two canonical forms with alternating double bonds was suggested by:**
    A) Faraday
    B) August Kekulé
    C) Newton
    D) Bohr
    *Answer: B*

52. **Benzene is ______ stable than expected for 'Cyclohexatriene' due to ______ .**
    A) Less; Mass
    B) More; Resonance Energy
    C) Equally; Color
    *Answer: B*

53. **The characteristic reactions of Benzene are:**
    A) Electrophilic Substitution Reactions
    B) Nucleophilic Addition
    C) Elimination
    D) Free radical only
    *Answer: A*

54. **'Nitration' of benzene involves the substitution of H with which group?**
    A) $-Cl$
    B) $-NO_2$
    C) $-SO_3H$
    D) $-CH_3$
    *Answer: B*

55. **'Halogenation' of benzene (e.g., Chlorination) requires a catalyst like:**
    A) $NaOH$
    B) $FeCl_3$ or $AlCl_3$ (Lewis Acids)
    C) $H_2O$
    D) $HCl$
    *Answer: B*

56. **'Sulphonation' of benzene uses which reagent?**
    A) Conc. $HCl$
    B) Conc. $H_2SO_4$ (or fuming $H_2SO_4$)
    C) $HNO_3$
    D) $NaCl$
    *Answer: B*

57. **The 'Friedel-Crafts' reaction is used to introduce ______ or ______ groups into the benzene ring.**
    A) Alkyl; Acyl
    B) Nitro; Chloro
    C) Hydroxyl; Amino
    *Answer: A*

58. **Reaction of benzene with $CH_3Cl$ in presence of $AlCl_3$ is:**
    A) Nitration
    B) Alkylation
    C) Sulphonation
    D) Combustion
    *Answer: B*

59. **Benzene is highly ______ and should be handled carefully.**
    A) Sweet
    B) Toxic and Carcinogenic (cancer-causing)
    C) Solid
    *Answer: B*

60. **Hydrocarbon derivatives like Toluene have a ______ group attached to the benzene ring.**
    A) $-OH$
    B) Methyl ($-CH_3$)
    C) $-NH_2$
    D) $-COOH$
    *Answer: B*

61. **'Phenol' is a benzene ring attached to a/an:**
    A) $-CH_3$
    B) $-OH$ (Hydroxyl)
    C) $-NO_2$
    D) $-Cl$
    *Answer: B*

62. **The main source of aromatic hydrocarbons today is:**
    A) Sea water
    B) Petroleum and Coal Tar
    C) Air
    D) Plants only
    *Answer: B*

63. **Ozonolysis of Alkenes is used to locate the ______ .**
    A) Total weight
    ) Position of the double bond
    C) Color
    D) Number of Hydrogens

64. **Polymerization of Vinyl Chloride produces:**
    A) Polystyrene
    B) PVC (Poly Vinyl Chloride)

65. **Teflon is a polymer of:**
    A) Ethylene

66. **Baeyer's Test is used to distinguish between:**
    A) Alkanes and Alkenes (Saturated and Unsaturated)
    B) Alcohols and Acids
    C) Benzene and Toluene
    *Answer: A*

67. **Alkanes ______ decolorise bromine water.**
    A) Do

    *Answer: B*

68. **Alkenes and Alkynes ______ decolorise bromine water.**
    A) Do (Addition reaction takes place)
    B) Do NOT

69. **General formula for Cycloalkanes is:**
    A) $C_nH_{2n+2}$
    B) $C_nH_{2n}$ (Same as Alkenes, but no double bond)
    C) $C_nH_{2n-2}$
