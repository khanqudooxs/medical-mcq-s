# Topic 05: Chemical Bonding (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Chemical Bonds and the Octet Rule

1. **Why do atoms form chemical bonds?**
   A) To increase their energy
   B) To achieve stability (usually a full outer shell)
   C) To become radioactive
   D) To increase their mass
   *Answer: B*

2. **The 'Octet Rule' suggests that atoms are most stable when they have how many valence electrons?**
   A) 2
   B) 8
   C) 18
   D) 0
   *Answer: B*

3. **Which group of elements naturally satisfies the octet rule without forming bonds?**
   A) Alkali metals
   B) Halogens
   C) Noble gases
   D) Alkaline earth metals
   *Answer: C*

4. **The valence electrons involved in bonding are located in the:**
   A) Nucleus
   B) Inner shells
   C) Outermost shell
   D) d-subshell of transition metals only
   *Answer: C*

5. **A chemical bond formed by the complete transfer of electrons from one atom to another is a:**
   A) Covalent bond
   B) Ionic bond
   C) Metallic bond
   D) Hydrogen bond
   *Answer: B*

6. **Ionic bonds typically form between:**
   A) Two non-metals
   B) A metal and a non-metal
   C) Two metals
   D) Two noble gases
   *Answer: B*

7. **A chemical bond formed by the sharing of electrons between atoms is a:**
   A) Ionic bond
   B) Covalent bond
   C) Metallic bond
   D) Coordinate bond
   *Answer: B*

8. **Covalent bonds typically form between:**
   A) A metal and a non-metal
   B) Two non-metals
   C) Two metals
   D) A metal and a noble gas
   *Answer: B*

9. **The electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions is the basis of:**
   A) Covalent bonding
   B) Ionic bonding
   C) Metallic bonding
   D) Van der Waals forces
   *Answer: B*

10. **A single covalent bond involve the sharing of how many electrons?**
    A) 1
    B) 2 (one pair)
    C) 4
    D) 6
    *Answer: B*

11. **A double covalent bond involves the sharing of:**
    A) 2 electrons
    B) 4 electrons (two pairs)
    C) 6 electrons
    D) 8 electrons
    *Answer: B*

12. **A triple covalent bond involves the sharing of:**
    A) 3 electrons
    B) 6 electrons (three pairs)
    C) 9 electrons
    D) 12 electrons
    *Answer: B*

13. **Which molecule contains a triple covalent bond?**
    A) $H_2$
    B) $O_2$
    C) $N_2$
    D) $Cl_2$
    *Answer: C*

14. **Which molecule contains a double covalent bond?**
    A) $Cl_2$
    B) $H_2O$
    C) $O_2$
    D) $CH_4$
    *Answer: C*

15. **A covalent bond where electrons are shared equally is called:**
    A) Polar covalent bond
    B) Non-polar covalent bond
    C) Ionic bond
    D) Coordinate bond
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Types of Bonding and Properties

16. **A covalent bond where electrons are shared UNequally due to a difference in electronegativity is:**
    A) Non-polar
    B) Polar covalent bond
    C) Metallic
    D) Hydrogen bond
    *Answer: B*

17. **Which molecule is an example of a polar covalent bond?**
    A) $H_2$
    B) $Cl_2$
    C) $HCl$
    D) $O_2$
    *Answer: C*

18. **A 'Coordinate Covalent Bond' (Dative Bond) is formed when:**
    A) Both atoms contribute one electron
    B) Only one atom provides both electrons for the shared pair
    C) Electrons are transferred
    D) Atoms are radioactive
    *Answer: B*

19. **The bond formed by a 'sea of mobile electrons' surrounding positive metal ions is:**
    A) Ionic bond
    B) Covalent bond
    C) Metallic bond
    D) Hydrogen bond
    *Answer: C*

20. **Which type of bond responsible for the high electrical conductivity and malleability of metals?**
    A) Ionic
    B) Metallic bond
    C) Covalent
    D) Polar
    *Answer: B*

21. **Intermolecular forces that occur between the hydrogen atom of one molecule and a highly electronegative atom (N, O, F) of another molecule are called:**
    A) Ionic bonds
    B) Metallic bonds
    C) Hydrogen bonds
    D) Covalent bonds
    *Answer: C*

22. **Hydrogen bonding is responsible for which unique property of water?**
    A) Its transparency
    B) Its high boiling point compared to molecules of similar mass
    C) Its ability to conduct electricity when pure
    D) Its saltiness
    *Answer: B*

23. **Which statement is true about ionic compounds?**
    A) They have low melting points
    B) They are usually liquids at room temperature
    C) They form crystalline solids and have high melting points
    D) They do not dissolve in water
    *Answer: C*

24. **Ionic compounds conduct electricity when:**
    A) They are in the solid state
    B) Melted (molten) or dissolved in water (aqueous)
    C) They are cold
    D) They are under high pressure
    *Answer: B*

25. **Covalent compounds (like sugar or oil) generally have:**
    A) High melting points
    B) Metallic luster
    C) Relatively low melting and boiling points
    D) Permanent radioactive charge
    *Answer: C*

26. **Short-lived dipole-dipole interactions between non-polar molecules are called:**
    A) Ionic bonds
    B) London Dispersion Forces (Van der Waals)
    C) Hydrogen bonds
    D) Metallic bonds
    *Answer: B*

27. **What is 'Bond Energy'?**
    A) The energy required to move an atom
    B) The energy required to break a chemical bond between two atoms
    C) The energy inside the nucleus
    D) The energy of a speeding electron
    *Answer: B*

28. **In general, a triple bond is ______ and ______ than a single bond.**
    A) Weaker; Longer
    B) Stronger; Shorter
    C) Equal; Longer
    D) Stronger; Longer
    *Answer: B*

29. **Electronegativity difference ($\Delta EN$) of greater than 1.7 usually results in:**
    A) Covalent bond
    B) Ionic bond
    C) Metallic bond
    D) No bond
    *Answer: B*

30. **Electronegativity difference of less than 0.4 usually results in:**
    A) Polar covalent bond
    B) Non-polar covalent bond
    C) Ionic bond
    D) Metallic bond
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Molecular Geometry and Theory

31. **VSEPR theory stands for:**
    A) Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion
    B) Very Small Electron Proton Reaction
    C) Valence Solid Electron Particle Ratio
    D) Visual Science Every Particle Reacts
    *Answer: A*

32. **VSEPR theory is used to predict the:**
    A) Color of a molecule
    B) Shape/Geometry of a molecule
    C) Mass of an atom
    D) Reactivity with water
    *Answer: B*

33. **What is the shape of a $CH_4$ (Methane) molecule?**
    A) Linear
    B) Trigonal Planar
    C) Tetrahedral
    D) Bent
    *Answer: C*

34. **What is the shape of a $H_2O$ molecule?**
    A) Linear
    B) Bent/V-shaped (due to lone pairs on Oxygen)
    C) Tetrahedral
    D) Trigonal Bipyramidal
    *Answer: B*

35. **What is the shape of a $CO_2$ molecule?**
    A) Bent
    B) Linear
    C) Square Planar
    D) Octahedral
    *Answer: B*

36. **A molecule with three bonding pairs and one lone pair (like $NH_3$) has a ______ shape.**
    A) Tetrahedral
    B) Trigonal Pyramidal
    ) Trigonal Planar
    D) Bent
    *Answer: B*

37. **The 'Dipole Moment' is a measure of the ______ of a molecule.**
    A) Mass
    B) Polarity
    C) Velocity
    D) Radioactivity
    *Answer: B*

38. **A molecule can have polar bonds but be non-polar overall if its shape is:**
    A) Asymmetrical
    B) Symmetrical (like $CO_2$ or $CH_4$)
    C) Very large
    D) Liquid
    *Answer: B*

39. **The blending of atomic orbitals of different energies to form new, equivalent orbitals is:**
    A) Fusion
    B) Hybridization
    C) Fission
    D) Ionization
    *Answer: B*

40. **$sp^3$ hybridization leads to which geometry?**
    A) Linear
    B) Trigonal Planar
    C) Tetrahedral

41. **$sp^2$ hybridization leads to which geometry?**
    A) Linear
    B) Trigonal Planar
    C) Tetrahedral

42. **$sp$ hybridization leads to which geometry?**
    A) Linear
    B) Bent
    C) Pyramidal
    D) Square
    *Answer: A*

43. **Sigma ($\sigma$) bonds are formed by the ______ overlap of orbitals.**
    A) Side-to-side
    B) Head-to-head (end-on-end)
    C) Random
    D) No

44. **Pi ($\pi$) bonds are formed by the ______ overlap of parallel p-orbitals.**
    A) Head-to-head
    B) Side-to-side (lateral)
    C) Vertical
    D) Double
    *Answer: B*

45. **Which is generally stronger?**
    A) Sigma ($\sigma$) bond
    B) Pi ($\pi$) bond
    *Answer: A*

46. **How many sigma and pi bonds are in a triple bond?**

    B) 1 sigma, 2 pi
    C) 2 sigma, 1 pi

    *Answer: B*

47. **How many sigma and pi bonds are in a double bond?**
    A) 2 sigma
    B) 2 pi
    C) 1 sigma, 1 pi
    D) 0 sigma
    *Answer: C*

48. **Molecular Orbital (MO) Theory describes the distribution of electrons in:**
    A) Single atoms
    B) The entire molecule (using bonding and anti-bonding orbitals)
    C) The nucleus
