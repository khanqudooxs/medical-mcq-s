# Topic 02: States of Matter (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: General Properties and Kinetic Molecular Theory

1. **How many primary states of matter are commonly recognized in basic chemistry?**
   A) 2
   B) 3 (Solid, Liquid, Gas)
   C) 5
   D) 1
   *Answer: B (Plasma is the 4th, and BEC is often considered the 5th).*

2. **According to the Kinetic Molecular Theory (KMT), particles of matter are:**
   A) Completely stationary
   B) In constant, random motion
   C) Stationary in solids only
   D) Only moving in gases
   *Answer: B*

3. **In which state of matter are the attractive forces between particles the strongest?**
   A) Gas
   B) Liquid
   C) Solid
   D) Plasma
   *Answer: C*

4. **Which state of matter has a definite shape and a definite volume?**
   A) Gas
   B) Liquid
   C) Solid
   D) Plasma
   *Answer: C*

5. **Which state of matter has a definite volume but takes the shape of its container?**
   A) Solid
   B) Liquid
   C) Gas
   D) BEC
   *Answer: B*

6. **Which state of matter has neither a definite shape nor a definite volume?**
   A) Solid
   B) Liquid
   C) Gas
   D) Ice
   *Answer: C*

7. **The process of a solid changing directly into a gas without becoming a liquid is called:**
   A) Evaporation
   B) Sublimation
   C) Condensation
   D) Deposition
   *Answer: B*

8. **The process of a gas changing directly into a solid is called:**
   A) Sublimation
   B) Deposition
   C) Fusion
   D) Vaporization
   *Answer: B*

9. **What happens to the kinetic energy of particles as temperature increases?**
   A) It decreases
   B) It increases
   C) It remains constant
   D) It becomes zero
   *Answer: B*

10. **The temperature at which a solid becomes a liquid is its:**
    A) Boiling point
    B) Melting point
    C) Critical point
    D) Triple point
    *Answer: B*

11. **The temperature at which the vapor pressure of a liquid equals the external atmospheric pressure is its:**
    A) Melting point
    B) Freezing point
    C) Boiling point
    D) Flash point
    *Answer: C*

12. **Which state of matter is the most compressible?**
    A) Solid
    B) Liquid
    C) Gas
    D) Glass
    *Answer: C*

13. **Plasma is often described as a 'gas-like' state consisting of:**
    A) Whole atoms only
    B) Neutrons and protons only
    C) Positively charged ions and free electrons
    D) Molecules of water
    *Answer: C*

14. **Where is plasma most commonly found in the universe?**
    A) In the Earth's core
    B) Inside stars (including the Sun)
    C) In the deep ocean
    D) In glaciers
    *Answer: B*

15. **The transition from a liquid to a gas at the surface of the liquid is called:**
    A) Boiling
    B) Evaporation
    C) Condensation
    D) Melting
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Solids and Liquids

16. **Solids that have a regular, repeating three-dimensional pattern of particles are:**
    A) Amorphous solids
    B) Crystalline solids
    C) Liquid crystals
    D) Plasma solids
    *Answer: B*

17. **Solids that lack an ordered internal structure (like glass or plastic) are:**
    A) Crystalline
    B) Amorphous
    C) Metallic
    D) Ionic
    *Answer: B*

18. **The smallest repeating unit of a crystal lattice is called a:**
    A) Molecule
    B) Atom
    C) Unit Cell
    D) Crystal Grain
    *Answer: C*

19. **Which type of crystalline solid is held together by covalent bonds (e.g., Diamond)?**
    A) Ionic solid
    B) Metallic solid
    C) Covalent Network solid
    D) Molecular solid
    *Answer: C*

20. **Which type of solid is a good conductor of electricity in both solid and liquid states?**
    A) Ionic solid
    B) Metallic solid
    C) Molecular solid
    D) Graphite (Network)
    *Answer: B (Ionic solids only conduct when melted or dissolved).*

21. **Viscosity is defined as a liquid's:**
    A) Resistance to flow
    B) Ability to evaporate
    C) Surface tension
    D) Boiling point
    *Answer: A*

22. **What happens to the viscosity of most liquids as the temperature increases?**
    A) It increases
    B) It decreases
    C) It stays the same
    D) It fluctuates randomly
    *Answer: B*

23. **The property of a liquid that causes it to behave as if its surface is covered by a stretched elastic membrane is:**
    A) Viscosity
    B) Surface Tension
    C) Capillary action
    D) Diffusion
    *Answer: B*

24. **The rise of a liquid in a narrow tube against the force of gravity is called:**
    A) Surface tension
    B) Capillary action
    C) Osmosis
    D) Siphoning
    *Answer: B*

25. **Liquids that evaporate easily at room temperature are described as:**
    A) Viscous
    B) Volatile
    C) Stable
    D) Inert
    *Answer: B*

26. **What is the significance of the 'Triple Point' on a phase diagram?**
    A) The point where only liquid and gas coexist
    B) The temperature and pressure where all three phases (solid, liquid, gas) coexist in equilibrium
    C) The absolute zero point
    D) The boiling point at high altitude
    *Answer: B*

27. **A 'Supercooled' liquid is a liquid that:**
    A) Is frozen solid
    B) Remains in the liquid state below its normal freezing point
    C) Is extremely hot
    D) Is under very high pressure
    *Answer: B*

28. **Water has an unusually high boiling point compared to other molecules of similar size because of:**
    A) Ionic bonding
    B) Metallic bonding
    C) Hydrogen bonding
    D) Dispersion forces only
    *Answer: C*

29. **When water freezes, it ______ in volume.**
    A) Decreases
    B) Increases (due to the formation of an open hexagonal lattice)
    C) Stays exactly the same
    D) Disappears
    *Answer: B*

30. **Which type of solid consists of molecules held together by relatively weak intermolecular forces (e.g., Ice or Dry Ice)?**
    A) Ionic solid
    B) Molecular solid
    C) Network solid
    D) Metallic solid
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Gases and Gas Laws

31. **The pressure of a gas is caused by:**
    A) The weight of the particles
    B) The collisions of particles with the walls of the container
    C) The temperature of the surroundings
    D) The color of the gas
    *Answer: B*

32. **Boyle's Law states that at constant temperature, the volume of a gas is ______ proportional to its pressure.**
    A) Directly
    B) Inversely
    C) Not
    D) Squarely
    *Answer: B ($P_1V_1 = P_2V_2$)*

33. **Charles's Law states that at constant pressure, the volume of a gas is ______ proportional to its absolute temperature (Kelvin).**
    A) Inversely
    B) Directly
    C) Squarely
    D) Logarithmically
    *Answer: B ($V_1/T_1 = V_2/T_2$)*

34. **Avogadro's Law states that equal volumes of gases at the same temperature and pressure contain equal numbers of:**
    A) Atoms only
    B) Particles or Molecules
    C) Grams
    D) Protons
    *Answer: B*

35. **The Ideal Gas Law is represented by the equation:**
    A) $PV = nRT$
    B) $P/V = RT$
    C) $V = PnT$
    D) $P + V = nRT$
    *Answer: A*

36. **The movement of gas particles from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration is:**
    A) Effusion
    B) Diffusion
    C) Convection
    D) Osmosis
    *Answer: B*

37. **The escape of gas particles through a tiny hole in a container is called:**
    A) Diffusion
    B) Effusion
    C) Evaporation
    D) Infusion
    *Answer: B*

38. **Graham's Law states that the rate of effusion/diffusion of a gas is inversely proportional to the square root of its:**
    A) Temperature
    ) Molar Mass
    C) Pressure
    D) Volume
    *Answer: B*

39. **Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures states that the total pressure of a mixture of gases is equal to the:**
    A) Average of the pressures
    B) Sum of the partial pressures of each gas
    C) Difference between the pressures
    D) Pressure of the most abundant gas
    *Answer: B*

40. **An 'Ideal Gas' is one that:**
    A) Exists in reality at all times
    B) Obeys all gas laws perfectly under all conditions (it assumes no volume for particles and no attractive forces)
    C) Is always colored
    D) Is toxic
    *Answer: B*

41. **Standard Molar Volume of an ideal gas at STP is approximately:**
    A) 1.0 L

42. **Gas temperature must always be converted to which scale for use in gas law calculations?**

43. **What is 'STP' in the context of gas study?**
