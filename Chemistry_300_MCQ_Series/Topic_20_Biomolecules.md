# Topic 20: Biomolecules (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Carbohydrates (Sugars and Starches)

1. **'Carbohydrates' are also known as:**
   A) Lipids
   B) Saccharides (Greek 'sakcharon' means sugar)
   C) Proteins
   D) Minerals
   *Answer: B*

2. **Chemically, carbohydrates are defined as ______-hydroxy aldehydes or ketones.**
   A) Mono
   B) Poly
   C) Di
   D) Non
   *Answer: B*

3. **The general formula for most simple carbohydrates is:**
   A) $C_n(H_2O)_n$
   B) $C_nH_{2n}$
   C) $R-COOH$
   D) $C_nH_{2n-2}$
   *Answer: A*

4. **The simplest carbohydrates that cannot be hydrolyzed into smaller ones are:**
   A) Disaccharides
   B) Monosaccharides (e.g., Glucose, Fructose)
   C) Polysaccharides
   *Answer: B*

5. **Carbohydrates that yield 2 to 10 monosaccharide units on hydrolysis are:**
   A) Monosaccharides
   B) Oligosaccharides (e.g., Sucrose, Lactose)
   C) Polysaccharides
   *Answer: B*

6. **Carbohydrates that yield a large number of monosaccharide units on hydrolysis are:**
   A) Sugars
   B) Polysaccharides (e.g., Starch, Cellulose)
   C) Lipids
   *Answer: B*

7. **Glucose ($C_6H_{12}O_6$) is also known as:**
   A) Fruit sugar
   B) Blood Sugar (or Grape sugar / Dextrose)
   C) Cane sugar
   D) Milk sugar
   *Answer: B*

8. **Fructose ($C_6H_{12}O_6$) is known as:**
   A) Blood sugar
   B) Fruit Sugar (Levulose)
   C) Malt sugar
   *Answer: B*

9. **Sucrose (Table sugar) is a disaccharide made of:**
   A) Two glucose molecules
   B) Glucose and Fructose
   C) Glucose and Galactose
   D) Two fructose molecules
   *Answer: B*

10. **Lactose (Milk sugar) is a disaccharide made of:**
    A) Glucose and Fructose
    B) Glucose and Galactose
    C) Two glucose units
    *Answer: B*

11. **Maltose is a disaccharide made of:**
    A) Glucose and Fructose
    B) Two Glucose units
    C) Two Galactose units
    *Answer: B*

12. **The link between two monosaccharide units in a di- or polysaccharide is the:**
    A) Peptide bond
    B) Glycosidic Linkage
    C) Ester bond
    D) Ionic bond
    *Answer: B*

13. **Starch is the main storage carbohydrate in:**
    A) Animals
    B) Plants (e.g., in potatoes, rice, corn)
    C) Bacteria only
    *Answer: B*

14. **Glycogen (Animal Starch) is the storage carbohydrate in:**
    A) Plants
    B) Animals (stored in liver and muscles)
    C) Fruits
    *Answer: B*

15. **Cellulose is the main component of the ______ in plants.**
    A) Cell wall
    B) Nucleus
    C) Mitochondria
    D) Cytoplasm
    *Answer: A*

## Part 2: Proteins and Amino Acids

16. **'Proteins' are high molecular weight polymers of:**
    A) Glucose
    B) Amino Acids
    C) Fatty acids
    D) Nucleotides
    *Answer: B*

17. **How many standard amino acids are found in human proteins?**
    A) 5
    B) 20
    C) 100
    D) 64
    *Answer: B*

18. **Amino acids that the body cannot synthesize and must be obtained from diet are:**
    A) Non-essential amino acids
    B) Essential amino acids
    C) Secondary amino acids
    *Answer: B*

19. **The bond that links amino acids in a protein chain is the:**
    A) Glycosidic bond
    B) Peptide Bond (Amide linkage)
    C) Phosphodiester bond
    *Answer: B*

20. **Proteins that act as biological catalysts are:**
    A) Hormones
    B) Enzymes
    C) Vitamins
    D) Minerals
    *Answer: B*

21. **The sequence of amino acids in a protein's polypeptide chain is its:**
    A) Primary Structure
    B) Secondary Structure
    C) Tertiary Structure
    D) Quaternary Structure
    *Answer: A*

22. **The coiling or folding of the polypeptide chain (like alpha-helix or beta-pleated sheets) is the:**
    A) Primary structure
    B) Secondary Structure
    C) Final structure
    *Answer: B*

23. **The overall three-dimensional shape of a protein is its:**
    A) Secondary structure
    B) Tertiary Structure
    C) First structure
    *Answer: B*

24. **'Denaturation' of a protein occurs due to changes in ______ or ______ resulting in loss of biological activity.**
    A) Color; Weight
    B) Temperature; pH
    C) Size; Name
    *Answer: B*

25. **Is the Primary structure of a protein lost during denaturation?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (only 2nd, 3rd, and 4th structures are disrupted)
    *Answer: B*

26. **Which protein is found in hair, nails, and skin?**
    A) Insulin
    B) Keratin
    C) Hemoglobin
    D) Gelatin
    *Answer: B*

27. **Insulin is a hormone that regulates ______ levels.**
    A) Salt
    ) Blood Glucose (Sugar)
    C) Calcium
    D) Iron
    *Answer: B*

28. **Hemoglobin is a protein used for ______ transport in the blood.**
    A) Sugar
    B) Oxygen
    C) Fat
    D) Carbon
    *Answer: B*

29. **Enzymes are highly ______ , usually catalyzing only one specific reaction.**
    A) General
    B) Specific
    C) Random
    *Answer: B*

30. **Which test is commonly used to detect the presence of proteins?**
    A) Benedict's test
    B) Biuret Test (gives a violet/purple color)
    C) Iodine test
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Lipids (Fats and Oils)

31. **Lipids are chemically ______ of fatty acids and glycerol.**
    A) Ethers
    B) Esters (specifically Triglycerides)
    C) Amides
    D) Polymers
    *Answer: B*

32. **Fats that are liquid at room temperature are usually called:**
    A) Solids
    B) Oils
    C) Sugars
    *Answer: B*

33. **Animal fats are usually ______ at room temperature.**
    A) Liquid
    B) Solid (due to saturated fatty acids)
    C) Gaseous
    *Answer: B*

34. **Vegetable oils are usually ______ at room temperature.**
    ) Solid
    B) Liquid (due to unsaturated fatty acids)
    *Answer: B*

35. **The reaction of fats with a base ($NaOH$) to form soap and glycerol is:**
    A) Hydrogenation
    B) Saponification
    C) Nitration
    D) Oxidation
    *Answer: B*

36. **Cholesterol is a prominent member of which lipid class?**
    A) Triglycerides
    B) Steroids
    C) Phospholipids
    D) Waxes
    *Answer: B*

37. **High levels of 'bad' cholesterol (LDL) are linked to:**
    A) Good health
    B) Heart disease (Atherosclerosis)
    C) Diabetes only
    *Answer: B*

38. **Phospholipids are major components of:**
    A) Bones
    B) Cell Membranes
    C) Hair
    D) Teeth
    *Answer: B*

39. **Hydrogenation of liquid oils produces solid ______ .**
    A) Soap
    B) Margarine/Vanaspati Ghee
    C) Sugar
    *Answer: B*

40. **Rancidity of fats is caused by ______ .**
    A) Cooling
    B) Oxidation (breaks down into smelly smaller molecules)
    C) Adding salt
    D) Darkness
    *Answer: B*

## Part 4: Nucleic Acids (DNA and RNA)

41. **'Nucleic Acids' are polymers of:**
    A) Amino acids
    B) Nucleotides
    C) Sugars
    D) Ions
    *Answer: B*

42. **The two types of nucleic acids are ______ and ______ .**
    A) DNA and RNA
    B) ATP and ADP
    C) NAD and FAD
    *Answer: A*

43. **DNA stands for:**
    A) Deoxyribonucleic Acid
    B) Dinucleic Acid
    C) Dual Nitrogen Acid
    *Answer: A*

44. **RNA stands for:**
    A) Ribonucleic Acid
    B) Round Nucleus Acid
    C) Rapid Nuclear Action
    *Answer: A*

45. **A nucleotide consists of three parts: a pentose sugar, a phosphate group, and a:**
    A) Metal ion
    B) Nitrogenous Base (A, T, C, G, U)
    C) Side chain
    *Answer: B*

46. **The sugar in DNA is ______ and in RNA is ______ .**
    A) Ribose; Deoxyribose
    B) Deoxyribose; Ribose
    *Answer: B*

47. **The nitrogenous bases in DNA are:**
    A) A, G, C, U
    B) Adenine (A), Guanine (G), Cytosine (C), Thymine (T)
    C) A, B, C, D
    *Answer: B*

48. **In RNA, Thymine (T) is replaced by which base?**
    A) Adenine
    B) Uracil (U)
    C) Guanine
    D) Cytosine
    *Answer: B*

49. **The double-helix structure of DNA was proposed by:**
    A) Mendel
    B) Watson and Crick
    C) Pasteur
    D) Darwin
    *Answer: B*

50. **In DNA, Adenine (A) always pairs with ______ and Guanine (G) with ______ .**
    A) C; T
    B) Thymine (T); Cytosine (C)
    C) U; A
    *Answer: B*

51. **The main function of DNA is to store and transmit:**
    A) Energy
    B) Genetic Information
    C) Protein directly
    D) Oxygen
    *Answer: B*

52. **The process of copying DNA is called:**
    ) Filtration
    B) Replication
    C) Transcription
    D) Translation
    *Answer: B*

53. **Synthesis of RNA from a DNA template is called:**
    A) Replication
    B) Transcription
    C) Translation
    D) Mutation
    *Answer: B*

54. **Synthesis of proteins from an RNA template (at the ribosome) is called:**
    A) Transcription
    B) Translation
    C) Replication
    D) Oxidation
    *Answer: B*

55. **ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) is known as the ______ currency of the cell.**
    A) Genetic
    B) Energy
    C) Structural
    *Answer: B*

56. **Vitamins are organic compounds required by the body in ______ amounts for normal health.**

    B) Small/Trace

    *Answer: B*
