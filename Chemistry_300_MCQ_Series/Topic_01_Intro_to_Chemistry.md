# Topic 01: Introduction to Chemistry (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Definition, Branches, and Importance

1. **How is 'Chemistry' best defined?**
   A) The study of living organisms and their environment
   B) The study of matter, its composition, properties, and the changes it undergoes
   C) The study of celestial bodies and the universe
   D) The study of human behavior and societies
   *Answer: B*

2. **The study of chemical processes within and relating to living organisms is known as:**
   A) Inorganic Chemistry
   B) Biochemistry
   C) Nuclear Chemistry
   D) Analytical Chemistry
   *Answer: B*

3. **Which branch of chemistry deals with the study of carbon-containing compounds (excluding simple oxides and carbonates)?**
   A) Inorganic Chemistry
   B) Physical Chemistry
   C) Organic Chemistry
   D) Industrial Chemistry
   *Answer: C*

4. **The branch of chemistry that focuses on substances that do not contain carbon-hydrogen bonds is:**
   A) Organic Chemistry
   B) Inorganic Chemistry
   C) Biochemistry
   D) Environmental Chemistry
   *Answer: B*

5. **Which branch of chemistry applies the techniques and theories of physics to the study of chemical systems?**
   A) Physical Chemistry
   B) Analytical Chemistry
   C) Biochemistry
   D) Organic Chemistry
   *Answer: A*

6. **The qualitative and quantitative determination of the components of a substance is the focus of:**
   A) Industrial Chemistry
   B) Analytical Chemistry
   C) Nuclear Chemistry
   D) Environmental Chemistry
   *Answer: B*

7. **Which branch of chemistry is involved in the manufacturing of chemical substances like fertilizers, glass, and cement on a large scale?**
   A) Analytical Chemistry
   B) Industrial Chemistry
   C) Environmental Chemistry
   D) Physical Chemistry
   *Answer: B*

8. **Nuclear Chemistry primarily deals with which of the following?**
   A) The arrangement of electrons in atoms
   B) Changes occurring in the atomic nucleus and radioactivity
   C) The study of carbon compounds
   D) The study of chemical reactions in the atmosphere
   *Answer: B*

9. **The study of the effects of chemicals on the environment and its inhabitants is called:**
   A) Industrial Chemistry
   B) Environmental Chemistry
   C) Biochemistry
   D) Physical Chemistry
   *Answer: B*

10. **Why is chemistry often called the 'Central Science'?**
    A) Because it is the easiest science to learn
    B) Because it connects physical sciences with life sciences and applied sciences
    C) Because it only deals with the center of the earth
    D) Because it was the first science ever discovered
    *Answer: B*

11. **The application of chemistry in the development of medicines and drugs is known as:**
    A) Medicinal/Pharmaceutical Chemistry
    B) Agricultural Chemistry
    C) Forensic Chemistry
    D) Nuclear Chemistry
    *Answer: A*

12. **Which of the following is an example of chemistry in daily life?**
    A) The process of digestion in the stomach
    B) The cleaning action of soap and detergents
    C) The rusting of an iron nail
    D) All of the above
    *Answer: D*

13. **Modern chemistry is considered to have started with the work of which scientist, often called the Father of Modern Chemistry?**
    A) Robert Boyle
    B) Antoine Lavoisier
    C) John Dalton
    D) Dmitri Mendeleev
    *Answer: B*

14. **Which branch of chemistry would be most useful in analyzing a soil sample to determine its nutrient content?**
    A) Organic Chemistry
    B) Analytical Chemistry
    C) Physical Chemistry
    D) Nuclear Chemistry
    *Answer: B*

15. **The study of the relationship between the chemical structure of a substance and its radioactive properties is part of:**
    A) Physical Chemistry
    B) Nuclear Chemistry
    C) Inorganic Chemistry
    D) Biochemistry
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Scientific Method and SI Units

16. **What is the first step in the scientific method?**

    B) Making an observation
    C) Conducting an experiment

    *Answer: B*

17. **A tentative, testable explanation for a scientific observation is a:**
    A) Law
    B) Hypothesis
    C) Theory
    D) Fact
    *Answer: B*

18. **A scientific theory is:**

    B) A well-substantiated explanation of an aspect of the natural world
    C) A statement that never changes
