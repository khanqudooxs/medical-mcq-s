# Topic 09: Chemical Equilibrium (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Reversible Reactions and the Nature of Equilibrium

1. **A reaction that can proceed in both forward and reverse directions is called a/an:**
   A) Irreversible reaction
   B) Reversible reaction
   C) Final reaction
   D) One-way reaction
   *Answer: B*

2. **A reaction that proceeds in only one direction until completion is called a/an:**
   A) Reversible reaction
   B) Irreversible reaction
   C) Cyclic reaction
   D) Oscillating reaction
   *Answer: B*

3. **'Chemical Equilibrium' is reached in a reversible reaction when:**
   A) The reaction completely stops
   B) The rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction
   C) All reactants are converted to products
   D) The mass of reactants equals the mass of products
   *Answer: B*

4. **At chemical equilibrium, the concentrations of reactants and products:**
   A) Are always equal
   B) Become zero
   C) Remain constant over time (though the reaction is still happening at a microscopic level)
   D) Always keep changing
   *Answer: C*

5. **Why is chemical equilibrium described as 'Dynamic'?**
   A) Because the reaction has stopped
   B) Because the forward and reverse reactions continue to occur at the same rate
   C) Because it only happens in moving liquids
   D) Because it is high in energy
   *Answer: B*

6. **Static equilibrium is found in ______ , while dynamic equilibrium is found in ______ .**
   A) Physical systems; Chemical reactions
   B) Chemical reactions; Physics
   C) Gaseous states; Solids
   D) Biology; Chemistry
   *Answer: A*

7. **For a reversible reaction to reach equilibrium, it must occur in a:**
   A) Open container
   B) Closed system (to prevent loss of reactants or products)
   C) Vacuum
   D) Large factory
   *Answer: B*

8. **Equilibrium can be recognized by the constancy of macroscopic properties such as:**
   A) Density
   B) Color intensity
   C) Pressure/Concentration
   D) All of the above
   *Answer: D*

9. **Can equilibrium be approached from either side (reactants or products)?**
   A) Yes
   B) No
   *Answer: A*

10. **A state of equilibrium involving the same phase for all reactants and products is:**
    A) Heterogeneous equilibrium
    B) Homogeneous equilibrium
    C) Solid equilibrium
    D) Gaseous only
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Law of Mass Action and Equilibrium Constant

11. **The Law of Mass Action was proposed by:**
    A) Haber and Bosch
    B) Guldberg and Waage
    C) Le Chatelier
    D) John Dalton
    *Answer: B*

12. **The Law of Mass Action states that the rate of a chemical reaction is proportional to the ______ of the reacting substances.**
    A) Total mass
    B) Product of the active masses (molar concentrations)
    C) Volume
    D) Temperature
    *Answer: B*

13. **For the general reaction $aA + bB \rightleftharpoons cC + dD$, the equilibrium constant $K_c$ is:**
    A) $[A][B] / [C][D]$
    B) $[C]^c [D]^d / [A]^a [B]^b$
    C) $a[A] + b[B]$
    D) $[C][D] - [A][B]$
   *Answer: B*

14. **In the expression for $K_c$, the concentrations of products are in the ______ and reactants are in the ______ .**
    A) Denominator; Numerator
    B) Numerator; Denominator
    C) Middle; End
    *Answer: B*

15. **The equilibrium constant $K_p$ is expressed in terms of:**
    A) Moles
    B) Partial Pressures (for gaseous reactions)
    C) Density
    D) Speed
    *Answer: B*

16. **How are $K_p$ and $K_c$ related?**
    A) $K_p = K_c$
    B) $K_p = K_c (RT)^{\Delta n}$
    C) $K_p = K_c / RT$
    D) $K_p = K_c + RT$
    *Answer: B*

17. **If the number of moles of gaseous products and reactants is equal ($\Delta n = 0$), then:**
    A) $K_p > K_c$
    B) $K_p < K_c$
    C) $K_p = K_c$
    D) $K_p = 0$
    *Answer: C*

18. **A very large value of $K_c$ (e.g., $10^{15}$) indicates that:**
    A) The reaction hardly starts
    B) At equilibrium, the mixture consists mostly of products (reaction almost complete)
    C) At equilibrium, the mixture consists mostly of reactants
    D) The reaction is very slow
    *Answer: B*

19. **A very small value of $K_c$ (e.g., $10^{-15}$) indicates that:**
    A) The reaction goes to completion
    B) Equilibrium is reached mostly on the reactant side (reaction hardly proceeds)
    C) The reaction is explosive
    D) The speed is high
    *Answer: B*

20. **$K_c$ remains constant for a specific reaction at a constant:**
    A) Pressure
    B) Volume
    C) Temperature
    D) Concentration
    *Answer: C*

21. **The 'Reaction Quotient' ($Q$) is calculated like $K_c$ but at:**
    A) Equilibrium only
    B) Any time during the reaction
    C) The start only
    *Answer: B*

22. **If $Q < K_c$, in which direction will the reaction proceed to reach equilibrium?**
    A) Backward (toward reactants)
    B) Forward (toward products)
    C) It's already at equilibrium
    *Answer: B*

23. **If $Q > K_c$, in which direction will the reaction proceed?**
    A) Forward
    B) Backward (toward reactants)
    C) No change
    *Answer: B*

24. **If $Q = K_c$, the system is:**
    A) Starting
    B) At Equilibrium
    C) Exploding
    *Answer: B*

25. **The equilibrium constant for the reverse reaction is the ______ of the $K_c$ of the forward reaction.**
    A) Negative
    B) Reciprocal (1 / $K_c$)
    C) Square
    D) Same
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Le Chatelier’s Principle

26. **Le Chatelier’s Principle states that if stress is applied to a system at equilibrium, the system will:**
    A) Break
    B) Shift its equilibrium position to counteract or 'undo' the stress
    C) Stop completely
    D) Become irreversible
    *Answer: B*

27. **Common 'stresses' that can affect equilibrium position include changes in:**
    A) Concentration
    B) Pressure/Volume
    C) Temperature
    D) All of the above
    *Answer: D*

28. **If more reactant is added to an equilibrium system, the equilibrium shifts toward the:**
    A) Reactants side
    B) Products side (Forward)
    C) It won't shift
    *Answer: B*

29. **If product is removed from an equilibrium system, the equilibrium shifts toward the:**
    A) Reactants
    B) Products side (Forward)
    *Answer: B*

30. **For gaseous reactions, an increase in pressure (decrease in volume) shifts equilibrium toward the side with:**
    A) More moles of gas
    B) Fewer moles of gas
    C) No change
    D) Solids
    *Answer: B*

31. **For the reaction $N_2(g) + 3H_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NH_3(g)$, increasing pressure will:**
    A) Shift equilibrium to the left
    B) Shift equilibrium to the right (more ammonia produced)
    C) Have no effect
    *Answer: B (4 moles of gas $\to$ 2 moles of gas).*

32. **If a gaseous reaction has equal moles of gas on both sides ($\Delta n=0$), a change in pressure will:**
    A) Shift it left
    B) Shift it right
    C) Have no effect on the equilibrium position
    *Answer: C*

33. **An increase in temperature for an ENDOTHERMIC reaction shifts the equilibrium toward the:**
    A) Reactants
    B) Products (Forward)
    C) Left
    *Answer: B (Heat is like a reactant here).*

34. **An increase in temperature for an EXOTHERMIC reaction shifts the equilibrium toward the:**
    A) Products
    B) Reactants (Backward)
    *Answer: B (Heat is like a product here).*

35. **Which of the following always changes the value of the equilibrium constant ($K_c$)?**
    A) Pressure change
    B) Concentration change
    C) Temperature change
    D) Adding a catalyst
    *Answer: C*

36. **Adding an inert gas (like Helium) to a gaseous mixture at constant volume will:**
    A) Shift equilibrium right
    B) Shift equilibrium left
    C) Have no effect on the equilibrium position
    *Answer: C*

37. **Adding a catalyst to a system at equilibrium will:**
    A) Shift it to the right
    B) Shift it to the left
    C) Not affect the equilibrium position (it only speeds up reaching it)
    *Answer: C*

38. **In the 'Haber Process' for ammonia, the optimum yield is obtained at ______ pressure and ______ temperature.**
    A) Low; High
    B) High; Moderate/Low (with catalyst)
    C) Low; Low
    D) Zero; High
    *Answer: B*

39. **The Solubility Product Constant ($K_{sp}$) is used for ______ electrolytes.**
    A) Highly soluble
    B) Sparingly soluble (hardly soluble)
    C) Liquid
    D) Gaseous
    *Answer: B*

40. **The decrease in the solubility of a salt in the presence of an ion that already exists in the solution is called the:**
    A) Rare ion effect
    B) Common Ion Effect
    C) Unusual ion effect
    D) Buffer effect
    *Answer: B*

## Part 4: Ionic Equilibrium and Solubility
