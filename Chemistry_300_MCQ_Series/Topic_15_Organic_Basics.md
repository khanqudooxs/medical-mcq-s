# Topic 15: Organic Chemistry Basics (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: History and Classification

1. **'Organic Chemistry' is the study of compounds containing:**
   A) Silicon
   B) Carbon
   C) Oxygen only
   D) Metals
   *Answer: B*

2. **The 'Vital Force Theory' suggested that organic compounds could only be produced by:**
   A) Laboratory machines
   B) Living organisms (Life force)
   C) Sunlight
   D) Volcanoes
   *Answer: B*

3. **Who synthesized 'Urea' (the first organic compound in the lab) from an inorganic substance (Ammonium cyanate) in 1828?**
   A) Louis Pasteur
   B) Friedrich Wöhler
   C) Dmitri Mendeleev
   D) John Dalton
   *Answer: B (This disproved the Vital Force Theory).*

4. **Carbon is unique because of its 'Catenation' property. What is catenation?**
   A) Ability to conduct electricity
   B) Ability to form long chains and rings by bonding with other carbon atoms
   C) Ability to melt easily
   D) Ability to react with water
   *Answer: B*

5. **The valency of Carbon is always:**
   A) 2
   B) 4 (Tetravalency)
   C) 6
   D) 0
   *Answer: B*

6. **Compounds consisting of only Carbon and Hydrogen are called:**
   A) Carbohydrates
   B) Hydrocarbons
   C) Polymers
   D) Isotopes
   *Answer: B*

7. **Organic compounds that contain straight or branched chains of carbon atoms are:**
   A) Cyclic
   B) Acyclic or Aliphatic
   C) Aromatic
   D) Inorganic
   *Answer: B*

8. **Organic compounds that contain at least one ring of carbon atoms (like Cyclohexane) are:**
   A) Acyclic
   B) Cyclic (or Ring compounds)
   C) Aliphatic
   *Answer: B*

9. **Compounds that contain a special 6-carbon ring with alternating double bonds (Benzene ring) are:**
   A) Aliphatic
   B) Aromatic (or Arenes)
   C) Saturated
   D) Alkyne
   *Answer: B*

10. **A series of compounds where members differ by a constant unit (usually $-CH_2-$) is a:**
    A) Cyclic series
    B) Homologous Series
    C) Random series
    D) Parallel series
    *Answer: B*

11. **Members of a homologous series have ______ chemical properties and ______ physical properties.**
    A) Different; Same
    B) Similar; Gradually changing
    C) Same; Same
    *Answer: B*

12. **In an 'Open-chain' compound, the carbon atoms are connected in a:**
    A) Loop
    ) Continuous sequence (straight or branched)
    C) Sphere
    *Answer: B*

13. **Cyclobutane is an example of a/an ______ compound.**
    A) Acyclic
    B) Alicyclic (Alicyclic = Aliphatic + Cyclic)
    C) Aromatic
    *Answer: B*

14. **Heterocyclic compounds are those where the ring contains atoms other than carbon, such as:**
    A) N, O, or S
    B) Only H
    C) Only Metals
    *Answer: A*

15. **Pyridine (contains N in the ring) is a/an ______ compound.**
    A) Homocyclic
    B) Heterocyclic
    C) Open-chain
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Functional Groups

16. **An atom or group of atoms that determines the characteristic chemical properties of an organic compound is the:**
    A) Molecular weight
    B) Functional Group
    C) Isomer
    D) Radical
    *Answer: B*

17. **Organic compounds with the same functional group belong to the same ______ .**
    A) Color
    B) Class (e.g., Alcohols, Acids)
    C) Weight
    *Answer: B*

18. **The functional group $-OH$ (Hydroxyl) is characteristic of:**
    A) Aldehydes
    B) Alcohols
    C) Ethers
    D) Carboxylic acids
    *Answer: B*

19. **The functional group $-CHO$ (Formyl) is characteristic of:**
    A) Ketones
    B) Aldehydes
    C) Alcohols
    D) Esters
    *Answer: B*

20. **The functional group $>C=O$ (non-terminal Carbonyl) is characteristic of:**
    A) Aldehydes
    B) Ketones
    C) Ethers
    D) Amines
    *Answer: B*

21. **The functional group $-COOH$ (Carboxyl) is characteristic of:**
    A) Esters
    B) Carboxylic Acids
    C) Amides
    D) Ketones
    *Answer: B*

22. **The functional group $-O-$ (Oxy) between two carbon chains is characteristic of:**
    A) Alcohols
    B) Ethers
    C) Esters
    D) Amines
    *Answer: B*

23. **The functional group $-COOR$ (Alkoxycarbonyl) is characteristic of:**
    A) Ethers
    B) Esters (fragrant compounds)
    C) Carboxylic acids
    *Answer: B*

24. **The functional group $-NH_2$ (Amino) is characteristic of:**
    A) Amines
    B) Nitro compounds
    C) Amides
    D) Nitriles
    *Answer: A*

25. **The functional group $-CONH_2$ (Amido) is characteristic of:**
    A) Amines
    B) Amides
    C) Nitriles
    D) Halides
    *Answer: B*

26. **What is the functional group of Alkenes?**
    A) $C-C$ single bond
    B) $C=C$ double bond
    C) $C\equiv C$ triple bond
    *Answer: B*

27. **What is the functional group of Alkynes?**
    A) $C=C$
    B) $C\equiv C$ (triple bond)
    C) $-X$
    *Answer: B*

28. **'Haloalkanes' contain which functional group?**
    A) $-OH$
    B) $-X$ (Halogen like Cl, Br, I)
    C) $-CHO$
    *Answer: B*

29. **Which class of compounds is known for pleasant, fruity smells?**
    A) Amines (smell of fish)
    B) Esters
    C) Carboxylic acids (stink)
    D) Ethers
    *Answer: B*

30. **Amines have what kind of odor?**
    A) Sweet
    B) Fishy/Ammonia-like
    C) Fruity
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Isomerism and Bonding

31. **Compounds having the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements are called:**
    A) Isotopes
    B) Isomers
    C) Homologues
    D) Polymers
    *Answer: B*

32. **'Structural Isomerism' occurs when atoms are bonded in:**
    A) The same order
    B) Different orders/sequences
    C) Only rings
    *Answer: B*

33. **Different carbon chain lengths (straight vs branched) for the same formula result in:**
    A) Position isomerism
    B) Chain Isomerism
    C) Functional isomerism
    *Answer: B*

34. **Butane and Isobutane (both $C_4H_{10}$) are examples of:**
    ) Chain isomers
    B) Functional isomers
    C) Tautomers
    *Answer: A*

35. **'Position Isomerism' occurs when the functional group is at:**
    A) Different locations on the same carbon chain
    B) The end only
    C) Different molecules
    *Answer: A*

36. **1-Propanol and 2-Propanol are examples of:**
    A) Chain isomers
    B) Position Isomers
    C) Tautomers
    *Answer: B*

37. **'Functional Group Isomerism' occurs when molecules have the same formula but:**
    A) Different functional groups (e.g., Ethanol and Dimethyl ether)
    B) Different chains
    C) Different colors
    *Answer: A*

38. **Ethanol ($C_2H_5OH$) and Dimethyl Ether ($CH_3OCH_3$) are:**
    A) Chain isomers
    B) Functional group isomers
    C) Metamers
    *Answer: B*

39. **'Metamerism' is caused by an unequal distribution of carbon atoms on either side of a:**
    A) Triple bond
    B) Functional group like $-O-$ or $-S-$ (e.g., in ethers)
    C) Side chain
    *Answer: B*

40. **A special type of functional isomerism where isomers exist in dynamic equilibrium (like Keto-Enol) is:**
    A) Metamerism
    B) Tautomerism
    C) Chain isomerism
    *Answer: B*

41. **'Stereoisomerism' is due to the different ______ of atoms in space.**
    A) Mass
    B) Spatial arrangement (3D)
    C) Color
    *Answer: B*

42. **Geometric (Cis-Trans) isomerism is common in alkenes because of:**
    A) Free rotation around single bonds
    B) Restricted rotation around double bonds ($C=C$)
    C) High heat
    *Answer: B*

43. **In 'Cis' isomer, similar groups are on the ______ side of the double bond.**
    A) Opposite
    B) Same
    *Answer: B*

44. **In 'Trans' isomer, similar groups are on the ______ side.**
    A) Same
    B) Opposite
    *Answer: B*

45. **Which type of isomerism involves non-superimposable mirror images (like left and right hands)?**
    A) Geometric
    B) Optical Isomerism (Enantiomers)
    C) Chain
    *Answer: B*

59. **Which hybridization is found in the carbon atoms of Alkanes (single bonds)?**

60. **Which hybridization is found in the doubly bonded carbons of Alkenes?**

62. **Homolytic fission of a covalent bond results in the formation of:**
    A) Ions

    *Answer: B*

63. **Heterolytic fission results in the formation of:**
    A) Radicals
    B) Ions (Carbocations and Carbanions)
    C) Gases

64. **A positively charged carbon atom is called a:**
    A) Carbanion
    B) Carbocation (or Carbonium ion)
    C) Radical
    *Answer: B*

65. **A negatively charged carbon atom is called a:**
    A) Carbocation
    B) Carbanion
    C) Neutral atom
    *Answer: B*

66. **'Electrophiles' are species that are:**
    A) Electron rich
    B) Electron loving/seeking (positively charged or neutral electron-deficient)
    C) Neutral always
    *Answer: B*

67. **'Nucleophiles' are species that are:**
    A) Electron seeking
    B) Nucleus loving/seeking (electron rich, with lone pairs or negative charge)
    C) Radioactive
    *Answer: B*

68. **Which of the following is a nucleophile?**
    A) $H^+$
    B) $OH^-$
    C) $BF_3$
    D) $Cl^+$
    *Answer: B*

69. **Which of the following is an electrophile?**
    A) $NH_3$
    B) $H_2O$
    C) $NO_2^+$
    D) $CN^-$
    *Answer: C*

70. **Inductive Effect (I-effect) is the permanent displacement of ______ along a carbon chain.**
    A) Protons
    B) Sigma ($\sigma$) electrons towards a more electronegative atom
    C) Pi ($\pi$) electrons
    *Answer: B*

71. **Alkyl groups are generally electron-______ through the inductive effect ($+I$).**
    A) Withdrawing
    B) Donating/Releasing
    C) Conserving
    *Answer: B*

72. **The delocalization of pi ($\pi$) electrons or lone pairs through a system of alternating single/double bonds is:**
    A) Resonance (or Mesomeric Effect)
    ) Inductive effect
    C) Solubility
    *Answer: A*

73. **Energy is always required to break a bond.**
    A) True
    B) False

74. **In the 'Resonance' model of Benzene, all carbon-carbon bonds are:**
    A) Pure single bonds

    C) Identical and intermediate between single and double

75. **Which is more stable: a Tertiary carbocation or a Primary carbocation?**
