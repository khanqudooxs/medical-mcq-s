# Topic 17: Alcohols, Phenols & Ethers (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Alcohols (Structure and Classification)

1. **Alcohols are organic compounds containing the ______ functional group.**
   A) Carbonyl ($>C=O$)
   B) Hydroxyl ($-OH$)
   C) Carboxyl ($-COOH$)
   D) Amino ($-NH_2$)
   *Answer: B*

2. **The general formula for aliphatic alcohols is:**
   A) $R-H$
   B) $R-OH$
   C) $R-CHO$
   D) $R-O-R$
   *Answer: B*

3. **An alcohol in which the $-OH$ group is attached to a carbon bonded to only one other carbon (or no other carbons) is a:**
   A) Primary Alcohol ($1^\circ$)
   B) Secondary Alcohol
   C) Tertiary Alcohol
   D) Quaternary Alcohol
   *Answer: A*

4. **An alcohol in which the $-OH$ group is attached to a carbon bonded to two other carbons is a:**
   A) Primary Alcohol
   B) Secondary Alcohol ($2^\circ$)
   C) Tertiary Alcohol
   *Answer: B*

5. **An alcohol in which the $-OH$ group is attached to a carbon bonded to three other carbons is a:**
   A) Primary Alcohol
   B) Secondary Alcohol
   C) Tertiary Alcohol ($3^\circ$)
   *Answer: C*

6. **Ethanol ($CH_3CH_2OH$) is a ______ alcohol.**
   A) Primary
   B) Secondary
   C) Tertiary
   *Answer: A*

7. **Isopropanol (2-Propanol) is a ______ alcohol.**
   A) Primary
   B) Secondary
   C) Tertiary
   *Answer: B*

8. **2-Methyl-2-propanol (t-butyl alcohol) is a ______ alcohol.**
   A) Primary
   B) Secondary
   C) Tertiary
   *Answer: C*

9. **Alcohols containing two $-OH$ groups are called:**
   A) Monohydric
   B) Dihydric (or Glycols)
   C) Trihydric
   D) Polyhydric
   *Answer: B*

10. **Glycerol (Glycerin) is a ______ alcohol containing three $-OH$ groups.**
    A) Dihydric
    B) Trihydric
    C) Monohydric
    *Answer: B*

11. **Wood spirit is a common name for:**
    A) Ethanol
    B) Methanol ($CH_3OH$)
    C) Isopropanol
    D) Phenol
    *Answer: B*

12. **Grain alcohol is a common name for:**
    A) Methanol
    B) Ethanol ($C_2H_5OH$)
    C) Glycol
    *Answer: B*

13. **Why do alcohols have much higher boiling points than alkanes of similar molar mass?**
    A) They are heavier
    B) Due to Intermolecular Hydrogen Bonding
    C) They are always liquid
    D) They are ionic
    *Answer: B*

14. **Lower alcohols (C1 to C3) are ______ in water.**
    A) Insoluble
    B) Highly soluble (due to hydrogen bonding)
    C) Sparingly soluble
    *Answer: B*

15. **Solubility of alcohols in water ______ as the alkyl chain length increases.**
    A) Increases
    B) Decreases (as the non-polar part becomes larger)
    C) Stays same
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Preparation and Tests of Alcohols

16. **Industrial production of Ethanol by the action of yeast on sugars is called:**
    ) Distillation
    B) Fermentation
    C) Oxidation
    D) Hydrogenation
    *Answer: B*

17. **Which enzyme in yeast converts Glucose into Ethanol and $CO_2$?**
    A) Invertase
    B) Zymase
    C) Diastase
    D) Maltase
    *Answer: B*

18. **Alcohol that has been made unfit for drinking by adding poisonous substances like Methanol or Pyridine is:**
    A) Absolute alcohol
    B) Denatured Alcohol (Methylated Spirit)
    C) Rectified spirit
    D) Power alcohol
    *Answer: B*

19. **100% pure Ethanol is known as:**
    A) Rectified spirit
    B) Absolute Alcohol
    C) Methylated spirit
    D) Proof spirit
    *Answer: B*

20. **Reagent used to distinguish between Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary alcohols is:**
    A) Tollen's reagent
    B) Lucas Reagent (conc. $HCl + ZnCl_2$)
    C) Fehling's solution
    D) Benedict's solution
    *Answer: B*

21. **In the Lucas test, which alcohol reacts immediately to form a cloudy solution?**
    A) Primary
    B) Secondary
    C) Tertiary (reacts in seconds)
    *Answer: C*

22. **In the Lucas test, which alcohol reacts after 5-10 minutes?**
    A) Primary
    B) Secondary
    C) Tertiary
    *Answer: B*

23. **In the Lucas test, which alcohol does not react at room temperature (stays clear)?**
    A) Primary
    B) Secondary
    C) Tertiary
    *Answer: A*

24. **Oxidation of a Primary alcohol with $K_2Cr_2O_7/H^+$ produces a/an ______ which can further oxidize to a/an ______ .**
    A) Ketone; Acid
    B) Aldehyde; Carboxylic Acid
    C) Ether; Acid
    *Answer: B*

25. **Oxidation of a Secondary alcohol produces a:**
    A) Aldehyde
    B) Ketone
    C) Ester
    D) Acid (harder to form)
    *Answer: B*

26. **Tertiary alcohols are resistant to oxidation under normal conditions.**
    A) True
    B) False
    *Answer: A*

27. **Dehydration of an alcohol (using conc. $H_2SO_4$ at 170°C) produces a/an:**
    A) Alkane
    B) Alkene (e.g., Ethanol $\to$ Ethene)
    C) Alkyne
    D) Ether
    *Answer: B*

28. **Dehydration of Ethanol at a lower temperature (140°C) produces:**
    A) Ethene
    B) Diethyl Ether
    C) Ethane
    D) Acetic acid
    *Answer: B*

29. **Alcohols react with active metals (like Na) to release which gas?**
    A) $O_2$
    B) $H_2$
    C) $CO_2$
    D) $Cl_2$
    *Answer: B*

30. **'Esterification' is the reaction between an Alcohol and a ______ to form an Ester and Water.**
    A) Halide
    B) Carboxylic Acid
    C) Base
    D) Metal
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Phenols (Aromatic Alcohols)

31. **Phenol is an organic compound where the $-OH$ group is directly attached to a:**
    A) Straight chain
    B) Benzene ring ($C_6H_5OH$)
    C) Alkane chain
    D) Double bond
    *Answer: B*

32. **Phenol is also known as:**
    A) Alcohol
    B) Carbolic Acid
    C) Acetic acid
    D) Salicylic acid
    *Answer: B*

33. **Phenol is ______ acidic than Alcohols.**
    A) Less
    B) More (due to resonance stabilization of the phenoxide ion)
    C) Equally
    *Answer: B*

34. **Does Phenol react with $NaOH$ to form salt and water?**
    A) Yes (Sodium phenoxide)
    B) No
    *Answer: A*

35. **Does Ethanol react with $NaOH$ solution?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (It is not acidic enough to react with $NaOH$, though it reacts with Na metal).
    *Answer: B*

36. **Phenol reacts with neutral $FeCl_3$ solution to give a ______ color.**
    A) Red
    B) Blue/Violet
    C) Green
    D) Yellow
    *Answer: B (This is a test for phenols).*

37. **Nitration of Phenol with dilute $HNO_3$ gives:**
    A) Picric acid
    B) o- and p-Nitrophenol
    C) Benzene
    *Answer: B*

38. **Nitration of Phenol with concentrated $HNO_3$ produces 'Picric Acid', which is chemically:**
    A) 2-Nitrophenol
    B) 2,4,6-Trinitrophenol
    C) Benzoic acid
    *Answer: B*

39. **Phenol reacts with Bromine water to give a ______ precipitate of 2,4,6-Tribromophenol.**
    A) Red
    B) White
    C) Black
    D) Green
    *Answer: B*

40. **Distillation of Phenol with Zinc dust produces:**
    A) Ethanol
    B) Benzene
    C) Toluene
    D) Phenoxide
    *Answer: B*

41. **Bakelite, a famous plastic, is a polymer of Phenol and ______ .**
    A) Acetaldehyde
    B) Formaldehyde
    C) Acetone
    D) Glycerol
    *Answer: B*

42. **'Kolbe’s Reaction' of sodium phenoxide with $CO_2$ produces:**
    A) Benzoic acid
    B) Salicylic Acid (used to make Aspirin)
    C) Benzene
    *Answer: B*

43. **Aspirin is chemically:**
    A) Methyl salicylate
    B) Acetylsalicylic Acid
    C) Phenyl salicylate
    *Answer: B*

44. **Phenol is toxic and can cause systemic poisoning if absorbed through the skin.**
    A) True
    B) False
    *Answer: A*

45. **Which is more soluble in water?**
    A) Ethanol
    B) Phenol
    *Answer: A*

## Part 4: Ethers (Structure and Properties)

46. **Ethers are organic compounds where an Oxygen atom is bonded to two:**
    A) Hydrogen atoms
    B) Alkyl or Aryl groups ($R-O-R^\prime$)
    C) Metals
    D) Halogens
    *Answer: B*

47. **If the two groups attached to the oxygen are the same, the ether is called:**
    A) Simple or Symmetrical Ether (e.g., $CH_3-O-CH_3$)
    B) Mixed or Unsymmetrical Ether
    C) Double ether
    *Answer: A*

48. **If the two groups are different, the ether is called:**
    A) Simple
    B) Mixed or Unsymmetrical (e.g., $CH_3-O-C_2H_5$)
    *Answer: B*

49. **Ethers are functional isomers of ______ with the same molecular formula.**
    A) Alkanes
    B) Alcohols (e.g., Dimethyl ether and Ethanol)
    C) Acids
    D) Ketones
    *Answer: B*

50. **Diethyl ether was historically used as an:**
    A) Antiseptic
    B) Anesthetic (during surgery)
    C) Fertilizer
    D) Food dye
    *Answer: B*

51. **Ethers have ______ boiling points compared to alcohols of similar mass because they ______ .**
    A) Higher; can hydrogen bond
    B) Lower; cannot form intermolecular hydrogen bonds
    C) Equal; are same
    *Answer: B*

    A) Yes
