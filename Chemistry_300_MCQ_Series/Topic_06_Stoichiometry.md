# Topic 06: Stoichiometry (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Relative Masses and the Mole Concept

1. **What is 'Stoichiometry' primarily concerned with?**
   A) The color of chemical reactions
   B) The quantitative relationship between reactants and products in a chemical reaction
   C) The speed of reactions
   D) The safety of laboratory equipment
   *Answer: B*

2. **The mass of an atom compared to 1/12th the mass of a Carbon-12 atom is its:**
   A) Relative Atomic Mass
   B) Absolute Mass
   C) Density
   D) Molarity
   *Answer: A*

3. **What is the value of one Atomic Mass Unit (amu) in grams?**
   A) $1.66 \times 10^{-24} g$
   B) $1.00 g$
   C) $9.11 \times 10^{-31} g$
   D) $6.02 \times 10^{23} g$
   *Answer: A*

4. **The sum of the atomic masses of all atoms in a molecule is the:**
   A) Formula Mass
   B) Molecular Mass
   C) Average Mass
   D) Total Mass
   *Answer: B*

5. **For ionic compounds (which don't have separate molecules), the term used instead of molecular mass is:**
   A) Formula Mass
   B) Atomic Mass
   C) Molar Volume
   D) Crystal Mass
   *Answer: A*

6. **The 'Mole' is the SI unit for:**
   A) Temperature
   B) Mass
   C) Amount of substance
   D) Volume
   *Answer: C*

7. **How many particles are contained in exactly one mole of any substance?**
   A) $1.66 \times 10^{-24}$
   B) $6.022 \times 10^{23}$
   C) $3.00 \times 10^8$
   D) 100
   *Answer: B*

8. **The number $6.022 \times 10^{23}$ is known as:**
   A) Dalton's Number
   B) Avogadro's Number ($N_A$)
   C) Planck's Constant
   D) Boyle's Constant
   *Answer: B*

9. **The mass of one mole of a substance expressed in grams is its:**
   A) Atomic mass
   B) Molar Mass
   C) Molecular weight
   D) Relative mass
   *Answer: B*

10. **The molar mass of Carbon (C) is exactly:**
    A) 1 gram/mol
    B) 12 grams/mol
    C) 14 grams/mol
    D) 6 grams/mol
    *Answer: B*

11. **The molar mass of Oxygen gas ($O_2$) is:**
    A) 16 g/mol
    B) 32 g/mol
    C) 8 g/mol
    D) 48 g/mol
    *Answer: B ($16 \times 2$)*

12. **The molar mass of Water ($H_2O$) is approximately:**
    A) 10 g/mol
    B) 18 g/mol
    C) 16 g/mol
    D) 2 g/mol
    *Answer: B ($2 \times 1.0 + 16.0$)*

13. **How many moles are in 36 grams of water?**
    A) 1 mole
    B) 2 moles
    C) 0.5 moles
    D) 18 moles
    *Answer: B ($36 / 18 = 2$)*

14. **What is the mass of 0.5 moles of Sulfuric Acid ($H_2SO_4$, Molar Mass = 98 g/mol)?**
    A) 98 g
    B) 49 g
    C) 196 g
    D) 50 g
    *Answer: B*

15. **One mole of $CO_2$ contains how many oxygen atoms?**
    A) $6.022 \times 10^{23}$
    B) $1.204 \times 10^{24}$ (2 times Avogadro's number)
    C) 2 atoms
    D) 16 atoms
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Percentage Composition and Empirical Formula

16. **The simplest whole-number ratio of atoms of each element in a compound is the:**
    A) Molecular Formula
    B) Empirical Formula
    C) Structural Formula
    D) Stoichiometric Formula
    *Answer: B*

17. **The formula that shows the actual number of atoms of each element in a molecule is the:**
    A) Empirical Formula
    B) Molecular Formula
    C) Ionic Formula
    D) Basic Formula
    *Answer: B*

18. **If the molecular formula of Glucose is $C_6H_{12}O_6$, its empirical formula is:**
    A) $C_6H_{12}O_6$
    B) $CH_2O$
    C) $CHO$
    D) $C_2H_4O_2$
    *Answer: B*

19. **Benzene has the molecular formula $C_6H_6$. Its empirical formula is:**
    A) $C_6H_6$
    B) $CH$
    C) $C_2H_2$
    *Answer: B*

20. **Which compound has the same empirical and molecular formula?**
    A) $H_2O$
    B) $C_2H_4$
    C) $C_6H_{12}O_6$
    D) $H_2O_2$
    *Answer: A*

21. **Percentage composition of an element in a compound is calculated as:**
    A) $(Total mass of element / Molar mass of compound) \times 100$

    A) 50%
    B) 88.9%
    C) 11.1%

23. **The mass percentage of Hydrogen in $H_2O$ is approximately:**
    A) 50%
    B) 11.1%
    C) 20%
    D) 5%
    *Answer: B*

24. **Combustion analysis is usually used to find the empirical formula of:**
    A) Metals
    B) Organic compounds (containing Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen)
    C) Noble gases
    D) Salts
    *Answer: B*

25. **In combustion analysis, Carbon is converted into ______ and Hydrogen into ______ .**
    A) $CO$ and $H_2$
    B) $CO_2$ and $H_2O$
    C) $C$ and $H$
    D) $O_2$ and $H_2$

## Part 4: Limiting Reagents and Yield

36. **The reactant that is completely consumed first in a chemical reaction and limits the amount of product formed is the:**
    A) Excess reagent
    B) Limiting Reagent
    C) Catalyst
    D) Inhibitor
    *Answer: B*

37. **The reactant that remains after the reaction stops is the:**
    A) Limiting reagent
    B) Excess Reagent
    C) Product

    *Answer: B*

    A) Actual Yield
    B) Theoretical Yield
