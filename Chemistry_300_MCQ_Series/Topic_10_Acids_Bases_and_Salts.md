# Topic 10: Acids, Bases & Salts (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Theories of Acids and Bases

1. **According to the Arrhenius Theory, an acid is a substance that produces ______ in water.**
   A) Hydroxyl ions ($OH^-$)
   B) Hydrogen ions ($H^+$ or $H_3O^+$)
   C) Electrons
   D) Oxygen
   *Answer: B*

2. **According to the Arrhenius Theory, a base is a substance that produces ______ in water.**
   A) Hydrogen ions ($H^+$)
   B) Hydroxyl ions ($OH^-$)
   C) Sodium ions
   D) Protons
   *Answer: B*

3. **What is a major limitation of the Arrhenius Theory?**
   A) It is too simple
   B) It only applies to aqueous (water) solutions
   C) It doesn't use math
   D) It was founded in Sweden
   *Answer: B*

4. **According to the Brønsted–Lowry Theory, an acid is a:**
   A) Proton donor ($H^+$ donor)
   B) Proton acceptor
   ) Electron pair donor
   D) Electron pair acceptor
   *Answer: A*

5. **According to the Brønsted–Lowry Theory, a base is a:**
   A) Proton donor
   B) Proton acceptor ($H^+$ acceptor)
   C) Oxygen donor
   D) Neutron acceptor
   *Answer: B*

6. **A pair of substances that differ only by a single proton ($H^+$) is called a:**
   A) Double pair
   B) Conjugate Acid-Base Pair
   C) Neutral pair
   D) Redox pair
   *Answer: B*

7. **When an acid loses a proton, it forms its:**
   A) Conjugate Base
   B) Conjugate Acid
   C) Salt
   D) Radical
   *Answer: A*

8. **When a base gains a proton, it forms its:**
   A) Conjugate Base
   B) Conjugate Acid
   C) Water
   D) Anion
   *Answer: B*

9. **Substances like water ($H_2O$) and bicarbonate ($HCO_3^-$) that can act as either an acid or a base are called:**
   A) Ambivalent
   B) Amphoteric (or Amphiprotic)
   C) Neutral
   D) Indicators
   *Answer: B*

10. **According to the Lewis Theory, an acid is an:**
    A) Electron pair donor
    B) Electron pair acceptor
    C) Proton donor
    D) Proton acceptor
    *Answer: B*

11. **According to the Lewis Theory, a base is an:**
    A) Electron pair acceptor
    B) Electron pair donor
    C) Neutron donor
    D) Oxygen acceptor
    *Answer: B*

12. **Which of the following is a Lewis Base?**
    A) $H^+$
    B) $BF_3$
    C) $NH_3$ (has a lone pair of electrons to donate)
    D) $AlCl_3$
    *Answer: C*

13. **Which of the following is a Lewis Acid?**
    A) $NH_3$
    B) $H_2O$
    C) $BF_3$ (electron deficient)
    D) $OH^-$
    *Answer: C*

14. **Which theory is the most general and includes substances that do not contain hydrogen?**
    A) Arrhenius
    B) Brønsted–Lowry
    C) Lewis
    *Answer: C*

15. **The reaction between an acid and a base is called:**
    A) Hydrogenation
    B) Neutralization
    C) Oxidation
    D) Combustion
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Strength, pH Scale, and Indicators

16. **An acid that completely ionizes (100%) in water is a:**
    A) Weak acid
    B) Strong acid (e.g., $HCl, H_2SO_4, HNO_3$)
    C) Dilute acid
    D) Organic acid
    *Answer: B*

17. **An acid that only partially ionizes in water is a:**
    A) Strong acid
    B) Weak acid (e.g., $CH_3COOH$)
    C) Concentrated acid
    D) Mineral acid
    *Answer: B*

18. **The pH scale was introduced by:**
    A) Sorenson
    B) Lewis
    C) Arrhenius
    D) Pasteur
    *Answer: A*

19. **pH is defined as the ______ of the molar concentration of $H^+$ ions.**
    A) Positive logarithm
    B) Negative logarithm ($- \log [H^+]$)
    C) Inverse
    D) Square root
    *Answer: B*

20. **A solution with a pH of 7 at 25°C is:**
    A) Acidic
    B) Basic
    C) Neutral
    D) Toxic
    *Answer: C*

21. **A solution with a pH less than 7 is:**
    A) Acidic
    B) Basic
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: A*

22. **A solution with a pH greater than 7 is:**
    A) Acidic
    B) Basic (or Alkaline)
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: B*

23. **If a solution has a $[H^+] = 10^{-5} M$, what is its pH?**
    A) 5
    B) -5
    C) 10
    D) 7
    *Answer: A*

24. **If a solution has a pH of 3, its $[H^+]$ concentration is:**
    A) 3 M
    B) $10^{-3} M$
    C) $10^3 M$
    D) 0.0001 M
    *Answer: B*

25. **pOH is defined as:**
    A) $- \log [OH^-]$
    B) $\log [H^+]$
    C) $14 + pH$
    D) $[OH] / [H]$
    *Answer: A*

26. **At 25°C, $pH + pOH$ always equals:**
    A) 7
    B) 14
    C) 1
    D) 0
    *Answer: B*

27. **A substance that changes color in response to a change in pH is a/an:**
    A) Catalyst
    B) Indicator
    C) Buffer
    D) Salt
    *Answer: B*

28. **Litmus paper turns ______ in an acid and ______ in a base.**
    A) Blue; Red
    B) Red; Blue
    C) Pink; Yellow
    D) Clear; Blue
    *Answer: B*

29. **Phenolphthalein is ______ in acidic solutions and ______ in basic solutions.**
    A) Pink; Colorless
    B) Colorless; Pink (Magenta)
    C) Blue; Red
    D) Yellow; Red
    *Answer: B*

30. **Methyl Orange turns ______ in acid and ______ in base.**
    A) Yellow; Red
    B) Red; Yellow
    C) Blue; Green
    *Answer: B*

31. **'Universal Indicator' is a mixture of several indicators that shows color across a:**
    A) Specific pH (e.g., 7 only)
    B) Wide range of pH (0 to 14)
    C) High temperature only
    *Answer: B*

32. **A solution of pH 2 is ______ times more acidic than a solution of pH 3.**
    A) 1
    B) 10
    C) 100
    D) 2
    *Answer: B (The scale is logarithmic).*

33. **A solution of pH 1 is ______ times more acidic than a solution of pH 3.**
    A) 2
    B) 20
    C) 100 ($10^2$)
    D) 10
    *Answer: C*

34. **The acid dissociation constant ($K_a$) is a measure of the ______ of an acid.**
    A) Concentration
    B) Strength (Stronger acid = larger $K_a$)
    C) Color
    D) Volume
    *Answer: B*

35. **The base dissociation constant ($K_b$) is a measure of the strength of a base.**
    A) True
    B) False
    *Answer: A*

## Part 3: Salts and Their Properties

36. **A compound formed from the positive ion of a base and the negative ion of an acid is a:**
    A) Salt
    B) Sugar
    C) Polymer
    D) Oxide
    *Answer: A*

37. **Common table salt is:**
    A) $KCl$
    B) $NaCl$ (Sodium Chloride)
    C) $MgCl_2$
    D) $CaCO_3$
    *Answer: B*

38. **The reaction of an acid and a base usually produces a salt and:**
    A) Oxygen
    B) Hydrogen
    C) Water ($H_2O$)
    D) Carbon Dioxide
    *Answer: C*

39. **A salt formed from a strong acid and a strong base (like $NaCl$) will generally produce a/an ______ solution.**
    A) Acidic
    B) Basic
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: C*

40. **A salt formed from a strong acid and a weak base (like $NH_4Cl$) will produce a/an ______ solution.**
    A) Acidic
    B) Basic
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: A*

41. **A salt formed from a weak acid and a strong base (like $CH_3COONa$) will produce a/an ______ solution.**
    A) Acidic
    B) Basic
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: B*

42. **The reaction of salt with water to produce acidity or alkalinity is called salt:**
    A) Hydration
    B) Hydrolysis
    C) Oxidation
    D) Fermentation
    *Answer: B*

43. **Salts containing $H^+$ that can still react with bases are called:**
    A) Normal salts
    B) Acidic salts (e.g., $NaHCO_3$)
    C) Basic salts
    D) Mixed salts
    *Answer: B*

44. **Salts containing $OH^-$ that can still react with acids are called:**
    A) Normal salts
    B) Basic salts (e.g., $Pb(OH)Cl$)
    C) Acidic salts
    D) Neutral salts
    *Answer: B*

45. **Baking soda is chemically:**
    A) Sodium Carbonate ($Na_2CO_3$)
    B) Sodium Bicarbonate ($NaHCO_3$)
    C) Sodium Chloride
    D) Sodium Hydroxide
    *Answer: B*

46. **Washing soda is chemically:**
    A) $NaOH$
    B) $Na_2CO_3 \cdot 10H_2O$
    C) $NaHCO_3$
    D) $CaCl_2$
    *Answer: B*

47. **Bleaching powder is chemically:**
    A) $CaO$
    B) $Ca(OCl)Cl$ (Calcium Hypochlorite)
    C) $NaOH$
    D) $NaCl$
    *Answer: B*

48. **Plaster of Paris is formed by heating ______ .**
    A) Lime
    B) Gypsum ($CaSO_4 \cdot 2H_2O$)
    C) Sand
    D) Coal
    *Answer: B*

49. **Epsom salt is chemically:**
    A) $MgSO_4 \cdot 7H_2O$
    B) $NaCl$
    C) $KCl$
    D) $AgNO_3$
    *Answer: A*

50. **Blue vitriol is chemically:**
    A) $FeSO_4 \cdot 7H_2O$
    B) $CuSO_4 \cdot 5H_2O$
    C) $ZnSO_4 \cdot 7H_2O$
    D) $AgNO_3$
    *Answer: B*

## Part 4: Acids and Bases in Daily Life

    A) Citric acid

    *Answer: B*

    A) Acetic acid
