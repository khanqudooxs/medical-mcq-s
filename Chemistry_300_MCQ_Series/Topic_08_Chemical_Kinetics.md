# Topic 08: Chemical Kinetics (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Reaction Rates and Measurement

1. **What does 'Chemical Kinetics' study?**
   A) The energy of reactants
   B) The rate (speed) of chemical reactions and the mechanisms by which they occur
   C) The physical state of chemicals
   D) The mass of the nucleus
   *Answer: B*

2. **The 'Rate of Reaction' is defined as the change in ______ of a reactant or product per unit time.**
   A) Mass
   B) Concentration
   C) Volume
   D) Color
   *Answer: B*

3. **What are the common units for the rate of a chemical reaction?**
   A) $mol/L$
   B) $mol/L \cdot s$ (Molarity per second)
   C) $g/L$
   D) $s/mol$
   *Answer: B*

4. **As a reaction progresses, the concentration of the reactants ______ and the concentration of the products ______ .**
   A) Increases; Decreases
   B) Decreases; Increases
   C) Stays same; Increases
   D) Increases; Stays same
   *Answer: B*

5. **The 'Instantaneous Rate' of a reaction is the rate at:**
   A) The beginning of the reaction
   B) A specific moment in time
   C) The very end of the reaction
   D) Over the entire time interval
   *Answer: B*

6. **The average rate is calculated over a ______ .**
   A) Single point
   B) Specific time interval
   C) Second
   D) Year
   *Answer: B*

7. **For a reaction $A \to B$, the rate can be expressed as:**
   A) $-\Delta [A] / \Delta t$
   B) $+\Delta [B] / \Delta t$
   C) Both A and B
   D) $\Delta [A] \times \Delta [B]$
   *Answer: C*

8. **Why is the minus sign used when expressing the rate in terms of reactants?**
   A) Because the rate is negative
   B) To make the rate value positive (since concentration decreases)
   C) It is a mathematical error
   D) Reactants are negative in charge
   *Answer: B*

9. **The 'Rate Law' expression relates the rate of reaction to the:**
   A) Temperature
   B) Concentrations of the reactants
   C) Volume of the container
   D) Pressure of the atmosphere
   *Answer: B*

10. **A typical rate law for $A + B \to C$ is $Rate = k [A]^x [B]^y$. Here $k$ is the:**
    A) Equilibrium constant
    B) Specific Rate Constant (Velocity Constant)
    C) Gas constant
    D) Boltzmann constant
    *Answer: B*

11. **The 'Order of Reaction' is the sum of the ______ of the concentration terms in the rate law.**
    A) Coefficients
    B) Powers (Exponents)
    C) Atomic masses
    D) Neutrons
    *Answer: B*

12. **If $Rate = k [A]^1 [B]^1$, the overall order of the reaction is:**
    A) 1
    B) 2 (Second-order)
    C) 0
    D) 1/2
    *Answer: B*

13. **Can the order of a reaction be zero or a fraction?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (only whole numbers)
    *Answer: A*

14. **In a 'Zero-Order' reaction, the rate is ______ of the concentration of the reactants.**
    A) Directly proportional
    B) Independent
    C) Inversely proportional
    D) Squarely dependent
    *Answer: B*

15. **The units of the rate constant ($k$) depend on moving to which order?**
    A) It's always $s^{-1}$
    B) It depends on the overall order of the reaction
    C) It's always $L/mol \cdot s$
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Factors Affecting Reaction Rates

16. **How does increasing the concentration of reactants generally affect the reaction rate?**
    A) It decreases the rate
    B) It increases the rate (due to more frequent collisions)
    C) It has no effect
    D) It stops the reaction
    *Answer: B*

17. **How does increasing the temperature affect the rate of almost all chemical reactions?**
    A) It decreases the rate
    B) It significantly increases the rate
    C) It only affects endothermic reactions
    D) It makes the reaction slower
    *Answer: B*

18. **A general rule of thumb is that the rate of many reactions ______ for every 10Â°C rise in temperature.**
    A) Halves
    B) Doubles
    C) Stays same
    D) Triples
    *Answer: B*

19. **How does surface area affect the reaction rate for solids?**
    A) Smaller surface area = Faster rate
    B) Larger surface area (finer powder) = Faster rate
    C) Surface area has no effect
    *Answer: B*

20. **A substance that increases the rate of a reaction without being permanently consumed is a:**
    A) Reactant
    B) Catalyst
    C) Product
    ) Inhibitor
    *Answer: B*

21. **A 'Positive Catalyst' works by providing an alternative reaction path with a:**
    A) Higher Activation Energy
    B) Lower Activation Energy
    C) Higher temperature
    D) Lower pressure
    *Answer: B*

22. **A substance that decreases the rate of a reaction is called a/an:**
    A) Catalyst
    B) Positive catalyst
    ) Inhibitor or Negative Catalyst
    D) Promoter
    *Answer: C*

23. **Can a catalyst change the equilibrium constant or the $\Delta H$ of a reaction?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (It only helps reach equilibrium faster)
    *Answer: B*

24. **Enzymes are ______ catalysts.**
    A) Inorganic
    B) Biological (Protein-based)
    C) Solid
    D) Poisonous
    *Answer: B*

25. **Heterogeneous catalysis occurs when the catalyst and the reactants are in:**
    A) The same phase
    B) Different phases (e.g., solid catalyst and gas reactants)
    C) Liquid only
    D) Space
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Collision Theory and Activation Energy

26. **According to 'Collision Theory', for a reaction to occur, particles must:**
    A) Just be near each other
    B) Collide with sufficient energy and proper orientation
    C) Be in a vacuum
    D) Be of the same size
    *Answer: B*

27. **The minimum energy required for a collision to result in a reaction is the:**
    A) Kinetic Energy
    B) Activation Energy ($E_a$)
    C) Potential Energy
    D) Thermal Energy
    *Answer: B*

28. **An unstable, high-energy arrangement of atoms formed at the peak of the activation energy barrier is the:**
    A) Reactant
    B) Product
    C) Activated Complex (or Transition State)
    D) Catalyst
    *Answer: C*

29. **Reactions with high activation energy tend to be ______ at room temperature.**
    A) Very fast
    B) Slow
    C) Explosive
    D) Liquid
    *Answer: B*

30. **The 'Arrhenius Equation' relates the rate constant ($k$) to temperature ($T$) and activation energy ($E_a$). It is written as:**
    A) $k = A e^{-E_a/RT}$
    B) $k = A + E_a/RT$
    C) $PV = nRT$
    D) $k = [A][B]$
   *Answer: A*

31. **In the Arrhenius equation, 'A' is called the:**
    A) Acid factor
    B) Frequency factor (or Pre-exponential factor)
    C) Atomic number
    D) Activation constant
    *Answer: B*

32. **A 'Photochemical Reaction' is a reaction that is initiated by:**
    A) Heat
    B) Light (Photons)
    C) Sound
    D) Electricity
    *Answer: B*

33. **An example of a photochemical reaction is:**
    A) Digestion
    B) Photosynthesis
    C) Rusting of iron
    D) Melting of ice
    *Answer: B*

34. **'Half-life' ($t_{1/2}$) of a reaction is the time required for:**
    A) The reaction to finish
    B) The concentration of a reactant to decrease to half of its initial value
    C) Half of the product to form
    D) The temperature to halve
    *Answer: B*

35. **The half-life of a 'First-Order' reaction is ______ of the initial concentration.**
    A) Directly proportional
    B) Independent
    C) Inversely proportional
    *Answer: B ($t_{1/2} = 0.693 / k$)*

## Part 4: Reaction Mechanisms

36. **The sequence of elementary steps by which a reaction occurs is the:**
    A) Reaction Rate
    B) Reaction Mechanism
    C) Stoichiometry
    D) Product yield

37. **A reaction that occurs in a single step is called a/an:**
    A) Complex reaction
    B) Elementary Reaction
    C) Balanced reaction
    D) Dual reaction
    *Answer: B*

38. **The 'Molecularity' of a reaction is the number of ______ involved in an elementary step.**
    A) Protons
    B) Reacting species (atoms, ions, or molecules)
    C) Final products
    D) Catalysts
    *Answer: B*

39. **A 'Unimolecular' reaction involves ______ molecule(s).**
    A) 1
    B) 2

40. **A 'Bimolecular' reaction involves ______ molecule(s).**
    A) 1
    B) 2
    C) 3
    D) 4
    *Answer: B*

41. **The slowest step in a multi-step reaction mechanism is the:**
    A) Fast step
    B) Rate-Determining Step (RDS)
    C) First step
    D) Last step
    *Answer: B*

42. **A chemical species formed in one step and consumed in a later step of a mechanism is a/an:**
    A) Reactant
    B) Intermediate
    C) Catalyst
    D) Side product

43. **Is the 'Order' of a reaction always the same as its 'Molecularity'?**

    B) No (Order is experimental; molecularity is for elementary steps)

44. **Which order reaction has a constant rate regardless of concentration?**

    B) Second-order

45. **Radioactive decay usually follows which order kinetics?**

46. **What happens to the rate constant ($k$) if the activation energy is lowered?**
