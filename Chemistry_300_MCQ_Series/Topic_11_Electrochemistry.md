# Topic 11: Electrochemistry (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Oxidation and Reduction (Redox)

1. **What is 'Electrochemistry' the study of?**
   A) The arrangement of planets
   B) The relationship between chemical energy and electrical energy
   C) The speed of sound in chemicals
   D) The mass of the electron
   *Answer: B*

2. **According to the classical concept, 'Oxidation' is the addition of ______ or removal of ______ .**
   A) Hydrogen; Oxygen
   B) Oxygen; Hydrogen
   C) Electrons; Protons
   D) Water; Oil
   *Answer: B*

3. **According to the modern Electronic Concept, 'Oxidation' is the ______ of electrons.**
   A) Gain
   B) Loss
   C) Sharing
   D) Balancing
   *Answer: B (LEO - Loss of Electrons is Oxidation).*

4. **According to the modern Electronic Concept, 'Reduction' is the ______ of electrons.**
   A) Loss
   B) Gain
   C) Sharing
   D) Multiplication
   *Answer: B (GER - Gain of Electrons is Reduction).*

5. **A reaction in which both oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously is called a/an:**
   A) Acid-Base reaction
   B) Redox Reaction
   C) Precipitation reaction
   D) Displacement reaction
   *Answer: B*

6. **The 'Oxidation Number' (or Oxidation State) of an atom is the ______ charge it would have in a molecule.**
   A) Real
   B) Apparent/Assigned
   C) Total
   D) Zero always
   *Answer: B*

7. **The oxidation state of an element in its free or uncombined state (e.g., $Na, O_2, H_2$) is always:**
   A) +1
   B) -1
   C) Zero
   D) +2
   *Answer: C*

8. **In simple monoatomic ions, the oxidation state is equal to the ______ of the ion.**
   A) Mass
   B) Charge (e.g., $Na^+$ is +1, $Cl^-$ is -1)
   C) Atomic number
   D) Valency only
   *Answer: B*

9. **The sum of the oxidation numbers of all atoms in a neutral molecule is:**
   A) 1
   B) Zero
   C) -1
   D) Variable
   *Answer: B*

10. **The oxidation state of Oxygen in most of its compounds (like $H_2O$) is:**
    A) -1
    B) -2
    C) +1
    D) +2
    *Answer: B*

11. **In 'Peroxides' (like $H_2O_2$), the oxidation state of Oxygen is:**
    A) -2
    B) -1
    C) 0
    D) +1
    *Answer: B*

12. **The oxidation state of Hydrogen in most of its compounds (with non-metals) is:**
    A) -1
    B) +1
    C) 0
    D) +2
    *Answer: B*

13. **In 'Metal Hydrides' (like $NaH$), the oxidation state of Hydrogen is:**
    A) +1
    B) -1
    C) 0
    D) +2
    *Answer: B*

14. **An 'Oxidizing Agent' (Oxidant) is a substance that ______ electrons from another substance.**
    A) Gives
    B) Takes/Gains (it gets reduced itself)
    C) Shares
    D) Destroys
    *Answer: B*

15. **A 'Reducing Agent' (Reductant) is a substance that ______ electrons to another substance.**
    A) Takes
    B) Gives/Loses (it gets oxidized itself)
    C) Steals
    D) Absorbs
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Electrochemical Cells

16. **A device that converts chemical energy into electrical energy (or vice versa) is a/an:**
    A) Thermometer
    B) Electrochemical Cell
    C) Barometer
    D) Microscope
    *Answer: B*

17. **Which type of cell uses a chemical reaction to produce electricity spontaneously?**
    A) Electrolytic Cell
    B) Galvanic (or Voltaic) Cell
    C) Thermal Cell
    D) Solar Cell
    *Answer: B*

18. **Which type of cell uses electricity to bring about a non-spontaneous chemical reaction?**
    A) Galvanic Cell
    B) Electrolytic Cell
    C) Dry Cell
    D) Battery
    *Answer: B*

19. **The electrode where oxidation occurs is called the:**
    A) Cathode
    B) Anode
    C) Electrolyte
    D) Salt bridge
    *Answer: B (An Ox - Anode Oxidation).*

20. **The electrode where reduction occurs is called the:**
    A) Anode
    B) Cathode
    C) Voltmeter
    D) Positive terminal only
    *Answer: B (Red Cat - Reduction Cathode).*

21. **In a Galvanic Cell, the 'Anode' is assigned a ______ charge.**
    A) Positive
    B) Negative (-)
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: B*

22. **In a Galvanic Cell, the 'Cathode' is assigned a ______ charge.**
    A) Negative
    B) Positive (+)
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: B*

23. **In an Electrolytic Cell, the 'Anode' is connected to the ______ terminal of the battery.**
    A) Negative
    B) Positive
    *Answer: B*

24. **In an Electrolytic Cell, the 'Cathode' is connected to the ______ terminal.**
    A) Positive
    B) Negative
    *Answer: B*

25. **The component that maintains electrical neutrality and completes the circuit in a Galvanic Cell is the:**
    A) Battery
    B) Salt Bridge
    C) Wire
    D) Voltmeter
    *Answer: B*

26. **What is a 'Salt Bridge' usually filled with?**
    A) Water only
    B) An inert electrolyte gel (e.g., $KCl$ in agar-agar)
    C) Acid
    D) Sugar
    *Answer: B*

27. **Electrolytes are substances that conduct electricity in the ______ or ______ state.**
    A) Solid; Gaseous
    B) Molten; Aqueous
    C) Cold; Dry
    D) Vacuum; Space
    *Answer: B*

28. **Strong electrolytes (like $NaCl$ or $HCl$) dissociate ______ in water.**
    A) Partially
    B) Completely
    C) Not at all
    *Answer: B*

29. **Weak electrolytes (like $CH_3COOH$) dissociate ______ in water.**
    A) Completely
    B) Partially
    C) Never
    *Answer: B*

30. **Non-electrolytes (like Sugar or Alcohol) ______ .**
    A) Conduct electricity well
    B) Do not form ions and do not conduct electricity
    C) Are metals
    D) Are strong acids
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Electrolysis and Faraday’s Laws

31. **The process of chemical decomposition produced by passing an electric current through an electrolyte is:**
    A) Photosynthesis
    B) Electrolysis
    C) Smelting
    D) Ionization
    *Answer: B*

32. **Faraday’s First Law of Electrolysis states that the mass of substance deposited at an electrode is ______ proportional to the quantity of electricity passed.**
    A) Inversely
    B) Directly ($W \propto Q$ or $W = ZIt$)
    C) Logarithmically
    D) Not
    *Answer: B*

33. **The constant 'Z' in $W = ZIt$ is called the:**
    A) Impedance
    B) Electrochemical Equivalent
    C) atomic weight
    D) Faraday constant
    *Answer: B*

34. **The quantity of electricity (charge) is measured in:**
    A) Volts
    B) Coulombs (C)
    C) Amperes (A)
    D) Ohms
    *Answer: B*

35. **One 'Faraday' (F) of electricity is equal to the charge of one mole of electrons, which is approximately:**
    A) 1.0 C
    B) 96,500 Coulombs
    C) $6 \times 10^{23} C$
    D) 32,000 C
    *Answer: B*

36. **Faraday’s Second Law states that when the same amount of electricity is passed through different electrolytes, the masses deposited are proportional to their:**
    A) Density
    B) Chemical Equivalent Weights
    C) Melting points
    D) Atomic numbers
    *Answer: B*

37. **The coating of one metal on the surface of another metal using electrolysis is called:**
    A) Painting
    B) Electroplating
    C) Polishing
    D) Galvanizing
    *Answer: B*

38. **Providing a layer of Zinc on Iron to prevent rusting is called:**
    ) Silvering
    B) Galvanizing
    C) Tinning
    D) Anodizing
    *Answer: B*

39. **In electroplating, the object to be plated is always made the:**
    A) Anode
    B) Cathode
    C) Salt bridge
    D) Battery
    *Answer: B*

40. **In the purification of Copper, the impure copper is used as the ______ and pure copper as the ______ .**
    A) Cathode; Anode
    B) Anode; Cathode
    *Answer: B*

## Part 4: Batteries and Potentials

41. **Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE) is used as a reference with an assigned potential of:**
    A) 1.0 V
    B) 0.00 Volts
    C) -1.0 V
    D) 96,500 V
    *Answer: B*

42. **The potential difference between an electrode and its ions in solution is:**
    ) Electrolytic capacity
    B) Electrode Potential
    C) Ohmic drop
    D) Resistance
    *Answer: B*

    A) Periodic Table
    B) Electrochemical Series (Electromotive Series)

44. **Metals with highly negative reduction potentials are ______ reducing agents.**
    A) Strong
    B) Weak

    *Answer: A*

45. **Which metal is at the top of the electrochemical series (best reducing agent)?**
    A) Gold
    B) Lithium (Li)
    C) Copper
    D) Iron

46. **Which non-metal is at the bottom (best oxidizing agent)?**
    A) Hydrogen

    C) Oxygen
    D) Chlorine
    *Answer: B*
