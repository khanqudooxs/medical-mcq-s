# Topic 07: Thermochemistry (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Basic Energetics and Systems

1. **What is 'Thermochemistry' the study of?**
   A) The arrangement of atoms in crystals
   B) The heat energy changes that accompany chemical reactions and physical changes
   C) The speed of chemical reactions
   D) The toxicity of chemicals
   *Answer: B*

2. **The part of the universe under study is called the:**
   A) Surroundings
   B) System
   C) Universe
   D) Boundary
   *Answer: B*

3. **Everything else in the universe except the system is called the:**
   A) System
   B) Surroundings
   C) Boundary
   D) Vacuum
   *Answer: B*

4. **A system that can exchange both matter and energy with its surroundings is a/an:**
   A) Closed system
   B) Open system
   C) Isolated system
   D) Adiabatic system
   *Answer: B*

5. **A system that can exchange energy but NOT matter with its surroundings is a/an:**
   A) Open system
   B) Closed system
   C) Isolated system
   D) Open vessel
   *Answer: B*

6. **A system that can exchange neither matter nor energy with its surroundings is a/an:**
   A) Open system
   B) Closed system
   C) Isolated system (e.g., a perfect thermos flask)
   D) Real system
   *Answer: C*

7. **A property that depends only on the current state of the system and not on the path taken to reach it is a:**
   A) Path function
   B) State Function
   C) Variable function
   D) Random function
   *Answer: B*

8. **Which of the following is a state function?**
   A) Work (w)
   B) Heat (q)
   C) Enthalpy (H)
   D) All of the above
   *Answer: C (q and w are path functions).*

9. **Energy can neither be created nor destroyed, but can be converted from one form to another. This is the:**
   A) Second Law of Thermodynamics
   B) First Law of Thermodynamics (Law of Conservation of Energy)
   C) Third Law of Thermodynamics
   D) Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics
   *Answer: B*

10. **The total energy of a system (Kinetic + Potential) is called its:**
    A) Enthalpy
    B) Internal Energy (E or U)
    C) Entropy
    D) Gibbis Free Energy
    *Answer: B*

11. **Internal energy ($E$) is a state function.**
    A) True
    B) False
    *Answer: A*

12. **According to the first law of thermodynamics, $\Delta E = q + w$. Here $q$ is:**
    A) Work done
    B) Heat absorbed/released
    C) Mass
    D) Gravity
    *Answer: B*

13. **Heat absorbed by the system from the surroundings is given a ______ sign.**
    A) Negative (-)
    B) Positive (+)
    C) Zero
    *Answer: B*

14. **Heat released by the system to the surroundings is given a ______ sign.**
    A) Positive (+)
    B) Negative (-)
    C) Infinity
    *Answer: B*

15. **Work done BY the system (expansion) is given a ______ sign in many chemistry conventions.**
    A) Positive
    B) Negative (because energy is lost by the system to do work)
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Enthalpy and Reaction Types

16. **'Enthalpy' (H) is defined as the heat content of a system at constant:**
    A) Temperature
    B) Pressure
    C) Volume
    D) Mass
    *Answer: B ($H = E + PV$)*

17. **$\Delta H$ represents the change in:**
    A) Internal energy
    B) Enthalpy (heat of reaction at constant pressure)
    C) Entropy
    D) Temperature
    *Answer: B*

18. **A reaction that releases heat to the surroundings is called a/an:**
    A) Endothermic reaction
    B) Exothermic reaction
    C) Isothermal reaction
    D) Nuclear reaction
    *Answer: B*

19. **In an exothermic reaction, $\Delta H$ is always:**
    A) Positive (+)
    B) Negative (-)
    C) Zero
    D) Infinite
    *Answer: B*

20. **A reaction that absorbs heat from the surroundings is called a/an:**
    A) Exothermic reaction
    B) Endothermic reaction
    C) Adiabatic reaction
    D) Cooling reaction
    *Answer: B*

21. **In an endothermic reaction, $\Delta H$ is always:**
    A) Negative (-)
    B) Positive (+)
    C) Zero
    *Answer: B*

22. **Which of the following is an exothermic process?**
    A) Melting of ice
    B) Combustion (burning) of fuel
    C) Photosynthesis
    D) Evaporation of water
    *Answer: B*

23. **Which of the following is an endothermic process?**
    A) Freezing of water
    B) Melting of ice
    C) Condensation of steam
    D) Formation of water from $H_2$ and $O_2$
    *Answer: B*

24. **Photosynthesis is a/an ______ process.**
    A) Exothermic (releases energy)
    B) Endothermic (requires sunlight energy)
    *Answer: B*

25. **Respiration (the breakdown of glucose in cells) is a/an ______ process.**
    A) Endothermic
    B) Exothermic
    *Answer: B*

26. **Standard Enthalpy of Formation ($\Delta H_f^\circ$) is the enthalpy change when one mole of a compound is formed from its elements in their:**
    A) Liquid state
    B) Gaseous state
    C) Standard states (at 298 K, 1 atm)
    D) Mixed states
    *Answer: C*

27. **By convention, the standard enthalpy of formation of a pure element in its most stable form (like $O_2$ gas) is:**
    A) 100 kJ/mol
    B) Zero
    C) -100 kJ/mol
    D) 1 kJ/mol
    *Answer: B*

28. **The heat evolved or absorbed when one mole of a substance is completely burned in excess oxygen is:**
    A) Heat of formation
    B) Heat of Combustion
    C) Heat of neutralization
    D) Heat of solution
    *Answer: B*

29. **The enthalpy change when one mole of $H^+$ ions from an acid reacts with one mole of $OH^-$ ions from a base is:**
    A) Heat of formation
    B) Heat of Neutralization
    C) Heat of solution
    D) Heat of fusion
    *Answer: B*

30. **Heat of neutralization for a strong acid and a strong base is approximately:**
    A) -13.7 kcal/mol (or -57.3 kJ/mol)
    B) -100 kJ/mol
    C) 0 kJ/mol
    D) +57.3 kJ/mol
    *Answer: A*

## Part 3: Calorimetry and Laws

31. **The measurement of heat changes in a chemical reaction is called:**
    A) Thermography
    B) Calorimetry
    C) Radiometry
    D) Geometry
    *Answer: B*

32. **A device used to measure the heat of reaction at constant volume is a:**
    A) Glass calorimeter
    B) Bomb Calorimeter
    C) Thermometer only
    D) Barometer
    *Answer: B*

33. **Hess's Law of Constant Heat Summation states that the total enthalpy change of a reaction is the same whether it occurs in:**
    A) One step or several steps
    B) The lab or at home
    C) Summer or winter
    D) Water or oil
    *Answer: A*

34. **Hess's Law is a direct consequence of which law?**
    A) Law of conservation of mass
    B) First Law of Thermodynamics (Energy conservation)
    C) Law of gravity
    D) Avogadro's Law
    *Answer: B*

35. **Lattice Energy is the enthalpy change when one mole of an ionic crystal is formed from its:**
    ) Solid elements
    B) Gaseous ions
    C) Liquid drops
    D) Atoms
    *Answer: B*

36. **The cycle used to calculate lattice energy from other thermodynamic data is the:**
    A) Carbon cycle
    B) Born-Haber Cycle
    C) Nitrogen cycle
    D) Krebs cycle
    *Answer: B*

37. **Standard conditions for thermochemical measurements are usually:**
    A) 0°C and 1 atm
    B) 25°C (298.15 K) and 1 atm (101.325 kPa)
    C) 100°C and 10 atm
    D) Room temperature (variable)
    *Answer: B*

38. **'Bond Enthalpy' is the energy required to ______ one mole of a particular type of bond.**
    A) Form
    B) Break
    C) Stretch
    D) Freeze
    *Answer: B*

39. **Breaking a chemical bond is always an ______ process.**
    A) Exothermic
    B) Endothermic (requires energy)
    *Answer: B*

40. **Forming a chemical bond is always an ______ process.**
    A) Endothermic
    B) Exothermic (releases energy)
    *Answer: B*

## Part 4: Entropy and Spontaneity

41. **'Entropy' (S) is a measure of the ______ of a system.**
    A) Heat
    B) Disorder or Randomness
    C) Speed
    D) Mass
    *Answer: B*

42. **According to the Second Law of Thermodynamics, the total entropy of an isolated system ______ over time.**
    A) Decreases
    B) Increases
    C) Remains constant
    D) Becomes zero
    *Answer: B*

43. **Which state of matter has the highest entropy?**
    A) Solid
    B) Liquid
    C) Gas
    D) Crystal
    *Answer: C*

44. **Which state of matter has the lowest entropy?**
    A) Gas
    B) Liquid
    C) Solid
    *Answer: C*

45. **The Third Law of Thermodynamics states that the entropy of a perfect crystal at 0 K is:**
    A) Maximum
    B) Zero
    C) 100
    D) Negative
    *Answer: B*

46. **A reaction that occurs by itself without continuous external help is a:**
    A) Non-spontaneous reaction
    B) Spontaneous reaction
    C) Forced reaction
    D) Slow reaction
    *Answer: B*

47. **'Gibbs Free Energy' ($G$) is used to predict the ______ of a reaction.**
    A) Speed
    B) Spontaneity (Direction of change)
    C) Color
    D) Cost
    *Answer: B*

48. **A spontaneous reaction always has a ______ change in Gibbs Free Energy ($\Delta G$).**
    A) Positive
    B) Negative (-)
    C) Zero
    *Answer: B*

49. **If $\Delta G$ is zero, the system is at:**
    A) Constant motion
    B) Equilibrium
    C) Its beginning
    D) Its end
    *Answer: B*

50. **The formula for Gibbs Free Energy change is:**
    A) $\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$
    B) $\Delta G = \Delta H + T\Delta S$
    C) $\Delta G = \Delta H / T\Delta S$
    D) $\Delta G = \Delta E + PV$
    *Answer: A*

51. **Units of Enthalpy ($\Delta H$) are usually:**
    A) Joules (J) or kiloJoules (kJ)
    B) Degrees Celsius
    C) Liters
    D) Moles
    *Answer: A*

52. **A thermos flask is an example of an ______ system.**
    A) Open
    B) Closed
    C) Isolated (nearly)
    *Answer: C*

53. **Heat capacity ($C$) is the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of a substance by:**
    A) 1°C
    B) 100°C
    C) 10°C
    D) 50°F
    *Answer: A*

54. **Specific heat capacity ($c$) is the heat needed to raise the temperature of ______ of a substance by 1°C.**
    A) 1 gram
    B) 1 kg
    C) 1 mole
    D) 1 liter
    *Answer: A*

55. **Water has a ______ specific heat capacity.**
    A) Very low

56. **Molar heat capacity ($C_m$) is for ______ of substance.**
