# Topic 21: Nursing & Medical Application of Chemistry (Bonus 100 MCQs)
## Part 1: IV Fluids and Electrolytes

1. **Which solution is considered 'Normal Saline' in a clinical setting?**
   A) 0.9% $NaCl$ (Isotonic)
   B) 5% $NaCl$
   C) 0.45% $NaCl$ (Hypotonic)
   D) 10% Glucose
   *Answer: A*

2. **An 'Isotonic' solution has the same osmotic pressure as:**
   A) Pure water
   B) Human Blood/Plasma
   C) Sea water
   D) Vinegar
   *Answer: B*

3. **What is the purpose of giving 'D5W' (5% Dextrose in Water)?**
   A) To provide protein
   B) To provide calories (glucose) and free water to the body
   C) To increase blood thickness
   D) To lower blood sugar
   *Answer: B*

4. **Which ion is the major extracellular cation responsible for fluid balance?**
   A) Potassium ($K^+$)
   B) Sodium ($Na^+$)
   C) Calcium ($Ca^{2+}$)
   D) Magnesium
   *Answer: B*

5. **Which ion is the major intracellular cation?**
   A) Sodium
   B) Potassium ($K^+$)
   C) Chloride
   D) Bicarbonate
   *Answer: B*

6. **The medical term for high sodium levels in the blood is:**
   A) Hypokalemia
   B) Hypernatremia
   C) Hyponatremia
   D) Hypercalcemia
   *Answer: B*

7. **The medical term for low potassium levels in the blood is:**
   A) Hyperkalemia
   B) Hypokalemia
   C) Hyponatremia
   D) Anemia
   *Answer: B*

8. **Ringer's Lactate (RL) is used in hospitals primarily for:**
   A) Treating high blood sugar
   B) Fluid resuscitation after trauma or surgery
   C) Nutrition only
   D) Killing bacteria
   *Answer: B*

9. **Why is Bicarbonate ($HCO_3^-$) important in the blood?**
   A) It carries oxygen
   B) It acts as a buffer to maintain blood pH
   C) It helps in blood clotting
   D) It gives blood its color
   *Answer: B*

10. **A solution of 0.45% Sodium Chloride is described as:**
    A) Isotonic
    B) Hypotonic
    C) Hypertonic
    *Answer: B*

11. **A solution of 3% or 5% $NaCl$ used in brain swelling is:**
    A) Hypotonic
    B) Hypertonic
    C) Isotonic
    *Answer: B*

12. **What happens to cells if they are placed in a highly 'Hypertonic' solution?**
    ) They swell and burst
    B) They shrink (dehydrate)
    C) No change
    *Answer: B*

13. **'Oral Rehydration Salt' (ORS) contains a mixture of:**
    A) Sugar only
    B) Salts (NaCl, KCl, Citrate) and Glucose
    C) Vitamin C
    D) Protein powder
    *Answer: B (Glucose helps in the absorption of Sodium).*

14. **Calcium is essential for which physiological process?**
    A) Bone mineralization
    B) Muscle contraction
    C) Blood clotting
    D) All of the above
    *Answer: D*

15. **Magnesium sulfate ($MgSO_4$) is often used in obstetrics to treat:**
    A) Fever
    B) Preeclampsia/Eclampsia (to prevent seizures)
    C) Hunger
    D) Cough
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Body pH and Gases

16. **The normal pH range of human arterial blood is:**
    A) 6.8 to 7.0
    B) 7.35 to 7.45
    C) 7.0 to 8.0
    D) 1.0 to 3.0
    *Answer: B*

17. **If the blood pH drops below 7.35, the condition is called:**
    A) Alkalosis
    B) Acidosis
    C) Equilibrium
    D) Normal
    *Answer: B*

18. **If the blood pH rises above 7.45, the condition is:**
    A) Acidosis
    B) Alkalosis
    C) Homeostasis
    *Answer: B*

19. **Respiratory Acidosis is often caused by:**
    A) Hyperventilation (breathing too fast)
    B) Hypoventilation (retaining too much $CO_2$)
    C) Eating too much sugar
    *Answer: B*

20. **Respiratory Alkalosis can be caused by:**
    A) Holding breath
    B) Hyperventilation (losing too much $CO_2$, e.g., in anxiety)
    C) Diarrhea
    *Answer: B*

21. **Metabolic Acidosis can occur in uncontrolled diabetes when the body produces:**
    A) Sugar
    B) Ketone bodies (Ketoacidosis)
    C) Alcohol
    D) Salt
    *Answer: B*

22. **The main gas involved in regulating blood pH by the lungs is:**
    A) Oxygen
    B) Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$)
    C) Nitrogen
    *Answer: B*

23. **The 'Bicarbonate Buffer System' involves the equilibrium between:**
    A) $H_2SO_4$ and $SO_4$
    B) $CO_2, H_2CO_3$, and $HCO_3^-$
    C) $HCl$ and $NaCl$
    *Answer: B*

24. **'Oxygen Saturation' ($SpO_2$) measures the percentage of ______ saturated with oxygen.**
    A) Plasma
    B) Hemoglobin
    C) White blood cells
    D) Platelets
    *Answer: B*

25. **Pulse Oximetry relies on the different light absorption properties of:**
    A) Water and Fat
    B) Oxygenated and Deoxygenated hemoglobin
    C) Skin and Bone
    *Answer: B*

34. **'Steroids' are used in medicine to reduce:**

35. **The active ingredient in Tylenol is:**

    A) Sugar
    B) Cholesterol and Triglycerides

38. **Bilirubin is a waste product of ______ breakdown; high levels cause Jaundice.**
    A) Bone
    B) Red Blood Cell (Hemoglobin)
    C) Fat
    D) Muscle
    *Answer: B*

39. **Creatinine and Urea levels in the blood are used to assess the function of the:**
    A) Heart
    B) Kidneys
    C) Lungs
    D) Brain

40. **Enzymes like 'Troponin' or 'CK-MB' are measured to diagnose:**
    A) Broken bones
    B) Heart attack (Myocardial Infarction)
    C) Pregnancy
    D) Hair loss
    *Answer: B*

41. **Standard tests for syphilis or HIV often detect ______ produced by the body.**
    A) Antigens or Antibodies
    B) Glucose
    C) Salt
    D) Carbon
    *Answer: A*

42. **Glycated Hemoglobin (HbA1c) reflects the average blood sugar over the past:**
    A) 1 hour

43. **A 'Urine Dipstick' test can detect which of the following?**
    A) Glucose (Sugar)
    B) Protein (Albumin)

44. **Specific Gravity of urine is a measure of its:**
