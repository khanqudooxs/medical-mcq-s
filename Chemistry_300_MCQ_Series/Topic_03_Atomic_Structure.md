# Topic 03: Atomic Structure (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Evolution of Atomic Theory

1. **Who is often credited with proposing the first scientific atomic theory in 1803?**
   A) Democritus
   B) John Dalton
   C) J.J. Thomson
   D) Ernest Rutherford
   *Answer: B*

2. **According to Dalton's Atomic Theory, atoms are:**
   A) Divisible into smaller parts
   B) Indivisible and indestructible
   C) Composed of light
   D) Only found in liquids
   *Answer: B (Later proven wrong by the discovery of subatomic particles).*

3. **Which scientist discovered the 'Electron' using the cathode ray tube experiment?**
   A) Robert Millikan
   B) J.J. Thomson
   C) James Chadwick
   D) Niels Bohr
   *Answer: B*

4. **The 'Plum Pudding Model' of the atom was proposed by:**
   A) Ernest Rutherford
   B) J.J. Thomson
   C) John Dalton
   D) Erwin Schrödinger
   *Answer: B*

5. **Who performed the famous 'Gold Foil' experiment?**
   A) Niels Bohr
   B) Ernest Rutherford
   C) Max Planck
   D) Werner Heisenberg
   *Answer: B*

6. **The Gold Foil experiment led to the discovery of the:**
   A) Electron
   B) Neutron
   C) Atomic Nucleus
   D) Orbit
   *Answer: C*

7. **Rutherford concluded that most of the atom is:**
   A) Solid
   B) Empty space
   C) Filled with protons
   D) Liquid
   *Answer: B*

8. **Which subatomic particle has a positive charge?**
   A) Electron
   B) Neutron
   C) Proton
   D) Photon
   *Answer: C*

9. **Which subatomic particle has no charge (neutral)?**
   A) Proton
   B) Electron
   C) Neutron
   D) Positron
   *Answer: C*

10. **The discovery of the 'Neutron' in 1932 is credited to:**
    A) Ernest Rutherford
    B) James Chadwick
    C) Marie Curie
    D) Niels Bohr
    *Answer: B*

11. **Which subatomic particle has the smallest mass?**
    A) Proton
    B) Neutron
    C) Electron
    D) Helium nucleus
    *Answer: C (Approximately 1/1836th of a proton).*

12. **The mass of an atom is concentrated primarily in the:**
    A) Electron cloud
    B) Nucleus
    C) Orbits
    D) Empty space
    *Answer: B*

13. **Protons and Neutrons are collectively referred to as:**
    A) Leptons
    B) Nucleons
    C) Isomers
    D) Valencies
    *Answer: B*

14. **The fundamental negatively charged particle of the atom is the:**
    A) Proton
    B) Electron
    C) Neutron
    D) Quark
    *Answer: B*

15. **Who determined the exact charge of an electron using the 'Oil Drop' experiment?**
    A) J.J. Thomson
    B) Robert Millikan
    C) Albert Einstein
    D) Isaac Newton
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Atomic Number, Mass Number, and Isotopes

16. **The 'Atomic Number' (Z) of an atom represents the number of:**
    A) Neutrons in the nucleus
    B) Protons in the nucleus
    C) Electrons in the outer shell only
    D) Protons and Neutrons together
    *Answer: B*

17. **In a neutral atom, the number of protons is always equal to the number of:**
    A) Neutrons
    B) Electrons
    C) Positrons
    D) Alpha particles
    *Answer: B*

18. **The 'Mass Number' (A) is the sum of:**
    A) Protons and Electrons
    ) Protons and Neutrons
    C) Neutrons and Electrons
    D) Atomic number and charge
    *Answer: B*

19. **How do you calculate the number of neutrons in an atom?**
    A) $Mass Number + Atomic Number$
    B) $Mass Number - Atomic Number$
    C) $Atomic Number / 2$
    D) $Mass Number \times Atomic Number$
    *Answer: B*

20. **Atoms of the same element that have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons are called:**
    A) Isomers
    B) Isotopes
    C) Isobars
    D) Isotones
    *Answer: B*

21. **Isotopes of an element have different ______ but the same ______ .**
    A) Atomic numbers; Mass numbers
    B) Physical properties; Chemical properties
    C) Number of electrons; Number of protons
    D) Mass numbers; Atomic numbers
    *Answer: D*

22. **Hydrogen has three naturally occurring isotopes. What is the name of the isotope with 0 neutrons?**
    A) Deuterium
    B) Tritium
    C) Protium
    D) Helium
    *Answer: C*

23. **The isotope of hydrogen that contains one proton and one neutron ($^2H$) is:**
    A) Protium
    B) Deuterium
    C) Tritium
    D) Carbon-12
    *Answer: B*

24. **Which isotope of hydrogen is radioactive and contains two neutrons ($^3H$)?**
    A) Deuterium
    B) Tritium
    C) Protium
    D) Lithium
    *Answer: B*

25. **Isotopes have the same chemical properties because they have the same:**
    A) Number of neutrons
    B) Mass number
    C) Electronic configuration (number of electrons)
    D) Weighing mass
    *Answer: C*

26. **Which of the following atoms has 6 protons, 6 electrons, and 6 neutrons?**
    A) Oxygen-16
    B) Carbon-12
    C) Nitrogen-14
    D) Boron-10
    *Answer: B*

27. **What is the mass number of an atom with 11 protons and 12 neutrons?**
    A) 11
    B) 12
    C) 23 (Sodium)
    D) 1
    *Answer: C*

28. **How many neutrons are in a Chlorine-35 atom (Atomic Number 17)?**
    A) 17
    B) 18
    C) 35
    D) 52
    *Answer: B ($35 - 17 = 18$)*

29. **The average atomic mass of an element (like Chlorine 35.5) is not a whole number because it is:**
    A) A mistake in measurement
    B) Calculated based on the relative abundance of isotopes
    C) The mass of the electrons is included
    D) Always changing
    *Answer: B*

30. **Carbon-14 is an isotope used primarily for:**
    A) Nuclear fuel
    B) Radiocarbon dating of ancient organic matter
    C) Treating cancer
    D) Coloring glass
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Bohr’s Model and Quantum Basics

31. **Who proposed the model where electrons move in fixed circular orbits (energy levels) around the nucleus?**
    A) John Dalton
    B) Niels Bohr
    C) Ernest Rutherford
    D) J.J. Thomson
    *Answer: B*

32. **According to Bohr, an electron can move to a higher energy shell by:**
    A) Releasing energy
    B) Absorbing energy (Photons)
    C) Losing mass
    D) Staying still
    *Answer: B*

33. **The state where an electron is in its lowest possible energy level is the:**
    A) Excited state
    B) Ground state
    C) Liquid state
    D) Solid state
    *Answer: B*

34. **The energy shells or orbits in an atom are designated by the letters:**
    A) X, Y, Z
    B) K, L, M, N...
    C) A, B, C, D...
    D) S, P, D, F... (These are subshells)
    *Answer: B*

    B) $2n^2$
