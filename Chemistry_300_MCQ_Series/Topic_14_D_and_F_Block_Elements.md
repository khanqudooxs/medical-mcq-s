# Topic 14: d and f Block Elements (Transition Elements) (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Characteristics of Transition Metals

1. **Transition elements are located in which part of the periodic table?**
   A) s-block
   B) p-block
   C) d-block (Groups 3 to 12)
   D) f-block only
   *Answer: C*

2. **A transition element is defined as an element that forms at least one ion with a/an:**
   A) Completely filled d-subshell
   B) Partially filled d-subshell
   C) Empty d-subshell
   D) Empty s-subshell
   *Answer: B*

3. **In the d-block elements, the ______ orbitals are being progressively filled.**
   A) (n)d
   B) (n-1)d
   C) (n)p
   D) (n-2)f
   *Answer: B*

4. **Why are transition metals called 'Transition' elements?**
   A) They change color
   B) They are a transition between the highly reactive s-block metals and the p-block non-metals
   C) They are radioactive
   D) They move around the table
   *Answer: B*

5. **Most transition elements are ______ .**
   A) Gases
   B) Non-metals
   C) Hard, dense metals with high melting points
   D) Low melting solids
   *Answer: C*

6. **Transition metals often show 'Variable Oxidation States' because:**
   A) They have many protons
   B) The energy difference between (n-1)d and (n)s orbitals is small
   C) They are always liquid
   D) They have no electrons
   *Answer: B*

7. **The common oxidation state shown by almost all transition elements is:**
   A) +1
   B) +2
   C) +7
   D) 0
   *Answer: B*

8. **Which transition metal shows the highest oxidation state (+7)?**
   A) Iron (Fe)
   B) Manganese (Mn) in $KMnO_4$
   C) Chromium (Cr)
   D) Zinc (Zn)
   *Answer: B*

9. **Many transition metal ions and compounds are colored. This is often due to:**
   A) Atomic vibrations
   B) d-d transitions of electrons
   C) Their large mass
   D) Proton movement
   *Answer: B*

10. **A substance that is attracted into a magnetic field is called:**
    A) Diamagnetic
    B) Paramagnetic
    C) Ferromagnetic
    D) Non-magnetic
    *Answer: B*

11. **Paramagnetism in transition metals is due to the presence of:**
    A) Paired electrons
    B) Unpaired electrons
    C) Protons
    D) Neutrons
    *Answer: B*

12. **A substance that is weakly repelled by a magnetic field (all electrons paired) is:**
    A) Paramagnetic
    B) Diamagnetic
    C) Ferromagnetic
    *Answer: B*

13. **Substances like Iron, Cobalt, and Nickel that can be permanently magnetized are:**
    A) Paramagnetic
    B) Diamagnetic
    C) Ferromagnetic
    D) Volatile
    *Answer: C*

14. **Which transition metal is a liquid at room temperature?**
    A) Silver
    B) Mercury (Hg)
    C) Copper
    D) Gold
    *Answer: B*

15. **Transition metals and their compounds are often used as ______ in industry.**
    A) Fuels
    B) Catalysts
    C) Solvents
    D) Indicators
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: Coordination Compounds (Complexes)

31. **A central metal ion bonded to surrounding molecules or ions is a:**
    A) Simple salt
    B) Coordination Compound (or Complex)
    C) Double salt
    D) Mixed oxide
    *Answer: B*

32. **The neutral molecules or ions that donate a pair of electrons to the metal are called:**
    A) Catalysts
    B) Ligands
    C) Solvents
    D) Protons
    *Answer: B*

33. **The bond formed between a ligand and the metal ion is a/an:**
    A) Ionic bond
    B) Coordinate Covalent Bond (Dative bond)
    C) Metallic bond
    D) Hydrogen bond
    *Answer: B*

34. **The number of ligand donor atoms directly bonded to the central metal ion is the:**
    A) Atomic number
    B) Coordination Number
    C) Valency
    D) Mass number
    *Answer: B*

35. **A ligand that can donate only one pair of electrons is called:**
    A) Monodentate (e.g., $NH_3, H_2O, Cl^-$)
    B) Bidentate
    C) Polydentate
    *Answer: A*

36. **A ligand that can donate two pairs of electrons from two different atoms is:**
    A) Unidentate
    B) Bidentate (e.g., Ethylenediamine)
    C) Ambidentate
    *Answer: B*

37. **A polydentate ligand that 'grips' the metal like a claw is a/an:**
    A) Catalyst
    B) Chelating Agent
    C) Inhibitor
    *Answer: B*

38. **The shape of a complex with coordination number 6 (like $[Fe(CN)_6]^{4-}$) is usually:**
    A) Tetrahedral
    B) Octahedral
    C) Square Planar
    D) Linear
    *Answer: B*

39. **The shape of a complex with coordination number 4 can be Tetrahedral or ______ .**
    A) Octahedral
    B) Square Planar
    C) Linear
    D) Pyramidal
    *Answer: B*

40. **$[Co(NH_3)_6]^{3+}$ is an example of a/an:**
    A) Anionic complex
    B) Cationic complex
    C) Neutral complex
    *Answer: B (it has a positive charge).*

41. **$[Fe(CN)_6]^{4-}$ is an example of a/an:**
    A) Cationic complex
    B) Anionic complex (it has a negative charge)
    C) Simple salt
    *Answer: B*

42. **In naming coordination compounds, ligands are listed in which order?**
    A) Numerical
    B) Alphabetical
    C) Charge-based
    *Answer: B*

43. **The suffix used for the metal name if the complex is an anion is:**
    A) -ide
    B) -ate (e.g., Ferrate, Cuprate)
    C) -ium
    D) -ic
    *Answer: B*

44. **Werner's Theory explains that metal ions have two types of valency: Primary and ______ .**
    A) Tertiary
    B) Secondary (Coordination Number)
    C) Ionic
    D) Static
    *Answer: B*

45. **Primary valency is usually ______ and corresponds to the oxidation state.**
    A) Non-ionizable
    B) Ionizable
    *Answer: B*
