# Nursing AI Question Bank - Set 1
## Subjects: Physics & Biochemistry for Nursing (International Edition)

### Physics in Nursing
1. **(Units)** Which metric unit is standard for measuring a patient's weight for drug calculations?
   A) Pounds
   B) Kilograms (kg)
   C) Ounces
   D) Stones
   *Answer: B (kg is the international standard)*

2. **(Fluids)** When an IV bag is raised higher, what happens to the hydrostatic pressure at the needle?
   A) Decreases
   B) Increases
   C) Stays the same
   D) Becomes zero
   *Answer: B*

3. **(Mechanics)** To maintain the most stable base of support, a nurse should:
   A) Stand on one leg
   B) Keep feet together
   C) Keep feet shoulder-width apart
   D) Bend at the waist
   *Answer: C*

4. **(Heat)** A patient loses heat when they come into direct contact with a cold metal exam table. This is:
   A) Convection
   B) Radiation
   C) Conduction
   ) Evaporation
   *Answer: C*

5. **(Pressure)** Normal Blood Pressure is measured in which unit?
   A) cmH2O
   B) mmHg
   C) PSI
   D) Pascals
   *Answer: B*

6. **(Optics)** A nurse uses a penlight to check pupil constriction. This tests the reaction to:
   A) Heat
   B) Sound
   C) Light (Electromagnetic Radiation)
   D) Pressure
   *Answer: C*

7. **(Newtonâ€™s Laws)** Moving a heavy patient bed requires more force than a light one because of:
   A) Gravity
   B) Inertia/Mass
   C) Friction only
   D) Magnetism
   *Answer: B*

8. **(Fluids)** Resistance in a blood vessel increases when the radius of the vessel:
   A) Increases (Vasodilation)
   B) Decreases (Vasoconstriction)
   C) Remains constant
   D) Disappears
   *Answer: B*

9. **(Electricity)** An ECG/EKG measures what type of activity in the heart?
   A) Mechanical
   B) Chemical only
   C) Electrical
   D) Thermal
   *Answer: C*

10. **(Sound)** A stethoscope works by capturing which type of waves?
    A) Light waves
    B) Longitudinal Sound waves
    C) Transverse waves
    D) Radio waves
    *Answer: B*

### Biochemistry for Nursing
11. **(pH)** The normal pH range of human arterial blood is:
    A) 7.00 - 7.10
    B) 7.35 - 7.45
    C) 7.50 - 7.60
    D) 6.80 - 7.00
    *Answer: B*

12. **(Ions)** Which ion is the major extracellular cation?
    A) Potassium (K+)
    B) Sodium (Na+)
    /C) Calcium (Ca++)
    D) Magnesium (Mg++)
    *Answer: B*

13. **(Metabolism)** The primary molecule used by cells for energy (The energy currency) is:
    A) Glucose
    B) DNA
    C) ATP
    D) Lipid
    *Answer: C*

14. **(Enzymes)** Enzymes are primarily made of which macromolecule?
    A) Carbohydrates
    B) Lipids
    C) Proteins
    D) Nucleic Acids
    *Answer: C*

15. **(Osmosis)** A 0.9% Normal Saline solution is considered:
    A) Hypotonic
    B) Hypertonic
    C) Isotonic
    D) Acidic
    *Answer: C*

16. **(Glucose)** Which hormone acts as a chemical "key" to allow glucose into cells?
    A) Glucagon
    B) Insulin
    C) Estrogen
    D) Thyroxine
    *Answer: B*

17. **(Proteins)** The breakdown of proteins for energy results in the production of:
    A) CO2 only
    B) Urea
    C) Glucose
    D) Oxygen
    *Answer: B*

18. **(Lipids)** Cholesterol is the chemical precursor for which type of hormones?
    A) Peptide hormones
    B) Steroid hormones
    C) Amino acid hormones
    D) None
    *Answer: B*

19. **(Buffers)** The main chemical buffer system in the blood is the:
    A) Phosphate buffer
    B) Bicarbonate buffer
    C) Protein buffer
    D) Hemoglobin buffer
    *Answer: B*

20. **(DNA)** The genetic blueprint of the cell is stored in the:
    A) Cytoplasm
    B) Nucleus
    C) Mitochondria
    D) Ribosome
    *Answer: B*
