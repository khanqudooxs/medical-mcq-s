# Topic 02: Kinematics (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Distance and Displacement

1. **Which of the following is defined as the total path length traveled by an object?**
   A) Displacement
   B) Distance
   C) Velocity
   D) Acceleration
   *Answer: B*

2. **The shortest straight-line distance between the initial and final position of an object is:**
   A) Distance
   B) Displacement
   C) Speed
   D) Path
   *Answer: B*

3. **Distance is a ______ quantity, while displacement is a ______ quantity.**
   A) Vector, Scalar
   B) Scalar, Vector
   C) Vector, Vector
   D) Scalar, Scalar
   *Answer: B*

4. **Can the magnitude of displacement ever be greater than the distance traveled by an object?**
   A) Yes, always
   B) No, never
   C) Only in circular motion
   D) Only in free fall
   *Answer: B*

5. **If an object moves in a complete circle and returns to its starting point, its displacement is:**
   A) Equal to the circumference
   B) Zero
   C) Equal to the diameter
   D) Infinite
   *Answer: B*

6. **A person walks 3 km North and then 4 km East. The total distance traveled is ______ and the displacement is ______ .**
   A) 7 km, 7 km
   B) 7 km, 5 km
   C) 5 km, 7 km
   D) 12 km, 1 km
   *Answer: B (Displacement = $\sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5$)*

7. **A car travels 100 m East and then 100 m West. The distance is ______ and displacement is ______ .**
   A) 200 m, 200 m
   B) 200 m, 0 m
   C) 0 m, 200 m
   D) 100 m, 100 m
   *Answer: B*

8. **In which of the following cases is distance equal to the magnitude of displacement?**
   A) Moving in a circle
   B) Moving in a straight line in one direction
   C) Moving back and forth
   D) Moving in a parabola
   *Answer: B*

9. **The SI unit of both distance and displacement is the:**
   A) Kilometer
   B) Meter
   C) Foot
   D) Mile
   *Answer: B*

10. **If an object moves along a semi-circle of radius $r$, the distance is ______ and the displacement magnitude is ______ .**
    A) $2r, \pi r$
    B) $\pi r, 2r$
    C) $2\pi r, r$
    D) $r, 2r$
    *Answer: B*

40. **A car comes to a stop. This means its final velocity ($v_f$) is:**

## Part 4: Equations of Motion

41. **The first equation of motion is:**
    A) $S = v_i t + 1/2 at^2$
    B) $v_f = v_i + at$
    C) $2aS = v_f^2 - v_i^2$
    D) $F = ma$
    *Answer: B*

42. **The second equation of motion is:**
    A) $v_f = v_i + at$
    B) $S = v_i t + 1/2 at^2$
    C) $2aS = v_f^2 - v_i^2$
    D) $v = d/t$
    *Answer: B*

43. **The third equation of motion is:**
    A) $S = vt$
    B) $v_f = v_i + at$
    C) $2aS = v_f^2 - v_i^2$
    D) $S = 1/2 at^2$
    *Answer: C*

44. **Equations of motion are applicable only when acceleration is:**
    A) Changing
    B) Constant (Uniform)
    C) Zero
    D) Negative only
    *Answer: B*

45. **For an object falling freely from rest, how much distance does it cover in 1 second? (Take $g = 10 m/s^2$)**
    A) 10 m
    B) 5 m (Using $S = 1/2 g t^2 = 1/2 \times 10 \times 1^2$)
    C) 1 m
    D) 20 m
    *Answer: B*

46. **How much velocity does a free-falling object gain every second?**
    A) $1 m/s$

    D) $19.6 m/s$

48. **Distance traveled by an object with uniform velocity $v$ in time $t$ is:**
    A) $S = 1/2 vt$
    B) $S = vt$
    C) $S = v/t$
    D) $S = v + t$
    *Answer: B*

49. **In the equation $S = v_i t + 1/2 at^2$, if the object starts from rest, it simplifies to:**
    A) $S = at$

## Part 6: Relative Motion and Projectile Motion (Basics)

61. **Motion of one object with respect to another object is called:**
    ) Absolute motion
    B) Relative Motion
    C) Static motion
    D) Circular motion
    *Answer: B*

62. **The velocity of a car A with respect to car B moving in the same direction is:**

    B) $v_A - v_B$
    C) $v_A \times v_B$
    D) $0$
    *Answer: B*

63. **Two cars move towards each other with speeds $10 m/s$ and $20 m/s$. Their relative speed is:**
    A) $10 m/s$
    B) $30 m/s$ ($10 + 20$)
    C) $200 m/s$
    D) $15 m/s$
    *Answer: B*

64. **The path followed by a projectile is called its:**
    A) Orbit
    B) Trajectory
    C) Range

    *Answer: B*

65. **The shape of the trajectory of a projectile (ignoring air resistance) is:**
    A) Circular
    B) Parabolic
    C) Linear
    D) Hyperbolic
    *Answer: B*

66. **A projectile is an object having ______ components of motion.**
    A) Only vertical
    B) Only horizontal

    *Answer: C*

67. **In projectile motion, the horizontal component of velocity ($v_x$) is ______ (ignoring air resistance).**

68. **In projectile motion, the vertical component of velocity ($v_y$) is ______ .**
    A) Constant
    B) Changing (due to gravity)
    C) Zero
    *Answer: B*

69. **At the highest point of a projectile's path, its vertical velocity is:**
    A) Maximum
    B) Zero
    C) Constant
    *Answer: B*

70. **The horizontal distance covered by a projectile is called its:**
    A) Height
    B) Range
    C) Displacement
    D) Altitude

71. **For a projectile, the maximum range is achieved at an angle of:**
    A) 30°
    B) 45°

    D) 90°
    *Answer: B*

72. **The vertical acceleration of a projectile is always:**
    A) $0$
    B) $g$ (downwards)
    C) Changing
    *Answer: B*

73. **The horizontal acceleration of a projectile is:**
    A) $g$
    B) $0$

    A) $2\theta$

75. **The time taken by a projectile to reach the maximum height is ______ the time taken to fall back to the same level.**
