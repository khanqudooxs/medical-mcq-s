# Topic 05: Circular Motion & Gravitation (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Circular Motion Basics (Kinematics)

1. **Motion of an object along a curved path at a constant distance from a fixed point is:**
   A) Linear motion
   B) Circular Motion
   C) Rectilinear motion
   D) Vibratory motion
   *Answer: B*

2. **The angle subtended at the center of a circle by an object moving along the circumference is:**
   A) Angular Velocity
   B) Angular Displacement ($\theta$)
   C) Angular Acceleration
   D) Linear distance
   *Answer: B*

3. **The SI unit of Angular Displacement is the:**
   A) Degree
   B) Radian (rad)
   C) Revolution
   D) Meter
   *Answer: B*

4. **One complete revolution is equal to how many radians?**
   A) $\pi$
   B) $2\pi$
   C) $360\pi$
   D) $1/2 \pi$
   *Answer: B*

5. **The rate of change of angular displacement is called:**
   A) Angular speed
   B) Angular Velocity ($\omega$)
   C) Angular acceleration
   D) Tangential velocity
   *Answer: B*

6. **The SI unit of Angular Velocity is:**
   A) $rad/s$
   B) $Degree/s$
   C) $m/s$
   D) $rad/s^2$
   *Answer: A*

7. **Linear velocity ($v$) and Angular velocity ($\omega$) are related by:**
   A) $v = \omega/r$
   B) $v = r \omega$
   C) $v = \omega^2 r$
   D) $v = r^2 \omega$
   *Answer: B*

8. **The rate of change of angular velocity is called:**
   A) Torque
   B) Angular Acceleration ($\alpha$)
   C) Centripetal acceleration
   D) Momentum
   *Answer: B*

9. **The SI unit of Angular Acceleration is:**
   A) $rad/s$
   B) $rad/s^2$
   C) $m/s^2$
   D) $Hz$
   *Answer: B*

10. **The relationship between linear acceleration ($a$) and angular acceleration ($\alpha$) is:**
    A) $a = r/\alpha$
    B) $a = r \alpha$
    C) $a = \alpha^2 r$
    *Answer: B*

11. **An object moving in a circle with constant speed is in:**
    A) Uniform Circular Motion
    B) Variable motion
    C) Linear motion
    D) Rest
    *Answer: A*

12. **In uniform circular motion, is the velocity constant?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (the direction changes continuously)
    *Answer: B*

13. **The 'Time Period' ($T$) of circular motion is the time taken for:**
    A) Half a revolution
    B) One complete revolution
    C) One second

14. **'Frequency' ($f$) is the number of revolutions per:**
    A) Minute
    B) Second (Measured in Hertz, Hz)

    A) $T = f$
    B) $T = 1/f$
    C) $T = f^2$

    A) Centrifugal force
    B) Centripetal Force

    A) $ma$
    B) $mv^2 / r$
