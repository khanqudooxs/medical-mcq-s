# Topic 04: Work, Energy & Power (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Work

1. **In physics, 'Work' is done when a force ______ an object.**
   A) Touches
   B) Displaces (moves)
   C) Heats
   D) Colors
   *Answer: B*

2. **The mathematical formula for work done by a constant force is:**
   A) $W = F + d$
   B) $W = F \times d \cos\theta$
   C) $W = F/d$
   D) $W = mgh$
   *Answer: B*

3. **Work is a ______ quantity.**
   A) Vector
   B) Scalar (it has magnitude but no direction)
   C) Tensor
   *Answer: B*

4. **The SI unit of Work is the:**
   A) Newton
   B) Joule (J)
   C) Watt
   D) Pascal
   *Answer: B*

5. **One 'Joule' is equal to:**
   A) $1 N \cdot m$
   B) $1 N/m$
   C) $1 kg \cdot m/s$
   D) $1 W/s$
   *Answer: A*

6. **Work done is maximum when the angle between Force and Displacement is:**
   A) 90°
   B) 0° (since $\cos 0^\circ = 1$)
   C) 180°
   D) 45°
   *Answer: B*

7. **Work done is zero if the angle between Force and Displacement is:**
   A) 0°
   B) 90° (since $\cos 90^\circ = 0$, e.g., carrying a load horizontally)
   C) 180°
   D) 60°
   *Answer: B*

8. **Work done is negative if the force acts ______ the direction of motion.**
   A) Along
   B) Opposite to (angle = 180°)
   C) Perpendicular to
   *Answer: B (e.g., work done by friction).*

9. **If a person holds a heavy box and stands still, the work done in the physical sense is:**
   A) Positive
   B) Negative
   C) Zero (no displacement)
   D) Maximum
   *Answer: C*

10. **A force of 10 N moves an object 5 m in the same direction. The work done is:**
    A) 2 J
    B) 50 J ($10 \times 5$)
    C) 15 J
    D) 0.5 J
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Kinetic and Potential Energy

11. **'Energy' is defined as the ______ to do work.**
    A) Speed
    B) Capacity
    C) Force
    D) Time
    *Answer: B*

12. **The SI unit of energy is the same as work, which is:**
    A) Watt
    B) Joule (J)
    C) Newton
    D) Volt
    *Answer: B*

13. **Energy possessed by a body due to its motion is:**
    A) Potential Energy
    B) Kinetic Energy (K.E.)
    C) Chemical Energy
    D) Nuclear Energy
    *Answer: B*

14. **The formula for Kinetic Energy is:**
    A) $mgh$
    B) $1/2 mv^2$
    C) $mv$
    D) $Fd$
    *Answer: B*

15. **If the velocity of an object is doubled, its kinetic energy becomes:**
    A) Doubled
    B) Halved
    C) Quadrupled (4 times, as it depends on $v^2$)
    D) Zero
    *Answer: C*

16. **Energy possessed by a body due to its position or configuration is:**
    A) Kinetic Energy
    B) Potential Energy (P.E.)
    C) Thermal Energy
    D) Solar Energy
    *Answer: B*

17. **The formula for Gravitational Potential Energy near Earth is:**
    A) $1/2 mv^2$
    B) $mgh$
    C) $F/d$

18. **If a 2 kg object is raised to a height of 10 m, its P.E. is: (Take $g = 10 m/s^2$)**
    A) 20 J
    B) 200 J ($2 \times 10 \times 10$)

    D) 5 J
    *Answer: B*

19. **Kinetic energy can be expressed in terms of momentum ($p$) as:**
    A) $p^2 m$
    B) $p^2 / 2m$
    C) $pm$

    *Answer: B*

20. **Energy stored in a compressed or stretched spring is:**
    A) Gravitational P.E.
    B) Elastic Potential Energy
    C) Kinetic energy
    D) Chemical energy
    *Answer: B*

21. **Potential energy of a spring is calculated as:**
    A) $mgh$
    B) $1/2 kx^2$ (where $k$ is spring constant)
    C) $1/2 mv^2$
    *Answer: B*

22. **When an object falls from a height, its P.E. ______ and its K.E. ______ .**
    A) Increases, Decreases
    B) Decreases, Increases
    C) Stays same, Stays same
    *Answer: B*

23. **Total Mechanical Energy is the sum of:**
    A) Heat and Light
    B) Kinetic and Potential Energy
    C) Mass and velocity
    *Answer: B*

24. **In the absence of friction, the total mechanical energy of a falling body remains:**
    A) Increasing
    B) Decreasing
    C) Constant (Law of Conservation)
    *Answer: C*

25. **When a car brakes, its kinetic energy is converted into:**
    A) Nuclear energy
    B) Heat energy (due to friction)
    C) Potential energy
    D) Gravity
