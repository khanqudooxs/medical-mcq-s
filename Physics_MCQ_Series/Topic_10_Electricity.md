# Topic 10: Electricity (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Electric Charge and Static Electricity

1. **The intrinsic property of matter that causes it to experience a force in an electromagnetic field is:**
   A) Mass
   B) Electric Charge
   C) Temperature
   D) Density
   *Answer: B*

2. **How many types of electric charges exist?**
   A) 1
   B) 2 (Positive and Negative)
   C) 3
   *Answer: B*

3. **Like charges (e.g., positive and positive) ______ each other.**
   A) Attract
   B) Repel
   C) Ignore
   *Answer: B*

4. **Unlike charges (positive and negative) ______ each other.**
   A) Repel
   B) Attract
   C) Destroy
   *Answer: B*

5. **A body becomes negatively charged by ______ of electrons.**
   A) Gaining
   B) Losing
   C) Sharing
   *Answer: A*

6. **A body becomes positively charged by ______ of electrons.**
   A) Gaining
   B) Losing (electrons are negative, so losing them leaves excess protons)
   C) Melting
   *Answer: B*

7. **The SI unit of electric charge is the:**
   A) Ampere
   B) Coulomb (C)
   C) Volt
   D) Joule
   *Answer: B*

8. **A proton has a charge of approximately:**
   A) $-1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$
   B) $+1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$
   C) $0 C$
   *Answer: B*

9. **An electron has a charge of:**
   A) $+1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$
   B) $-1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$
   C) $1 C$
   *Answer: B*

10. **The study of charges at rest is called:**
    A) Current electricity
    B) Electrostatics
    C) Magnetism
    *Answer: B*

11. **Coulomb's Law states that force between charges is proportional to their ______ and inversely to ______ .**
    A) Mass, Distance
    B) Product of charges, Square of distance ($F \propto q_1q_2 / r^2$)
    C) Speed, Time
    *Answer: B*

12. **The method of charging a conductor by bringing a charged object NEAR it (without touching) is:**
    A) Friction
    B) Induction
    C) Conduction
    *Answer: B*

13. **Materials that allow charges to flow easily are:**
    A) Insulators
    B) Conductors (like metals)
    C) Polymers
    *Answer: B*

14. **Materials that do not allow charges to flow easily are:**
    A) Conductors
    B) Insulators (like rubber and plastic)
    C) Semiconductors
    *Answer: B*

15. **Lightning is a massive ______ occurring in the atmosphere.**
    A) Solar flare
    B) Electric discharge (static electricity)
    C) Chemical reaction

## Part 3: Ohm's Law and Resistance

31. **Ohm's Law states that at constant temperature, current is proportional to ______ .**
    A) Resistance
    B) Potential Difference ($V = IR$)
    C) Mass
    *Answer: B*

32. **The property of a conductor to oppose the flow of current through it is:**
    A) Conductivity
    B) Resistance ($R$)
    C) Capacitance
    D) Potential
    *Answer: B*

33. **The SI unit of Resistance is the:**
    A) Volt
    B) Ohm ($\Omega$)
    C) Ampere
    D) Watt
    *Answer: B*

34. **If $V = 10 V$ and $I = 2 A$, the resistance $R$ is:**
    A) $20 \Omega$
    B) $5 \Omega$ ($10/2$)
    C) $12 \Omega$
    D) $8 \Omega$
    *Answer: B*

35. **Components that follow Ohm's Law are called ______ conductors.**
    A) Non-Ohmic
    B) Ohmic
    C) Superconductors
    *Answer: B (e.g., most metals at constant T).*

36. **Resistance of a wire depends on:**
    A) Length ($L$)
    B) Area of cross-section ($A$)
    C) Nature of material (Resistivity)
    D) All of the above
    *Answer: D*

37. **If the length of a wire is doubled (area constant), its resistance ______ .**
    A) Halves
    B) Doubles ($R \propto L$)
    C) Quadruples
    *Answer: B*

38. **If the thickness (area) of a wire is doubled, its resistance ______ .**
    A) Doubles
    B) Halves ($R \propto 1/A$)
    C) Stays same
    *Answer: B*

39. **The resistance of a conductor ______ as temperature increases (for most metals).**
    A) Decreases
    B) Increases
    C) Remains constant
    *Answer: B*

40. **Resistance per unit length and unit area of a material is its:**
    A) Conductance
    B) Resistivity ($\rho$)
    C) Capacitance
    *Answer: B*

41. **The SI unit of Resistivity is:**
    A) $\Omega$
    B) $\Omega \cdot m$ (Ohm-meter)
    C) $\Omega/m$
    *Answer: B*

42. **A device used to provide a variable resistance in a circuit is a:**
    A) Galvanometer
    B) Rheostat (or Potentiometer)
    C) Battery
    D) Diode
    *Answer: B*

43. **Resistance of an ideal 'Perfect' conductor would be:**
    A) Infinite
    B) Zero
    C) 1
    *Answer: B*

44. **Substances that have practically zero resistance at very low temperatures are:**
    A) Insulators
    B) Superconductors
    C) Semiconductors
    *Answer: B*

45. **Resistance of an ideal ammeter should be ______ .**
    A) Very high
    B) Very low (ideally zero, so it doesn't drop voltage)
    *Answer: B*

46. **Resistance of an ideal voltmeter should be ______ .**
    A) Zero
    B) Very high (ideally infinite, so it doesn't draw current)
    *Answer: B*

    B) Too many (drawing more current than the wire can handle)
