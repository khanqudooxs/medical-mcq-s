## Part 1: Units, Dimensions, and Constants

1. **How many 'Base Units' are in the SI (International System of Units)?**
   A) 3
   B) 7 (Meter, Kilogram, Second, Ampere, Kelvin, Mole, Candela)
   C) 10
   D) 5
   *Answer: B*

2. **The SI unit of Temperature is:**
   A) Celsius
   B) Kelvin (K)
   C) Fahrenheit
   *Answer: B*

3. **The SI unit of 'Amount of Substance' is the:**
   A) Gram
   B) Mole (mol)
   C) Litre
   *Answer: B*

4. **Which of the following is a 'Derived' unit?**
   A) Meter
   B) Newton (N) (derived from $kg \cdot m/s^2$)
   C) Second
   *Answer: B*

5. **The unit of 'Luminous Intensity' is:**
   A) Mole
   B) Candela (cd)
   C) Ampere
   *Answer: B*

6. **Pressure is defined as Force per unit ______ .**
   A) Mass
   B) Area (Pascal = $N/m^2$)
   C) Time
   *Answer: B*

7. **One 'Pascal' is equal to:**
   A) $1 kg/m$
   B) $1 N/m^2$
   C) $1 J/s$
   *Answer: B*

8. **One 'Joule' is the unit of ______ and ______ .**
   A) Power and Force
   B) Work and Energy
   C) Mass and Weight
   *Answer: B*

9. **One 'Watt' is equal to one ______ per second.**
   A) Newton
   B) Joule ($1 W = 1 J/s$)
   C) Coulomb
   *Answer: B*

10. **The dimensions of 'Velocity' are:**
    A) $[L T]$
    B) $[L T^{-1}]$
    C) $[M L T^{-2}]$
    *Answer: B (Length / Time)*

11. **The dimensions of 'Force' are:**
    A) $[M L T^{-1}]$
    B) $[M L T^{-2}]$
    C) $[L T^{-2}]$
    *Answer: B (Mass \times Acceleration)*

12. **The prefix 'Nano' represents:**
    A) $10^{-6}$
    B) $10^{-9}$
    C) $10^{9}$
    *Answer: B*

13. **The prefix 'Mega' represents:**
    A) $10^3$
    B) $10^6$ (one million)
    C) $10^9$
    *Answer: B*

14. **Significant figures represent the ______ of a measurement.**
    A) Color
    B) Precision and reliability

    *Answer: B*

15. **A vector quantity has both ______ and ______ .**
    A) Speed and color
    B) Magnitude and Direction
    C) Mass and weight
