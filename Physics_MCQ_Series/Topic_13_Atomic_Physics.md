## Part 1: Early Discovery and Atom Models

1. **The word 'Atom' comes from the Greek word 'Atomos', which means:**
   A) Tiny
   B) Indivisible

   *Answer: B*

3. **Who discovered the electron in 1897?**
   A) James Chadwick
   B) J.J. Thomson

4. **J.J. Thomson's model of the atom is often called the ______ model.**
   A) Nuclear

5. **Who performed the famous 골드-호일(Gold Foil) experiment?**

   A) Bounced back

   A) Orbit
   B) Nucleus
   C) Shell

8. **According to Rutherford, electrons revolve around the nucleus like ______ .**
   A) Bees
   B) Planets around the Sun
   C) Fixed points
   *Answer: B*

9. **The main drawback of Rutherford's model was that it could not explain the ______ of the atom.**

   B) Stability (classical physics said orbiting electrons should radiate energy and spiral into the nucleus)

## Part 4: The Photoelectric Effect

31. **The emission of electrons from a metal surface when light of a suitable frequency falls on it is the:**
    A) Thermionic emission

    *Answer: B*

32. **Who discovered the photoelectric effect in 1887 (experimentally)?**
    A) Einstein
    B) Heinrich Hertz
    C) Newton
    *Answer: B*

33. **Who explained the photoelectric effect using the concept of Light Quanta (Photons) in 1905?**
    A) Bohr
    B) Albert Einstein
    C) Planck
    *Answer: B (he won the Nobel Prize for this).*

34. **A 'Photon' is a discrete packet or 'quantum' of ______ energy.**
    A) Magnetic
    B) Light (Electromagnetic)
    C) Mechanical
    *Answer: B*

35. **The energy of a photon is given by the formula:**
    A) $E = mc^2$
    B) $E = hf$ (where $h$ is Planck's constant)
    C) $E = 1/2 mv^2$
    *Answer: B*

36. **The value of Planck's constant ($h$) is approximately:**
    A) $9.1 \times 10^{-31} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
    B) $6.626 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
    C) $3 \times 10^8 \text{ J}\cdot\text{s}$
    *Answer: B*

37. **The minimum frequency required to eject an electron from a metal surface is the:**
    A) Maximum frequency
    B) Threshold Frequency ($f_o$)
    C) Resonance frequency
    *Answer: B*

38. **If the frequency of light is LESS than the threshold frequency, will electrons be ejected?**
    A) Yes, if we wait long enough
    B) No, regardless of intensity
    *Answer: B*

39. **The minimum energy required to liberate an electron from a metal surface is the:**
    A) Ionization energy
    B) Work Function ($\Phi$ or $W_o$)
    C) Kinetic energy
    *Answer: B*

40. **Einstein's Photoelectric Equation is:**
    A) $hf = \Phi + K.E._{max}$
    B) $hf = mc^2$
    C) $E = h / \lambda$
    *Answer: A (Photon energy = Work function + Kinetic energy).*

41. **Increasing the 'Intensity' (brightness) of light increases the ______ of emitted photoelectrons.**

    *Answer: B*

42. **Increasing the 'Frequency' (color) of light increases the ______ of individual photoelectrons.**
    A) Number
    B) Maximum Kinetic Energy / Speed

43. **Is there any time lag between the arrival of light and the emission of electrons?**

44. **The photoelectric effect provides strong evidence for the ______ nature of light.**
    A) Wave

45. **Devices that convert light into electricity using this effect are:**
    A) Batteries
