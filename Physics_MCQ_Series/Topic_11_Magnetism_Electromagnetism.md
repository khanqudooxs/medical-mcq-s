# Topic 11: Magnetism & Electromagnetism (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Basic Magnetism

1. **A 'Magnet' is a material that produces a ______ field.**
   A) Electrical
   B) Magnetic
   C) Gravitational
   D) Thermal
   *Answer: B*

2. **Every magnet has how many poles?**
   A) 1
   B) 2 (North and South)
   C) 4
   *Answer: B*

3. **Like magnetic poles (e.g., North and North) ______ each other.**
   A) Attract
   B) Repel
   C) Ignore
   *Answer: B*

4. **Unlike magnetic poles (North and South) ______ each other.**
   A) Repel
   B) Attract
   C) Destroy
   *Answer: B*

5. **A 'Monopole' (a magnet with only one pole) has ______ been found in nature.**
   A) Always
   B) Never (magnets always exist as dipoles)
   C) Recently
   *Answer: B*

6. **If you break a bar magnet in half, you get:**
   A) One North pole and one South pole
   B) Two smaller complete magnets (each with N and S)
   C) No magnetism
   *Answer: B*

7. **The space around a magnet where its force is felt is the:**
   A) Magnetic Field
   B) Magnetic Flux
   C) Magnetosphere
   *Answer: A*

8. **Magnetic field lines originate from the ______ pole and end at the ______ pole.**
   A) South, North
   B) North, South
   *Answer: B*

9. **Can two magnetic field lines ever cross each other?**
   A) Yes
   ) No (meaning the field has a unique direction at every point)
   *Answer: B*

10. **Magnetic field lines are ______ loops.**
    A) Open
    B) Continuous closed
    C) Straight
    *Answer: B*

11. **The density of field lines represents the ______ of the magnetic field.**
    A) Color
    B) Strength (Stronger where lines are closer)
    C) Direction
    *Answer: B*

12. **A freely suspended bar magnet always points in the ______ direction.**
    A) East-West
    B) North-South (approximate)
    C) Up-Down
    *Answer: B (due to Earth's magnetism).*

13. **Earth's magnetic North pole is geographically near the ______ pole.**
    A) North
    B) South
    *Answer: B (The magnetic pole that attracts a compass's North is actually a magnetic south).*

14. **Instruments that use a magnet to find directions are:**
    A) Barometers
    B) Compasses
    C) Thermometers
    *Answer: B*

15. **Materials that are strongly attracted to magnets (like iron, nickel, cobalt) are:**
    A) Paramagnetic
    B) Ferromagnetic
    C) Diamagnetic
    *Answer: B*

## Part 2: Magnetic Effects of Electric Current (Oersted effect)

16. **Who discovered that an electric current produces a magnetic field?**
    A) Newton
    B) Hans Christian Oersted (1820)

17. **The magnetic field around a straight current-carrying wire is in the form of:**

    C) Spirals

18. **The 'Right-Hand Thumb Rule' is used to find the ______ of the magnetic field.**
    A) Strength
    B) Direction
    C) Mass
    *Answer: B*

19. **In the Right-Hand Thumb Rule, if the thumb points in the direction of current, the fingers wrap in the direction of ______ .**
    A) Force
    B) Magnetic Field ($B$)
    C) Voltage
    *Answer: B*

20. **The SI unit of magnetic field strength (magnetic flux density) is the:**
    A) Weber
    B) Tesla (T)
    C) Gauss

    *Answer: B*

21. **One Tesla is equal to how many Gauss (CGS unit)?**
    A) 10
    B) 10,000 ($10^4$)

    *Answer: B*

22. **A long coil of wire with many turns is called a:**
    A) Resistor
    B) Solenoid
    C) Capacitor
    D) Diode
    *Answer: B*

23. **The magnetic field inside a current-carrying solenoid is ______ .**
    A) Zero
    B) Uniform (parallel straight lines)
    C) Circular
    *Answer: B*

24. **A solenoid with a soft iron core inside is called an:**
    A) Permanent magnet
    B) Electromagnet
    C) Insulator
    *Answer: B*

25. **The strength of an electromagnet can be increased by:**
    A) Increasing the current
    B) Increasing the number of turns in the coil
    C) Using a soft iron core
    D) All of the above
    *Answer: D*

26. **Which of the following is used in electric bells and loud speakers?**
    A) Permanent magnets
    B) Electromagnets
    C) Plastic rods
    *Answer: B*

27. **Do electromagnets lose their magnetism when current is turned off?**
    A) No
    B) Yes (mostly)
    *Answer: B (this is why they are useful for scrap metal lifting).*

28. **Magnetic field strength $B$ at the center of a circular loop is inversely proportional to its ______ .**
    A) Current
    B) Radius ($r$)
    C) Mass
    *Answer: B*

32. **The force is zero if the wire is ______ to the field.**

    B) Parallel

    *Answer: B*

33. **The direction of the force is found using ______ .**
    A) Fleming's Left-Hand Rule

    A) Magnetic Field

35. **In Fleming's Left-Hand Rule, the 'Forefinger' represents:**
    A) Current

    A) Tension

39. **The force on a single moving charge ($q$) in a magnetic field is the ______ force.**

40. **Does a stationary charge experience a force in a magnetic field?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (as $v=0$)

41. **The path of a charged particle moving perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field is a:**
    A) Straight line

42. **Mass spectrometers and Cyclotrons use magnetic fields to ______ charged particles.**

## Part 4: Electromagnetic Induction (Faraday's discovery)

43. **The production of electric current by a changing magnetic field is:**
    A) Conduction
    B) Electromagnetic Induction
    C) Thermal emission
    *Answer: B*

44. **Who discovered electromagnetic induction?**
    A) Ohm
    B) Michael Faraday (1831)
    C) Oersted
    *Answer: B*

45. **Total number of magnetic field lines passing through a surface is:**
    A) Magnetic Intensity
    B) Magnetic Flux ($\Phi$)
    C) Magnetism
    *Answer: B*

46. **The SI unit of magnetic flux is the:**
    A) Tesla
    B) Weber (Wb)
    C) Henry
    D) Ampere
    *Answer: B*

47. **$1 \text{ Weber}$ is equal to ______ .**
    A) $1 T \cdot m^2$
    B) $1 T / m$
    C) $1 V \cdot s$
    D) Both A and C
    *Answer: D*

48. **An induced EMF is produced only when there is a ______ in magnetic flux.**
    A) High value
    B) Change
    C) Absence
    *Answer: B*

49. **'The magnitude of induced EMF is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux' is:**
    A) Ohm's Law
    B) Faraday's Law of Induction
    C) Lenz's Law
    *Answer: B ($\epsilon = d\Phi/dt$)*

50. **'The direction of induced current is such that it opposes the change that produced it' is:**
    A) Faraday's Law
    B) Lenz's Law
    C) Newton's Law
    *Answer: B*

51. **Lenz's Law is a consequence of the Law of Conservation of ______ .**
    A) Mass
    B) Energy
    C) Momentum
    *Answer: B*

52. **Fleming's ______ Rule is used to find the direction of 'induced' current.**
    A) Left-Hand
    B) Right-Hand (Used for Generators)
    *Answer: B*

53. **In Fleming's Right-Hand Rule, the 'Thumb' represents the direction of ______ .**
    A) Induced current
    B) Motion of conductor
    C) Flux
    *Answer: B*

54. **In Fleming's Right-Hand Rule, the 'Middle' finger represents the direction of ______ .**
    A) Magnetic Field
    B) Induced Current
    C) Speed
    *Answer: B*

55. **A device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy is a/an:**
    A) Motor
    B) Electric Generator (or Dynamo)
    C) Heater
    *Answer: B*

56. **A generator that uses 'slip rings' produces ______ current.**
    A) Direct (DC)
    B) Alternating (AC)
    C) Neutral
    *Answer: B*

57. **A generator that uses a 'split ring commutator' produces ______ current.**
    A) Alternating (AC)
    B) Direct (DC)
    C) High
    *Answer: B*

## Part 5: Transformers and Inductance

58. **A 'Transformer' works on the principle of ______ .**
    A) Self-induction
    B) Mutual Induction (changing current in one coil induces EMF in another)
    C) Friction
    *Answer: B*

59. **Transformers work ONLY with ______ current.**
    A) Direct (DC)
    B) Alternating (AC) (because only AC produces changing flux)
    C) Battery
    *Answer: B*

60. **A transformer used to increase AC voltage is a:**
    A) Step-down transformer
    B) Step-up transformer
    C) Power resistor
    *Answer: B*

61. **In a Step-up transformer, the number of 'Secondary' turns ($N_s$) is ______ than 'Primary' turns ($N_p$).**
    A) Less
    B) Greater ($N_s > N_p$)
    C) Equal
    *Answer: B*

62. **A transformer used to decrease AC voltage is a:**
    A) Step-up
    B) Step-down transformer
    *Answer: B*

63. **In a Step-down transformer, $N_s$ is ______ than $N_p$.**
    A) Greater
    B) Less ($N_s < N_p$)
    *Answer: B*

64. **The ratio $V_s / V_p = N_s / N_p = I_p / I_s$ is the ______ ratio.**
    A) Power
    B) Transformation (or Turns)
    C) Resistance
    *Answer: B*

65. **Are transformers 100% efficient?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (energy is lost as heat due to resistance and 'Eddy currents')
    *Answer: B*

66. **'Eddy Currents' are circulating currents induced in the ______ of the transformer.**
    A) Wires
    B) Solid core
    C) Oil
    *Answer: B*

67. **Eddy currents are reduced by using a ______ core.**
    A) Solid metal
    B) Laminated (made of thin insulated sheets)
    C) Plastic
    *Answer: B*

68. **The property of a coil to induce EMF in 'itself' when the current changes is:**
    A) Mutual Induction
    B) Self-Inductance ($L$)
    C) Conductance
    *Answer: B*

69. **The SI unit of Inductance (both self and mutual) is the:**
    A) Weber
    B) Henry (H)
    C) Tesla
    D) Farad
    *Answer: B*

70. **Inductance depends on the ______ and number of turns of the coil.**
    A) Weight
    B) Geometry (Shape/Size)
    C) Color
    *Answer: B*

    B) Special steel or alloys like 'Alnico' (Aluminum, Nickel, Cobalt)

    A) Heating to high temperature (Curie point)
    B) Dropping/Hammering repeatedly
    C) Placing in an AC coil and gradually withdrawing
    D) All of the above

82. **The Temperature at which a ferromagnetic material becomes paramagnetic is the:**
    A) Melting point
    B) Curie Temperature
    C) Boiling point
    *Answer: B*

    A) Roll
    B) Levitate (float using magnetic repulsion)
    C) Fly
    *Answer: B*

84. **Magnetic Record keeping (like HDD and Tapes) uses magnetic ______ .**
    A) Storage
    B) Domains

    *Answer: B*

85. **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) uses ______ magnetic fields to image the body.**

    B) Extremely strong (several Teslas)

86. **Does a transformer work on DC?**

87. **The core of a transformer is usually ______ .**

89. **The property of magnetic shielding is achieved using materials with high ______ .**

    A) $B = \mu H$

91. **In a vacuum, $\mu$ is equal to permeability of free space ($\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} T\cdot m/A$).**

100. **The magnetism of the Earth is thought to be generated by the ______ in its outer core.**
