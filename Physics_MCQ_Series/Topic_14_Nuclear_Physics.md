# Topic 14: Nuclear Physics (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Nucleus and Binding Energy

1. **The central part of an atom containing protons and neutrons is the:**
   A) Shell
   B) Nucleus
   C) Orbit
   D) Plasma
   *Answer: B*

2. **Protons and Neutrons together are known as:**
   A) Leptons
   B) Nucleons
   C) Positrons
   D) Quarks
   *Answer: B*

3. **The 'Atomic Number' ($Z$) is the number of ______ in the nucleus.**
   A) Neutrons
   B) Protons
   C) Electrons
   D) Both A and B
   *Answer: B*

4. **The 'Mass Number' ($A$) is the sum of ______ and ______ .**
   A) Protons and Electrons
   B) Protons and Neutrons
   C) Neutrons and Electrons
   *Answer: B*

5. **Number of Neutrons ($N$) in a nucleus is calculated as:**
   A) $Z + A$
   B) $A - Z$
   C) $A \times Z$
   *Answer: B*

6. **The force that holds protons and neutrons together in the nucleus is the:**
   A) Gravitational force
   B) Electromagnetic force
   C) Strong Nuclear Force
   D) Weak force
   *Answer: C*

7. **The Strong Nuclear Force is ______ in range.**
   A) Infinite
   B) Extremely Short (only within the nucleus)
   C) Long
   *Answer: B*

8. **Is the Strong Nuclear Force charge-dependent?**
   A) Yes
   B) No (it attracts $p-p, n-n, \text{ and } p-n$ equally)
   *Answer: B*

9. **The mass of a nucleus is always ______ than the sum of masses of its individual nucleons.**
   A) Greater
   B) Slightly Less
   C) Equal
   *Answer: B*

10. **The difference in mass (Question 9) is known as the:**
    A) Mass defect ($\Delta m$)
    B) Binding energy
    C) Isotope difference
    *Answer: A*

11. **Mass defect is converted into energy according to $E = \Delta m \cdot c^2$. This energy is the:**
    A) Chemical energy
    B) Nuclear Binding Energy (energy required to separate nucleons)
    C) Electrical energy
    *Answer: B*

12. **The stability of a nucleus is determined by its binding energy ______ .**
    A) Per atom
    B) Per Nucleon (B.E. / A)
    C) Total
    *Answer: B*

13. **Which element's nucleus is the most stable (highest B.E. per nucleon)?**
    A) Uranium
    B) Iron (Fe-56)
    C) Hydrogen
    D) Gold
    *Answer: B*

14. **Smallest nucleus is of ______ .**
    A) Helium
    B) Hydrogen (just one proton)
    C) Lithium
    *Answer: B*

15. **Nuclei with the same number of neutrons but different number of protons are:**
    A) Isotopes
    B) Isotones
    C) Isobars
    *Answer: B*

16. **Nuclei with the same mass number ($A$) but different atomic number ($Z$) are:**
    A) Isotopes
    B) Isobars
    C) Isomers
    *Answer: B*

    A) Electrons

    C) High energy light
    *Answer: B*

    A) Protons
    B) Electrons ($e^-$) or Positrons ($e^+$)

    *Answer: B*

    A) Gamma
    B) Beta

    A) Alpha (stopped by paper)

26. **Which radiation is NOT deflected by electric or magnetic fields?**

27. **During Alpha decay, the atomic number (Z) ______ by 2 and mass number (A) ______ by 4.**
    A) Increases, Decreases
    B) Decreases, Decreases

    *Answer: B*

28. **During Beta-minus decay, a neutron turns into a proton, so Z ______ by 1.**
    A) Decreases
    B) Increases

29. **The time required for half of the atoms in a radioactive sample to decay is the:**
    A) Full-life
    B) Half-Life ($T_{1/2}$)
    C) Stability time
    *Answer: B*

30. **If a sample has a half-life of 10 days, how much remains after 20 days?**
    A) Half
    B) One-fourth (1/4)
    C) Zero
    *Answer: B*

31. **The SI unit of radioactivity is the:**
    A) Curie
    B) Becquerel (Bq) (1 decay per second)
