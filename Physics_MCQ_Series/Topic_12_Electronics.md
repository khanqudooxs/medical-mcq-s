# Topic 12: Electronics Basics (150 MCQs Series)
## Part 1: Conductors, Insulators, and Semiconductors

1. **Materials that have a large number of free electrons and allow current to flow easily are:**
   A) Insulators
   B) Conductors
   C) Semiconductors
   D) Gases
   *Answer: B (e.g., Copper, Silver).*

2. **Materials that have almost no free electrons and do not allow current to flow are:**
   A) Conductors
   B) Insulators
   C) Semiconductors
   D) Metals
   *Answer: B (e.g., Glass, Rubber).*

3. **Materials whose electrical properties lie between conductors and insulators are:**
   A) Superconductors
   B) Semiconductors
   C) Polymers
   *Answer: B (e.g., Silicon, Germanium).*

4. **In the 'Band Theory' of solids, the range of energy levels occupied by valence electrons is the:**
   A) Conduction band
   B) Valence Band
   C) Forbidden gap
   *Answer: B*

5. **The range of energy levels in which electrons are free to move and conduct current is the:**
   A) Valence band
   B) Conduction Band
   C) Zero band
   *Answer: B*

6. **The energy gap between the valence band and conduction band is the:**
   A) Fermi level
   B) Forbidden Gap (Energy Gap, $E_g$)
   C) Overlap
   *Answer: B*

7. **For a 'Conductor', the valence and conduction bands ______ .**
   A) Are far apart
   B) Overlap (making conductivity high)
   C) Don't exist
   *Answer: B*

8. **For an 'Insulator', the forbidden gap is ______ .**
   A) Zero
   B) Very large (typically $> 3 \text{ eV}$)
   C) Small
   *Answer: B*

9. **For a 'Semiconductor', the forbidden gap is ______ .**
   A) Infinite
   ) Small (around $1 \text{ eV}$)
   C) Zero
   *Answer: B*

10. **At 'Absolute Zero' (0 K), a pure semiconductor acts as a/an ______ .**
    A) Perfect conductor
    B) Insulator
    C) Battery
    *Answer: B*

11. **Conductivity of a semiconductor ______ with an 'increase' in temperature.**
    A) Decreases
    B) Increases (unlike metals)
    C) Stays same
    *Answer: B*

12. **A pure semiconductor (without any impurities) is called a/an ______ semiconductor.**
    A) Extrinsic
    B) Intrinsic
    C) Doped
    *Answer: B*

13. **The process of adding a small amount of controlled impurity to a semiconductor is:**
    A) Polishing
    B) Doping
    C) Refining
    *Answer: B*

14. **Doping is done to ______ the conductivity of the material.**
    A) Decrease
    B) Increase
    C) Change color of
    *Answer: B*

15. **A semiconductor with added impurities is called a/an ______ semiconductor.**
    A) Intrinsic
    B) Extrinsic
    C) Natural
    *Answer: B*

18. **When doped with a 'Trivalent' impurity (3 valence electrons, e.g., Boron), it becomes a/an:**
    A) N-type
    B) P-type (Positive-type)
    *Answer: B*

19. **In a P-type semiconductor, the majority charge carriers are ______ .**
    A) Electrons
    B) Holes (absence of an electron)
    C) Neutrons
    *Answer: B*

20. **A 'Hole' has an effective ______ charge.**
    A) Negative
    B) Positive
    C) Zero
    *Answer: B*

21. **In an N-type material, holes are the 'minority' carriers.**
    A) True
    B) False
    *Answer: A*

22. **In a P-type material, electrons are the minority carriers.**
    A) True
    B) False
    *Answer: A*

23. **Is an N-type semiconductor overall negatively charged?**
    A) Yes
    B) No (it is electrically neutral; the extra electrons come with extra protons in the impurity atoms)

24. **Pure Silicon has ______ valence electrons.**
    A) 2
    B) 4
    C) 8
    *Answer: B*

## Part 3: The P-N Junction and Diodes

25. **When a P-type material is joined to an N-type material, the boundary is called a:**
    A) Switch
    B) P-N Junction
    C) Gap
    *Answer: B*

26. **The thin region at the junction where no mobile charge carriers exist is the:**
    A) Conduction zone
    B) Depletion Region (or Layer)
    C) Barrier zone
    *Answer: B*

27. **The potential difference across the depletion region that opposes further flow is the:**
    A) Battery voltage
    B) Barrier Potential
    C) Output voltage
    *Answer: B*

28. **Barrier potential for Silicon is approximately ______ .**
    A) 0.3 V
    B) 0.7 V

29. **A P-N junction device that allows current to flow in only one direction is a:**
    A) Capacitor

    A) Reverse Biased

    C) Unbiased

31. **In 'Forward Bias', the depletion region width ______ .**
    A) Increases

32. **When the P-side is negative and N-side is positive, the diode is:**
    A) Forward biased
    B) Reverse Biased

33. **In 'Reverse Bias', the depletion region width ______ .**
    A) Decreases
    B) Increases

34. **Does an ideal diode conduct any current in reverse bias?**

37. **A 'Full-wave Rectifier' uses ______ current cycles.**
    A) Zero

38. **A bridge rectifier uses how many diodes?**
    A) 1

39. **A special diode designed to work in the reverse breakdown region to regulate voltage is a:**
    A) LED
    B) Zener Diode
